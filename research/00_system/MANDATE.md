# The Mandate — My research approach

> The north star for everything in this folder.

## The one-line aim

> "Find out **when** I think the market's perception on the valuation of an equity is going to change, then provide a **range of values** for what the stock will change by."

Two deliverables, always:
1. **A trigger / timing** — the catalyst or moment the market's view shifts.
2. **A magnitude range** — how much the stock re-rates, expressed as a range, not a point.

## What that requires

To do this I have to know my companies extremely well — the ins and outs of all of them:
- The **narrative** — what drives them, the story the market is telling itself.
- The **news flow** and how each type of news lands.
- **Management** — who they are, credibility, capital-allocation track record.
- The **important lines in the income statement** (and balance sheet / cash flow).
- **Legal / regulatory status**.
- The **sector**, the **competitors**, and what impacts them.
- The **global environment** and the prevailing **narratives**.

The emphasis throughout is on **thematics and narratives**.

## How I'm interpreting it — the three-layer top-down

1. **A model of the world (macro regime).** What is the *pivotal* story moving the market right now — rates, inflation, growth, geopolitics, oil, elections, AI usage/cost, credit spreads, gold, confidence in America, etc. Plus the smaller narratives underneath.

2. **A model of each sector** my companies sit in. Ask the same questions again: what is the pivotal narrative and the smaller narratives moving *this sector*? What are the key events, details and drivers — everything, not just the obvious one or two.

3. **A model of each company.** Exactly how it fits into its sector and the world. What moves this specific stock, what the market cares about, what its narrative is, and **how to spot a narrative shift**. How different news maps onto: top line, bottom line, total vs organic growth, debt issuance, buybacks, AI capex, etc.

## The skill being built

> Read a piece of news and **instantly** have a range for how much it moves the stock — because I understand the consensus/feel for the company, the sell-side view, and **what the market is already pricing in** so well that I can predict the reaction.

That is the goal. That is how in-depth I must know the list.

## The core principle that makes it all work

**Price = the consensus set of expectations.** News only moves a stock to the degree it *differs from what was already priced in*. So the job decomposes into three questions, every time:

1. **What is priced in?** (consensus estimates, positioning, the prevailing narrative)
2. **What could change it?** (the catalyst calendar + the surprises)
3. **How sensitive is the stock to that change?** (the elasticity — multiple × earnings revision × positioning unwind)

Magnitude ≈ (size of surprise vs expectations) × (the stock's sensitivity to that variable).

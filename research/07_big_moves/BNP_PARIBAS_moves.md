## The big-move log

| Date | Move | What drove it |
|---|---|---|
| **Mar 2020 (COVID)** | **≈ −30–40%** peak-to-trough | Pandemic crash + recession/credit fear; capped by the **ECB/ACPR dividend ban (27 Mar 2020)** — banks couldn't return capital. |
| **2022 (ECB liftoff)** | **multi-month re-rate up** | The end of negative rates → NII tailwind → the "great European bank re-rating" from sub-0.5× book. |
| **13–15 Mar 2023** | **≈ −7% to −10%** (sector −7.1% one-day, steepest in >1yr) | **SVB + Credit Suisse banking crisis.** Contagion fear hit all European banks; Spain/Italy lenders −4%+; CS −24% one-day before the UBS rescue. ## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **Systemic/contagion fear is the biggest *down* driver** — Mar-23 shows BNP falls hard on *other* banks' failures regardless of its own health (then mean-reverts).
2. **French political/sovereign risk is the idiosyncratic swing** — the OAT-Bund spread is BNP's unique risk premium (Jun-24). Diversification means it falls *less* than pure-French peers.
3. **The rate cycle is the structural re-rate** — 2022 liftoff up; a recession/cost-of-risk spike would be the mirror.
4. **Capital return surprises** (buyback size vs CET1) move it more than EPS beats.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "NII second wind + fee pivot re-rates P/B to ~1.0×":** the oil-shock hike gives NII a leg the consensus didn't model; AXA IM adds capital-light fees; buybacks below 0.8× book are accretive → the market finally credits a durable >13% ROTE.
- **Bear — "recession lifts cost of risk":** eurozone already soft; a downturn turns the rate tailwind into a credit headwind (NII up but provisions up more) — the classic bank kill-switch, amplified if private-credit stress (AXA IM Alts) bites.
- **Variant — "French risk is the cheap option":** BNP is penalised for French domicile but is the *least* French-exposed of the trio; any easing of OAT-spread stress is asymmetric upside vs SocGen/ACA.

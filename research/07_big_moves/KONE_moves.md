# Kone — Big Moves: what drove the largest single-day re-rates

> A quality compounder whose multi-year re-rate (and de-rate) is **all about China construction**; single-day moves on **orders/margins vs guide**. Pairs with [factual §7](../03_companies/KONE.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/KONE_narrative.md), [history](../06_history/KONE_history.md). Research date 2026-06-29. *(%/dates approximate.)*

## The big-move log

| Date | Move | What drove it |
|---|---|---|
| **2010s (China boom)** | **multi-year re-rate up; +112% 2011–16** | China's elevator/escalator base grew ~+16%/yr — Kone's growth engine; became a dividend-aristocrat quality compounder. |
| **Mar 2020 (COVID)** | **≈ −25–30%** then recovery | Construction/installation disruption; the maintenance annuity cushioned it; recovered fast. |
| **2021 (China peak)** | **topping out** | Chinese new-build demand peaked; the order-book mix began rolling over. |
| **2022–2024 (China property bust)** | **multi-year de-rate** | New-equipment demand fell sharply with the China property downturn (Greater-China order-book share ~40%→~30%); margin pressure from China price competition. The defining drawdown. |
| **Quarterly prints (2022–26)** | **±5–10%** | **China orders (the 2nd derivative)** + adjusted EBIT margin vs guide. Signs of China *bottoming* can re-rate it even with absolute weakness. |

## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **China construction is the whole multi-year arc** — the boom made it (2010s), the property bust de-rated it (2020s). One variable dominates.
2. **The services/maintenance annuity dampens the downside** — recurring revenue means earnings-day drops are cushioned vs a pure new-build name.
3. **★ The 2nd derivative matters** — because the China weakness is well-known, the stock can re-rate on signs China is *less-bad*/bottoming, not just on absolute growth.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "China bottoming + stimulus-funded modernisation":** China new-build stabilises and **stimulus ("two new" + treasury bonds) funds equipment modernisation/renewal**, offsetting the new-build drag; services keep compounding; margin recovers into the 12.3–13.0% guide → re-rate toward the ~€62 PT.
- **Bear — "China property deteriorates further":** new-equipment demand + pricing keep falling and stimulus underwhelms — the modernisation/services offset isn't enough.
- **Variant — "the services-annuity quality is under-priced":** strip out China new-build and Kone is a high-margin recurring-revenue compounder; the market over-weights the cyclical China drag vs the durable maintenance base.

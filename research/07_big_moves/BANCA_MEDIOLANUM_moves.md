# Banca Mediolanum — Big Moves: what drove the largest single-day re-rates

> Less a lending bank, more an **asset gatherer** — so it moves on **equity markets, net flows, rates, and Italian (BTP) risk**, not loan losses. ## The big-move log

| Date | Move | What drove it |
|---|---|---|
| **Mar 2020 (COVID)** | **≈ −40%+** peak-to-trough | Market crash hit AUM + fees simultaneously; the asset-gatherer's amplified beta. Recovered strongly as inflows proved sticky. |
| **Mar 2023 (SVB/CS)** | **≈ −5–8%** | Italian financials sold off with the European bank rout (Spain/Italy lenders −4%+); BMED fell on sector contagion despite its low credit risk. |
| **2022 (rates + risk-off)** | **drawdown** | The equity bear market hit AUM/fees, partly offset by the new NII tailwind from ECB hikes. |
| **2024–25 Italian wealth-M&A saga** | **moves on flow data + sector re-rating** | The Mediobanca / Banca Generali / Italian consolidation saga re-rated Italian wealth managers; BMED moves on monthly **net-inflow** releases and the sector's scarcity premium. |
| **Monthly inflow data** | **±several %** | BMED reports monthly net flows — managed-inflow beats/misses are the high-frequency catalyst (unusual for a bank). |

## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **It's a markets + flows stock** — AUM, fees, and inflows all move with equity markets; a drawdown hits all three at once (the amplified downside).
2. **Cost of risk barely matters** — unlike BNP/ACA, the loan book isn't the driver; **monthly net inflows** are the tell.
3. **Italian beta** — BTP-spread/political risk + sector contagion (Mar-23) drag it even when its own model is fine.
4. **Low free float (~70% Doris/Fininvest)** → can gap on flow data.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "the structural Italian-savings compounder":** managed inflows stay resilient (~€9bn) *despite* attractive deposit yields → proves the Family-Banker moat; NII tailwind + market gains compound to records; premium sustained.
- **Bear — "savers flee to deposits/BTPs + a market drawdown":** higher-for-longer makes risk-free yields too attractive (inflow cannibalisation) and a risk-off move hits AUM/fees/inflows together — the asset-gatherer's triple hit.
- **Variant — "scarcity in Italian wealth M&A":** consolidation re-rates the whole Italian wealth complex; BMED's quality + low float = a takeout-optionality/scarcity premium the market under-prices.

| **Q1-26 (market turbulence)** | **CASA earnings −7.9%** | The **2026 Iran-war market turbulence** dented CASA's fee/insurance/CIB lines even as the *Group's* retail NII rose — the CASA-vs-Group split in action. |

## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **More market-beta than BNP** — CASA's insurance + Amundi + CIB mean a market drawdown hits *earnings*, not just sentiment (Q1-26). In risk-off, ACA underperforms BNP.
2. **Most French-political-sensitive** of the book's banks (Jun-24) — the OAT-spread is its sharpest lever.
3. **Same systemic-contagion + rate-cycle drivers** as all banks.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "deep-value + markets recover":** at ~7× / ~6.9% yield / 13.5% RoTE, a NII tailwind + an Amundi/market rebound (which *helps* CASA's fee tilt) closes the discount toward the €20+ PT.
- **Bear — "market drawdown + French risk":** a risk-off move hits fees/insurance/CIB simultaneously *and* widens the OAT spread — ACA's double sensitivity makes it the funded-bear vs BNP.
- **Variant — "the highest-yield way to own the rate trade":** if you want bank-NII exposure with a fat, growing dividend and don't mind the market-beta, ACA pays you the most to wait.

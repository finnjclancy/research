## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **Pipeline readouts are binary and brutal** — a clinical miss vs *management's own promises* (CagriSema ≥25%) is the worst combination: number + credibility. Double-digit moves.
2. **Guidance regime-change** (growth → decline, Feb-26) re-rates the whole multiple, not just the year.
3. **Competition is priced relative to Lilly** — Novo moves on Lilly's data as much as its own.
4. **★ Washed-out = upside asymmetry.** Post-bust, positioning is so clean that *minor* good news (the May-26 pill beat) sparks sharp relief rallies. Weight positioning, not just fundamentals.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "deep-value turnaround on amycretin + oral":** sentiment/positioning are washed out; the **amycretin/AMAZE Phase 3** (24% weight loss in 36 wks in Ph2) + the fast oral-pill ramp re-ignite the pipeline narrative → a violent re-rate off a 74%-off base. *Catalyst: AMAZE readout; weekly US script-share stabilising.*
- **Bear — "structural #2 / value trap":** Lilly keeps taking US *and* ex-US share, MFN pricing + LOE erode the cash engine, and the next pipeline asset disappoints → cheap-for-a-reason, earnings keep falling.
- **Variant — "the category, not the leader":** even as #2, the obesity TAM is so large and under-penetrated that Novo compounds respectably; the market is pricing terminal decline that the category growth won't allow. *Edge = the gap between "priced for decline" and "still a structural grower."*
- **★ Trading thesis (event-driven):** Novo is the book's best *binary-catalyst* vehicle — pre-position ranges around script data + the AMAZE readout, and respect the positioning amplifier (good news > priced reaction).

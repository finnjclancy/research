# Melexis — Big Moves: what drove the largest single-day re-rates

> An auto-semi — the moves track the **automotive chip cycle** (inventory + production), not AI. Pairs with [factual §7](../03_companies/MELEXIS.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/MELEXIS_narrative.md), [history](../06_history/MELEXIS_history.md). Research date 2026-06-29. *(%/dates approximate.)*

## The big-move log

| Date | Move | What drove it |
|---|---|---|
| **2018 (auto peak)** | **topping** | Record sales (€569m) at the prior cycle peak. |
| **2019 (auto downturn)** | **de-rate; sales −14%** | The pre-COVID auto-semiconductor down-cycle — a clean example of how Melexis trades a downturn (sales €569m→€487m). |
| **Mar 2020 (COVID)** | **≈ −30%+** then sharp recovery | Auto plants shut → demand shock; then the chip-shortage super-cycle began. |
| **2021–2022 (chip shortage)** | **multi-quarter re-rate up** | Record sales (~€960m) + content-per-car growth; the boom. |
| **2023–2025 (inventory correction)** | **de-rate; FY2025 sales −10%** | The post-shortage destock; Melexis cut its capex plan (Q3 2025); the current trough. |
| **Quarterly prints** | **±5–10%** | Sales trajectory vs the cycle (the H1→H2 inflection), margins vs guide, lead-time/booking commentary. |

## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **It's an auto-cycle stock, NOT an AI stock** — read it off auto production (SAAR), the inventory cycle, and peers (NXP/Infineon/STMicro), never AI-capex.
2. **Early-cyclical: sharp moves off troughs** — the 2019 and 2023–25 down-cycles + the 2021 recovery show big swings around the trend.
3. **Content-per-car is the structural uptrend** under the cyclical noise (more sensors per EV/ADAS).
4. **High-dividend / founder-controlled** → owned partly for yield; a defensive holder base.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "the trough is in + China-localization growth":** H2-2026 sales re-accelerate as guided, the inventory correction ends, and the "China Speed" (Shanghai WFOE + local fab) strategy wins share in its fastest-growth market → a cyclical re-rate off the trough.
- **Bear — "EV-demand stall + China local competition":** European EV growth keeps slowing (incentive cuts) so content-per-car can't offset weak unit volumes, and Chinese domestic sensor makers undercut Melexis in China → margins compress.
- **Variant — "robotics optionality":** Melexis is targeting humanoid/industrial-robot sensing — a *new* TAM independent of autos that the market isn't pricing yet (a call option on the robotics theme).

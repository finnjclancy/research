# Unilever — Big Moves: what drove the largest single-day re-rates

> A low-beta staple — the big single-day moves are almost all **M&A / portfolio events**, not trading. Pairs with [factual §7](../03_companies/UNILEVER.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/UNILEVER_narrative.md), [history](../06_history/UNILEVER_history.md). Research date 2026-06-29. *(%/dates approximate.)*

## The big-move log

| Date | Move | What drove it |
|---|---|---|
| **17 Feb 2017** | **≈ +12–14%** (one of its biggest-ever 1-day jumps) | **Kraft Heinz's $143bn takeover approach** ($50/share, ~18% premium). Rejected within days and withdrawn — but it permanently re-rated how the market (and management) thought about Unilever's margins/value. ([HL](https://www.hl.co.uk/shares/share-research/201702/unilever-shares-jump-on-$143bn-kraft-heinz-approach)) |
| **Jan 2022** | **≈ −10%** | **The £50bn/$68bn bid for GSK Consumer Health** — investors revolted at the price/strategy; the stock fell hard and Unilever walked away. Drew activist Nelson Peltz onto the board. ([Fierce Pharma](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/no-deal-for-gsk-consumer-health-unilever-stops-raising-68b-bid-amid-investor-pushback)) |
| **Mar 2020 (COVID)** | **≈ −25%** then defensive recovery | Broad crash; staples bid back as a defensive. |
| **2018 (dual-structure U-turn)** | **moves on governance** | Abandoned plan to drop the Anglo-Dutch structure (UK shareholder revolt); later unified to London (2020). |
| **Dec 2025 / Mar 2026 (portfolio surgery)** | **re-rating on focus** | Ice Cream demerger + the Foods→McCormick combination — reshaping toward higher-growth/GLP-1-light categories. |
| **Quarterly USG prints** | **±3–6%** | Underlying-sales-growth *volume vs price* split is the swing — volume-led beats re-rate; price-only growth de-rates. |

## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **M&A is the single-day driver** — the biggest moves (Kraft Heinz +, GSK −) are deal events, not the operating quarter. A low-beta compounder gets repriced by *structural* news.
2. **The market polices capital allocation** — it cheered a takeover *of* Unilever (+12%) and punished a bad deal *by* Unilever (−10%). Discipline matters.
3. **Operationally it trades on the USG volume line** — slow grinds, not gaps.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "reshaped RemainCo re-rates":** post Ice Cream + Foods, a focused, higher-margin, GLP-1-light Beauty/Personal/Home-Care compounder with returning volume growth (Q1-26 +2.9%) → the premium ~23× is justified and the multiple holds/expands.
- **Bear — "EM can't offset saturated DM":** developed-market volumes stall, EM wobbles, input costs (oil) squeeze margin → a premium multiple on low-single-digit growth de-rates.
- **Variant — "break-up value / activist optionality":** the serial portfolio surgery (Peltz-era) could continue; sum-of-the-parts + further disposals are an under-appreciated value lever.

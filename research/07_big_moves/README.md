# Big Moves — the comparable bank for pricing news

> One file per name cataloguing the **largest single-day moves and what drove each** — so when similar news breaks, you have a **historical analog to size the range** (the core job, and the calibration the [recap](../04_recaps/HOW_TO_RECAP.md) loop feeds). Each file ends with **fresh theses** (bull / bear / variant). Research date 2026-06-29.
>
> **★ Verification convention (every move is marked):** ✅ = date *and* % confirmed via a cited source · ◑ = event/date confirmed but the single-day % is approximate (not independently confirmed) · ⚠ = unverified, flagged. **Honesty note:** for many European industrials/utilities/staples, exact historic single-day percentages are not reliably web-surfaceable, so those moves are anchored to a **verified dated event + driver** rather than a possibly-wrong number. US/large-cap and event-day moves (earnings, deals, trial readouts) are mostly ✅.

## ★ The taxonomy of big moves (catalyst types, ranked by typical magnitude)

1. **Binary pipeline/clinical readouts** — the most violent. *Novo CagriSema REDEFINE 1 (−18% to −27%, the biggest 1-day in its history)*; REDEFINE 4 (−16%). A miss *vs management's own promises* is the worst combination (number + credibility).
2. **Forward-guidance withdrawal / cuts** — bigger than the print itself. *ASML −16% (bookings + 2025 guide cut), −11% ("cannot confirm 2026"); Zalando −17% (profit warning); Novo −14% (guide to decline).* **A beat can sell off if the forward look disappoints (ASML Jul-25, AMD Apr-24, Amazon May-26).**
3. **M&A / deal events** — *Unilever +12% (Kraft Heinz approach), −10% (GSK bid); AMD +24% (OpenAI deal); Embracer −40% (Saudi/Savvy collapse).* Structural news repriced even low-beta names.
4. **Systemic / contagion shocks** — *COVID Mar-2020 (−25% to −40% across every name); the Mar-2023 SVB/Credit Suisse bank rout (−7%+ one-day for the banks).* You fall on *others'* failures.
5. **Political / sovereign shocks** — *French snap election Jun-2024 (BNP −8% intraday, the trio −10–14%).* The OAT/BTP spread is the European-bank/Italian-name swing.
6. **Macro-tape days (not company news)** — *AMD's & Amazon's single biggest UP days (+24% / +12%) were a tariff-pause and a soft-CPI market rally.* High-beta names move most on the *market*, not themselves.
7. **Geographic / commodity / regulatory shocks** — *Carlsberg (Russia 2015 & the 2022–23 Baltika exit); Iberdrola/Enel (2022 energy crisis + windfall taxes); the auto-cycle (Melexis −14% in 2019).*

## The meta-lessons (what to internalise for the reflex)
- **The biggest moves come from *expectations vs reality* — especially forward guidance and binary events — not the reported quarter.** Always anchor to what's priced.
- **A beat is not enough at a high multiple or against a high bar** (ASML, AMD, Amazon). Conversely, **washed-out names pop on minor good news** (Novo) — positioning is the amplifier.
- **Know whether a name's big moves are idiosyncratic or macro** — AMD/Amazon are high-beta to the tape; ASML/Novo move on their own catalysts; banks move on systemic + sovereign risk.
- **Use the analog:** when news breaks, ask *"how did this name (or a peer) move on similar news before?"* — these files are where the comparable lives. That's the difference between guessing a range and pricing one.

## How this connects
The Big Moves files are the **evidence base** for the [`NARRATIVE_ENGINE`](../00_system/NARRATIVE_ENGINE.md) reflex and the [recap](../04_recaps/) calibration loop: priors should be *built from comparables*, and every recap that marks a prediction to market adds a new data point here.

## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **Expectations are the whole game.** AMD's *down* days (Apr-24, Oct-24) were on *beats* that didn't clear an already-high bar. At ~58× forward, in-line ≠ good.
2. **Deal/validation news is the biggest *up* catalyst** — MI300 launch (Dec-23), OpenAI (Oct-25). The market pays for *credibility vs Nvidia*, datably.
3. **It's high-beta to the macro/AI-sentiment tape** — its single biggest up day (Apr-25) was a tariff-pause market rally, not a company event.
4. **The inference-share datapoint** (Data Center growth rate) is the quarter's swing variable.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "inference is the leveler":** ROCm matures, MI400 (2nm) ships on time, more hyperscalers sign → AMD takes real *inference* share toward double digits; the deal backlog (OpenAI/Meta/Oracle) converts → the 58× multiple is defended by 40%+ growth.
- **Bear — "custom ASICs eat the merchant GPU":** Broadcom-built in-house silicon (TPU/Trainium/Maia) bypasses *both* Nvidia and AMD; AMD's share plateaus at 20–25%, the warrants dilute ~20%, and a single MI400 slip resets the narrative at a priced-for-perfection multiple.
- **Variant — "second-source premium is structural":** even if AMD never out-designs Nvidia, hyperscalers *must* fund a #2 for negotiating leverage and supply security → a durable, profitable franchise the bears under-rate.

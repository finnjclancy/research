## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **Leverage amplifies the rate beta** — Enel's higher debt makes it the *more* rate-sensitive utility; it falls harder than Iberdrola in a yield shock and rallies harder on a dovish turn.
2. **The deleveraging trajectory is the re-rate** — the Cattaneo turnaround compressed the risk premium; net debt is the metric the market grades.
3. **LatAm is the idiosyncratic tail** (FX/political) that Iberdrola doesn't carry.
4. **Windfall taxes / Italian-state influence** are the policy swings.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "deleveraging + a dovish pivot":** continued debt reduction (toward ND/EBITDA ~2.0×) + falling yields (if oil keeps easing) → Enel, as the highest-rate-leverage utility, *outperforms* on a dovish turn; +18% EPS to 2028 delivered.
- **Bear — "higher-for-longer + LatAm shock":** sticky rates cap a still-levered name and an EM FX/political hit (Chile/Brazil/Colombia) or a fresh windfall tax dents earnings.
- **Variant — "the cheap, high-leverage rate-cut option":** if you believe rates fall, Enel is the most geared utility to that view in the book (more upside than premium-priced Iberdrola), with a turnaround + buyback underneath.

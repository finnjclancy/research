# Embracer — Big Moves: what drove the largest single-day re-rates

> A special-situation name — the big moves are **financing/corporate-action shocks**, not the games cycle. Pairs with [factual §7](../03_companies/EMBRACER.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/EMBRACER_narrative.md), [history](../06_history/EMBRACER_history.md). Research date 2026-06-29. *(%/dates approximate.)*

## The big-move log

| Date | Move | What drove it |
|---|---|---|
| **2020–Jul 2021 (the rollup boom)** | **≈ +30× from 2016 IPO to peak** | The debt/equity-funded acquisition supernova (27 deals in 12 months; Gearbox, Saber, Asmodee) → briefly Europe's most valuable games company (>$11bn). |
| **24 May 2023** | **≈ −40%+ in a day** | **The Saudi/Savvy $2bn deal collapsed.** A ~$2bn, 6-year contracted-development partnership (with PIF-backed Savvy Games) fell through at the last minute, leaving Embracer with >$2bn debt and no funding; it cut its forecast (FY guide from ~$965m–1.3bn to ~$655–840m) and launched mass layoffs/restructuring. ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2023/05/24/embracer-group-deal-collapse), [Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/report-embracer-s-doomed-2b-deal-was-with-saudi-backed-savvy-games)) |
| **2023–2024 (restructuring)** | **continued de-rating + volatility** | Studio closures, 900+ layoffs, 15+ cancelled projects; the over-levered empire unwound. |
| **Feb 2025 / Dec 2025 (spin-offs)** | **re-rating of the parts** | Asmodee listed (Feb-25, re-rated up); Coffee Stain listed (Dec-25, *de-rated*) — spin-offs don't guarantee a re-rate. |
| **2026 (Fellowship)** | **corporate-action driven** | The remaining business → Fellowship Entertainment (LOTR/Tomb Raider), Nasdaq Stockholm listing path. |

## What the big moves teach (the pattern)
1. **Single-financing dependence is the killer** — the −40% day (May-23) was one deal falling through, not an operating miss. Debt-funded rollups are fragile to a single capital source.
2. **It's a corporate-action stock** — the big moves are deal collapses and spin-offs, not game sales. Trade the structure, not a P/E.
3. **Spin-offs cut both ways** — Asmodee re-rated up, Coffee Stain down. Don't assume value-unlock = re-rate.

## Fresh theses
- **Bull — "sum-of-the-parts + IP catalysts":** the listed pieces (Asmodee + Coffee Stain + Fellowship) are worth more apart, net of debt; LOTR/Tomb Raider IP ("among the most undervalued") gets monetised via the Tomb Raider series (2027) + LOTR film (Dec 2027) + a new LOTR RPG → SOTP re-rate.
- **Bear — "spin-offs disappoint + hit-driven misses":** the parts don't re-rate (Coffee Stain precedent), a key AAA game slips/flops, and the complex restructuring keeps visibility poor.
- **Variant — "deep-value optionality, low correlation":** macro-uncorrelated, idiosyncratic special situation — a portfolio diversifier whose payoff depends on corporate-action execution + IP, not the market/rate/AI cycle.

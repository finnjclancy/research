## PART 0 — The one equation (read this first)

**Magnitude of a move ≈ (surprise vs what was *priced in*) × (the stock's *sensitivity* to that variable) × (the *positioning* amplifier), signed by whether the news *confirms* or *breaks* the dominant narrative.**

Five corollaries that explain almost every move below:
1. **News only moves a stock vs expectations.** "Good news" with nothing priced against it = no move. The whole game is the *gap* to consensus.
2. **A beat can fall.** If the bar was already sky-high (AMD Apr-24, −9% on a beat) or the *forward* outlook softens (ASML Jul-25, −11% on a Q2 beat), the stock drops.
3. **A wash-out can pop on minor good news.** When positioning is clean and sentiment dead, small positives rip (Novo Aug-23 +17%; the May-26 pill pop).
4. **Forward guidance > the printed quarter.** The next-year outlook moves more than the result (ASML "cannot confirm 2026"; Amazon's capex guide).
5. **Some names move on the *market*, not themselves.** High-beta names' biggest days are often macro (AMD +23.8% on a tariff pause; Amazon +12.2% on a CPI print).

## PART 2 — ★ THE MARKET DRIVERS (the core — learn these)

> For each driver: **the mechanism · most-sensitive names · typical magnitude · documented examples · what to watch · the nuance.**

### Driver 1 — AI capex cycle / order bookings
- **Mechanism:** the AI build-out is funded by hyperscaler capex (~$725bn in 2026). That capex *is* the order book for the AI supply chain. Bookings/orders are a leading indicator of revenue 1–2 years out, so the market trades the *order surprise*, not the printed quarter.
- **Most sensitive:** ASML (litho monopoly), AMD (accelerators), Amazon (AWS = both spender and seller), ABB (data-centre electrical equipment), Enel/Iberdrola (data-centre power demand).
- **Typical magnitude:** ±10–20% on a bookings/guide surprise (semis); ±5–10% on order/capex commentary (ABB, Amazon).
- **Examples:** ASML −16% (Oct-24 bookings miss); ASML +11% (Jan-25 bookings beat); AMD +23.7% (OpenAI deal); Amazon −18% (capex/FCF fear).
- **What to watch:** ASML net bookings vs consensus; hyperscaler capex guides (**Amazon's is the cleanest tell for the whole complex**); TSMC/Intel/Samsung/SK Hynix capex; Nvidia results.
- **★ Nuance:** this is the book's biggest *shared* exposure — one capex guide-down hits ASML+AMD+Amazon+ABB+utilities together, and because of the leverage in the financing chain, the move *overshoots*.

### Driver 2 — Forward guidance (the next-year outlook)
- **Mechanism:** valuation discounts *future* cashflows, so a change in the forward outlook moves the stock more than the current result. A *beat* on the quarter can sell off if next year's guide softens.
- **Most sensitive:** every name, but most violently the high-multiple ones (ASML, AMD, Amazon, Zalando, Novo).
- **Typical magnitude:** ±8–18%.
- **Examples:** ASML −11% (Q2-25 *beat* but "cannot confirm 2026"); Novo −17% (guide to a 2026 *decline*); Zalando −17% (FY guide slashed); AMD −9% (in-line forward guide vs a high bar).
- **What to watch:** the year-n+1 language on the call; any words like "cannot confirm," "softer," "cautious," "decline."
- **★ Nuance:** the *direction of estimate revisions* is one of the most powerful return predictors — a guide that triggers a wave of analyst downgrades extends the move for days/weeks.

### Driver 3 — Binary clinical / pipeline readouts
- **Mechanism:** a drug trial result is a discrete, high-stakes event that re-prices the entire future franchise in seconds. Worst case = a miss *vs management's own promises* (number + credibility).
- **Most sensitive:** Novo Nordisk (the only pure case in the book).
- **Typical magnitude:** ±15–27%.
- **Examples:** Novo −18% to −27% (CagriSema REDEFINE 1, vs the ≥25% promised — biggest day in its history); Novo −16% (REDEFINE 4 head-to-head failure); Novo +17% (SELECT trial success).
- **What to watch:** trial-readout calendar (amycretin/AMAZE); the *bar management set*; competitor data (Lilly) that reprices Novo by comparison.
- **★ Nuance:** these are the most violent moves in the book and the hardest to position for — size the range around the *probability-weighted* outcomes, and respect that a washed-out name (Novo now) has upside asymmetry.

### Driver 4 — M&A and strategic deals
- **Mechanism:** a takeover bid, a big acquisition, a divestment, or a transformative partnership reprices the equity on new strategic value — instantly, and even for low-beta names.
- **Most sensitive:** Unilever, Embracer, AMD, ABB, BNP, Carlsberg.
- **Typical magnitude:** target of a bid +10–15%; a *bad* deal by the company −10%; a deal collapse −40%; a validation partnership +20%+.
- **Examples:** Unilever +12% (Kraft Heinz approach) / −10% (GSK bid backlash); AMD +23.7% (OpenAI); Embracer −40% (Saudi/Savvy collapse); ABB (Power Grids→Hitachi; Robotics→SoftBank).
- **What to watch:** the market polices capital allocation — it *rewards* being a target and *punishes* over-paying. Deal-funding dependence is a fragility (Embracer).
- **★ Nuance:** spin-offs cut both ways — Embracer's Asmodee re-rated *up*, Coffee Stain *down*. "Value unlock" ≠ guaranteed re-rate.

### Driver 5 — Rates & central banks (the rate axis)
- **Mechanism:** rates set the discount rate on all cashflows *and* bank net-interest margins. Higher rates help banks (NII) and hurt long-duration assets (utilities = bond proxies; high-multiple growth via the discount rate). The 2026 oil-shock flipped the ECB to its first hike since 2023 and the Warsh Fed hawkish.
- **Most sensitive:** **Banks ↑ (BNP, ACA, BMED)** vs **Utilities ↓ (Enel, Iberdrola)** — a natural pairs trade. Secondary: high-multiple growth (ASML/AMD/Amazon/Zalando) via the discount rate.
- **Typical magnitude:** ±2–6% on a CB day / surprise CPI; the *structural* re-rate is bigger (the 2022 bank re-rating; the 2022–23 utility de-rating).
- **Examples:** banks' 2022 re-rate up on ECB lift-off; utilities' 2022–23 de-rate as yields surged; Enel's leverage makes it the most rate-geared utility (falls/rallies hardest).
- **What to watch:** ECB/Fed meetings + dots; eurozone & US CPI; the Bund/Treasury 10y; **the bank/utility ratio vs the 10y yield** (the cleanest rotation tell).
- **★ Nuance:** the *same* hike that re-rates banks can also cause the recession that lifts their cost of risk — the bank bull case is "rates up, but credit holds."

### Driver 6 — Systemic / contagion (banking crises)
- **Mechanism:** a bank failure triggers counterparty/contagion fear; *all* banks fall together regardless of their own health, then mean-revert.
- **Most sensitive:** BNP, ACA, BMED (and the whole sector).
- **Typical magnitude:** −5% to −10% one-day for the sector; individual failures far more (Credit Suisse −24% in a day).
- **Examples:** Mar 13–15 2023 SVB/Credit Suisse — European bank sector −7.1% one-day (steepest in >1yr); COVID Mar-2020 −40%.
- **What to watch:** credit spreads (the early-warning system); deposit-flight headlines; any single-bank stress; **AI-financing fragility** (neocloud GPU debt, private credit) as the *next* potential contagion source.
- **★ Nuance:** these are often the best *buying* opportunities for survivors (BNP fell on others' failures in 2023, then recovered) — but only if the contagion doesn't become fundamental.

### Driver 7 — Political & sovereign shocks
- **Mechanism:** elections, fiscal blow-ups, and policy threats reprice sovereign risk (bond spreads) and sector-specific policy risk (bank taxes, utility windfall taxes).
- **Most sensitive:** French banks (BNP, ACA) via the OAT-Bund spread; Italian names (Enel, BMED) via BTP; utilities via windfall taxes.
- **Typical magnitude:** −5% to −14% over the episode.
- **Examples:** Macron's June-2024 snap election — BNP −8% at open, the trio −10–14% over weeks (fiscal + bank-tax fears); Spain's 2022 utility windfall tax (1.2% of sales).
- **What to watch:** election calendars, budget/fiscal events, the OAT-Bund and BTP-Bund spreads, proposed sector taxes.
- **★ Nuance:** diversification cushions it — BNP (less French-exposed) fell *less* than SocGen/ACA on the same news.

### Driver 8 — Macro tape days (CPI, jobs, tariffs)
- **Mechanism:** a market-wide risk-on/off day driven by a macro print drags high-beta names regardless of company news.
- **Most sensitive:** the high-beta US names (AMD, Amazon) and growth multiples generally.
- **Typical magnitude:** ±8–24% for high-beta on a big macro day.
- **Examples:** AMD +23.8% (Apr-9-2025, Trump tariff pause); Amazon +12.2% (Nov-10-2022, soft CPI); Amazon −8.3% (Aug-5-2024, weak jobs + carry-unwind).
- **What to watch:** US CPI/PCE, jobs (NFP), tariff/trade headlines, the VIX.
- **★ Nuance:** a stock's *biggest* day is sometimes a macro day, not a company day — don't over-attribute a move to fundamentals; check the tape.

### Driver 9 — Energy / commodities / windfall taxes
- **Mechanism:** energy is the upstream driver of inflation→rates→everything; commodity input costs hit margins; energy-crisis windfalls trigger sector taxes.
- **Most sensitive:** Utilities (power prices + windfall taxes: Iberdrola, Enel); staples/industrials input costs (Unilever, Carlsberg, ABB, Melexis); the whole book via oil→inflation→rates.
- **Typical magnitude:** sector-specific (utility windfall-tax overhangs are multi-quarter, not single-day).
- **Examples:** 2022 European energy crisis + Spanish/Italian windfall taxes; the 2026 Iran/Hormuz oil shock that drove the ECB hike.
- **What to watch:** Brent, gas, power prices; OPEC+; Middle East/Strait risk; proposed windfall taxes.
- **★ Nuance:** energy is *the connective tissue* — it cascades into rates (Driver 5) and thus the whole bank/utility axis.

### Driver 10 — China (property, consumer, chips, stimulus)
- **Mechanism:** China is a major end-market; its property cycle, consumer health, chip policy, and stimulus move the China-exposed names.
- **Most sensitive:** Kone (property — the cleanest proxy), Carlsberg (consumer/beer), Unilever (EM), ASML (chip demand/export controls), Melexis (autos/localization — the one name where China is a *growth* story).
- **Typical magnitude:** Kone/Carlsberg ±5–10% on China data/earnings; ASML on China-% + export-control headlines.
- **Examples:** Kone's 2022–24 de-rate (China property bust, China sales −20%); Carlsberg's China-sensitive quarters; ASML's China-revenue cliff guidance.
- **What to watch:** China property prices/starts, credit impulse, consumer confidence, stimulus ("two new"/treasury bonds — now funding Kone *modernisation*), CNY, export-control policy.
- **★ Nuance:** watch the *second derivative* — China-exposed names can re-rate on signs China is *less-bad*/bottoming, even with absolute weakness still.

### Driver 11 — FX / the dollar
- **Mechanism:** EUR-reporting names earning in USD/EM get translation swings; a weak dollar / "Sell America" rotation favours ex-US/European assets.
- **Most sensitive:** Unilever (EM/USD), ASML (USD bookings), the banks (relative beneficiaries of a weak-dollar rotation).
- **Typical magnitude:** a slow translation drag/boost, not usually a single-day catalyst (except on big DXY moves).
- **What to watch:** DXY, EUR/USD, USD/CNY; Fed-independence/"Sell America" narratives.

### Driver 12 — Profit warnings & cyclical inflections (the cyclicals)
- **Mechanism:** for cyclical industrials/auto-semis, a profit warning (cut guidance mid-cycle) or an inventory inflection drives the move.
- **Most sensitive:** Melexis (auto cycle), Kone (China construction), ABB (short-cycle orders), Zalando (consumer).
- **Typical magnitude:** −5% to −17% on a warning; sharp rallies off troughs.
- **Examples:** Melexis profit warnings (Oct-2023, Oct-2024; FY24 sales −3%, Q4 −21%); Zalando −17% (2022 warning); Kone EPS −32% (2022).
- **What to watch:** auto production (SAAR) + inventory for Melexis; China orders for Kone; book-to-bill for ABB; the European consumer for Zalando.
- **★ Nuance:** early-cyclicals move *sharply off troughs* on the *second derivative* — the "less-bad" inflection re-rates before the absolute numbers turn.

### Driver 13 — Corporate actions (spin-offs, breakups, dividend bans)
- **Mechanism:** structural events — spin-offs, demergers, breakups, forced dividend cuts — reprice the equity on sum-of-the-parts or capital-return changes.
- **Most sensitive:** Embracer (breakup/SOTP), Unilever (Ice Cream/Foods demergers), ABB (Power Grids/Robotics/Accelleron), banks (the 2020 ECB dividend ban).
- **Typical magnitude:** varies — a deal collapse −40% (Embracer); a demerger re-rate; the dividend ban capped bank recoveries in 2020.
- **What to watch:** each structural milestone, and whether the spun pieces actually re-rate (mixed: Asmodee up, Coffee Stain down).

### Driver 14 — Positioning / sentiment (the amplifier on everything)
- **Mechanism:** *how the trade is positioned* amplifies the reaction. Crowded longs fall harder on disappointment; washed-out names pop harder on good news.
- **Most sensitive:** crowded AI longs (ASML, AMD, Amazon — asymmetric *down*); washed-out names (Novo — asymmetric *up*).
- **Examples:** Novo +17% / pill-pop (washed-out → upside asymmetry); ASML/AMD's hard falls on disappointment (crowded → downside asymmetry).
- **What to watch:** short interest, fund ownership, "crowded long/short" surveys, how the stock *responds* to news (does it shrug off good news? = exhausted bulls).
- **★ Nuance:** this is *the* factor most people under-weight. When you reason from fundamentals but the stock does the opposite, it's usually positioning.

## PART 5 — The cross-asset cascade (how one shock ripples the whole book)

**The chains matter more than any single move:**

1. **Energy → inflation → rates → the book.** Oil shock (2026 Iran/Hormuz) → inflation → ECB/Fed hawkish → **banks ↑ (NII), utilities ↓ (discount rate), growth multiples ↓**. *Reverse it when oil falls (dovish pivot): utilities + growth re-rate up, banks cap.*
2. **AI capex → the supply chain.** A hyperscaler capex guide (Amazon first) → ASML/AMD bookings → ABB data-centre orders → Enel/Iberdrola power demand. *One crack hits all five; the financing leverage makes it overshoot.*
3. **China → the China cluster.** China property/consumer/stimulus → Kone (property), Carlsberg (beer), Unilever (EM), ASML/Melexis (chips). *Stimulus is the shared upside catalyst.*
4. **Systemic/credit → the banks → risk-off everywhere.** A banking-crisis or private-credit event → BNP/ACA/BMED contagion → broad de-risking.
5. **GLP-1 → consumption.** Obesity drugs → Novo (maker) vs Unilever/Carlsberg (demand destruction).

## PART 6 — How to use this when news breaks (the reflex)

1. **Classify the driver** — which of the 14 above does the news touch?
2. **Find the analog** — locate the closest documented move in Part 1/Part 2. *"How did this name (or a peer) move on similar news before?"*
3. **Adjust for what's priced** — is the surprise bigger/smaller than the analog's? Is the bar higher now?
4. **Apply the positioning amplifier** — crowded long (bigger down-move) or washed-out (bigger up-move)?
5. **Size the range** — use the Part-3 ladder for the archetype, scaled by 1–4.
6. **Output:** *stock — range — reason — follow-up.* Then log the prediction in a recap and mark it to market (that's how the reflex compounds).

> **The meta-lesson of every move above:** the market pays for *the gap between what happens and what was already believed.* Know what's priced, know the sensitivity, know the positioning — and the magnitude becomes predictable.

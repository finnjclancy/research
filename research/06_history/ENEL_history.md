# Enel — 10-Year History, Trajectory & Analyst Sentiment

> Two CEOs, two strategies: **Starace built the green giant (2014–2023) — and over-levered it; Cattaneo (2023–) de-risked and re-focused.** The stock's perception swung from "growth utility" → "balance-sheet worry" → "disciplined turnaround." ## 2. The two eras (the key to the perception)
**★ The Starace era (2014–2023) — "Open Power / build the green giant":** Francesco Starace (CEO May 2014, ex-Enel Green Power) reorganised Iberia/LatAm, integrated sustainability, and **massively expanded renewables** — by 2019, **renewable capacity (42 GW) exceeded thermal (39 GW)** for the first time, with 3,029 MW added that year alone. TSR 2015–2019 was **+131% vs +41% for the FTSE MIB and +56% for Stoxx Utilities** — huge outperformance. **But the aggressive capex pushed net debt from €37.5bn (2015) to €45bn+ (2019) and beyond ~€60bn by 2022** — and the over-extension (plus rising rates) became the bear case.

**★ The Cattaneo era (2023–) — "discipline & deleveraging":** Flavio Cattaneo took over (May 2023) and **pivoted hard to balance-sheet repair** — asset disposals, capital discipline, a leaner geographic focus (Italy/Iberia/LatAm/US), net debt falling (ND/EBITDA 2.7→2.4×). The perception shifted from "indebted, sprawling green-growth utility" to "**de-risked, disciplined income compounder**." (Russia exit / Baltica-type disposals were part of the 2022–23 clean-up.)

## 3. Perception evolution
1. **2015–2019 — "the green-growth utility":** Starace's renewables build-out + outperformance; a *growth* utility.
2. **2020–2022 — "the over-levered ESG darling":** huge capex/ambition; debt + rate sensitivity became the worry.
3. **2023–2026 — "the Cattaneo turnaround":** deleveraging + buyback + capital discipline; re-rating on *de-risking* (see [narrative map](../05_narratives/ENEL_narrative.md)).

## 4. Analyst sentiment (Jun 2026)
- Owned for **yield + the deleveraging re-rate**; ~14× earnings, ~5% dividend, stock +13% YTD near analyst fair value — see [factual file §0/§8](../03_companies/ENEL.md). The €53bn 2026–28 plan targets +18% EPS to 2028.
- **The debate:** does the turnaround keep compressing the risk premium (ND/EBITDA → 2.0×, re-rate), or does higher-for-longer rates + LatAm/Italian risk cap a still-levered utility? **Watch the 10y bond yield and the net-debt line — those set the multiple.**

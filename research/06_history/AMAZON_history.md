# Amazon — 10-Year History, Trajectory & Analyst Sentiment

> The throughline of the decade: **AWS profits quietly subsidised a low-margin retail empire**, then advertising + retail efficiency diversified the profit base — interrupted by the **2022 overbuild bust**, now defined by the **AI-capex epicentre** debate. Companions: [factual](../03_companies/AMAZON.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/AMAZON_narrative.md). Research date 2026-06-28.

---

## 1. The arc
**2015–2019 — "AWS is the profit engine":** AWS broke out as a reported segment in 2015 and immediately showed the pattern that defined the decade — **>60% of operating income on ~17% of revenue.** Retail was a low/no-margin share-grab funded by AWS. **2017: Whole Foods acquired ($13.7bn)** — the move into physical grocery ([Business Stats](https://businesstats.com/amazon-statistics-facts/)).

**2020–2021 — the COVID e-commerce surge:** demand exploded; Amazon hired 100k+ and massively built out fulfilment + cloud capacity. The stock soared.

**★ 2022 — the overbuild bust:** post-COVID demand normalised into an over-built cost base; Amazon posted its **first annual net loss in years** (partly a Rivian markdown), did its largest-ever layoffs (~27k), and the **stock fell ~50%.** The lesson: *Amazon over-built into a demand pull-forward* — directly relevant to today's AI-capex debate.

**2023–2025 — the diversified recovery:** AWS growth re-accelerated, **advertising matured (~$70bn trailing)**, retail margins expanded (regionalised logistics + automation), and **international retail turned profitable.** The profit base finally diversified beyond AWS.

**2026 — the AI-capex epicentre:** AWS +28% (fastest since late 2022), a **$364bn+ forward-demand book + Trainium**, but **~$200bn of capex** with FCF crossing zero — the stock fell ~18% from its May high *despite* a beat (see [narrative map](../05_narratives/AMAZON_narrative.md)).

## 2. Perception evolution
1. **2015–2019 — "unprofitable retail subsidised by AWS":** the AWS-is-everything frame.
2. **2020–2021 — "the COVID winner":** e-commerce + cloud secular shift, priced richly.
3. **2022 — "the overbuilt loss-maker":** demand normalisation + cost bloat → −50%.
4. **2023–2025 — "the three-engine compounder":** AWS + ads + retail margin → re-rate.
5. **2026 — "AI-capex epicentre":** AWS reaccel vs the $200bn-capex/FCF question.

## 3. The lesson in the arc
Amazon has *already lived* a capex-overbuild bust (2022) — which is exactly why the market is hyper-sensitive to the current **$200bn AI capex** and why Amazon's capex guide is the cleanest trigger for the whole AI complex ([S3](../01_macro/MACRO_SCENARIO_TREE.md)). It's the most important read-through on the watchlist.

## 4. Analyst sentiment (Jun 2026)
- Broadly bullish on the three-engine story, but the **capex/FCF compression** makes valuation optically harder and sentiment fragile (the ~18% drawdown from the May ATH) — see [factual file §0/§8](../03_companies/AMAZON.md).
- **The debate:** AWS reacceleration validates the capex (re-rate) vs an AI-capex overbuild repeat of 2022. **Watch AWS growth + the capex guide *together*.**

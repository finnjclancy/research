# 10-Year Histories — the path each name has taken

> One file per name: the **decade financial arc**, the **perception evolution** (how the market's view swung), **key inflection points**, and **current sell-side/buy-side sentiment**. The point: to *see the path* — pre-COVID baseline, COVID shock, post-COVID, today — so that when news breaks you have **comparables** for how the stock has re-rated on similar information before.

## ★ The cross-cutting decade lessons (patterns that repeat)

**1. The "perception round-trip" — COVID pull-forward names.** Zalando (€105 → €27) and Amazon (the 2022 −50% overbuild bust) both show how the market **extrapolates a demand pull-forward as permanent, then de-rates brutally on normalisation.** *This is the live template for the AI-capex debate* — is AI a structural shift or a pull-forward? Amazon has lived this exact movie once.

**2. Turnarounds re-rate on *self-help*, not the cycle.** ABB (Spiesshofer → Rosengren decentralization), Enel (Starace build-out → Cattaneo deleveraging), AMD (near-bankruptcy → Zen), Unilever (Kraft Heinz bid → portfolio surgery) — each re-rated when management **fixed the company**, often independent of the macro. Watch for the *self-help* inflection.

**3. The banks' decade is one story: negative rates → 2022 lift-off.** BNP and ACA ground sideways at sub-0.5× book through the negative-rate wilderness (2014–2021), then re-rated on the rate normalisation. The **2020 COVID dividend ban** still shapes how the market trusts bank capital return. The 2026 oil-shock hike is the *latest* twist of the same rate story.

**4. Narrative stocks re-rate violently *both ways* on expectations vs reality.** Novo did the full boom-and-bust *within the decade* — 2016 insulin crash → GLP-1 supernova (Europe's #1) → −75% CagriSema/Lilly bust. The market paid up for "≥25%" promises, then punished the miss. The current washed-out setup is the mirror of 2021.

**5. China is the swing in two names' whole decade.** Kone rode the 2010s China new-build boom to dividend-aristocrat status, then the 2020s property bust reframed it as a services-resilience story. Carlsberg's decade was bookended by Eastern crises (2015 Russia shock → 2023 Baltika exit) with China the growth swing.

**6. Debt-funded empires are fragile.** Embracer's rollup (>$11bn) collapsed when a single $2bn financing (Saudi/Savvy) fell through — now a sum-of-the-parts breakup. A reminder for the AI complex's circular financing (see [AI_STACK_WARS](../01_macro/AI_STACK_WARS.md) §4).

**7. The quiet compounders just kept compounding.** ASML (~5× sales/decade as EUV went from doubted to monopoly to AI toll-booth), Iberdrola (Galán's consistent 20-year networks+renewables build), Melexis (content-per-car), Banca Mediolanum (AUM gathering) — less drama, steady re-rates, with the *narrative* (not just the numbers) doing the multiple work.

## How to use a history file live
When news breaks on a name, open its history to ask: **"how did this stock move on *similar* news before?"** — the Oct-2024 ASML bookings shock, Novo's 2016 insulin crash, Amazon's 2022 overbuild, Zalando's 2022 normalisation. The market hands you comparables; the history files are where they live.

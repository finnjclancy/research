# Carlsberg — 10-Year History, Trajectory & Analyst Sentiment

> Bookended by two crises in its Eastern exposure — the **2015 Russia/Baltika profit shock** that triggered the SAIL turnaround, and the **2023 Russia exit** (a ~DKK 48bn loss) — with premiumisation + Asia growth the throughline. Companions: [factual](../03_companies/CARLSBERG.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/CARLSBERG_narrative.md). Research date 2026-06-28.

---

## 1. The arc
**2015 — the Russia shock:** a collapse in Russian/Eastern-European beer demand forced **~2,000 job cuts + DKK 2bn cost savings**, and **9M-2015 swung to a DKK 2.665bn loss** (from DKK 4.66bn profit) on big impairments ([TAdviser](https://tadviser.com/index.php/Company:Carlsberg_Group)). New CEO **Cees 't Hart** arrived and launched **SAIL'22 (Mar 2016)** — "Strengthen the core, Position for growth, Winning culture, Deliver value," with a ~50% payout ([Carlsberg](https://www.carlsberggroup.com/who-we-are/about-the-carlsberg-group/our-strategy/)).

**2016–2019 — the SAIL'22 recovery:** margin expansion, premiumisation (craft — London Fields 2017), and **Asia (esp. China — Carlsberg's Western-China strength) as the growth engine.** A clean turnaround story.

**2020–2022 — COVID + SAIL'27:** pandemic disruption then recovery; SAIL'27 launched (2022) raising ambitions.

**★ 2023 — the Russia exit:** following the Ukraine war, Carlsberg **sold Baltika (Jun 2023)**, recognising a **~DKK 7bn impairment + DKK 41.5bn of accumulated FX/hedging losses = ~DKK 47.75bn total loss** from discontinuing Russia ([Interfax](https://interfax.com/newsroom/top-stories/99188/)). A massive, painful clean-up of the 2008-era Baltika acquisition.

**2024–2026 — Accelerate SAIL + Britvic:** **Accelerate SAIL (Feb 2024)** lifted the organic-growth ambition to **4–6%**, prioritising premium, **Beyond Beer**, and Asia (China/Vietnam/India). **Britvic acquired (£3.3bn)** for non-alc diversification (CEO **Jacob Aarup-Andersen** from 2023). See [narrative map](../05_narratives/CARLSBERG_narrative.md).

## 2. Perception evolution
1. **2015 — "the Russia casualty":** profit shock, impairments, new-CEO reset.
2. **2016–2019 — "the SAIL'22 turnaround":** margin + premium + Asia growth.
3. **2020–2023 — "premiumiser exiting Russia":** the Baltika sale removed an overhang (and a chunk of volume).
4. **2024–2026 — "value-over-volume + Beyond Beer":** premium mix + Britvic vs structural alcohol decline + China-consumer swing.

## 3. Analyst sentiment (Jun 2026)
- ~15× earnings (cheaper than Unilever), ~3.3% yield; the bull is premiumisation + Britvic synergies + China share gains, the bear is the China consumer + structural alcohol decline + (looming) GLP-1 — see [factual file §0/§7](../03_companies/CARLSBERG.md).
- **The debate:** can value (premium mix + non-alc) keep beating falling volumes, and does China stabilise? **Watch China beer value/volume + Britvic synergies.**

# Crédit Agricole — 10-Year History, Trajectory & Analyst Sentiment

> Same decade arc as BNP — **negative-rate wilderness → 2022 re-rating** — but a more fee/insurance-tilted, higher-yield, structurally-complex (CASA vs Group) version. Companions: [factual](../03_companies/CREDIT_AGRICOLE.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/CREDIT_AGRICOLE_narrative.md). Research date 2026-06-28.

---

## 1. The net-income arc (CASA, group share)
- **2015:** €6,043m net income; dividend €0.60 (+71%) — a strong start to the decade ([CA 2015 results](https://www.credit-agricole.com/en/finance/financial-press-releases/results-for-the-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2015)).
- **2016:** dividend €0.60 (cash).
- **2017:** ~€7bn — the **highest of the 2015–2023 span**; earnings were volatile through the period ([Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/225219/profit-and-loss-of-credit-agricole/)).
- **2018–2019:** solid, negative-rate-constrained.
- **2020:** **COVID** — provisions rose; per the ECB/ACPR freeze, CASA allocated the 2019 result to reserves to keep dividend options open ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-08-06/credit-agricole-sees-provisions-rise-as-covid-hits-big-clients)).
- **2021–2024:** the rate-lift-off recovery; rising NII + Amundi growth.
- **2025:** dividend **€1.13 (+3%)**, RoTE 13.5% — see [factual file](../03_companies/CREDIT_AGRICOLE.md).
- **Dividend has grown ~12%/yr over the decade but volatile** (payout ~53%) ([Simply Wall St](https://simplywall.st/stocks/fr/banks/epa-aca/credit-agricole-shares/dividend)).

## 2. The defining episodes (shared with BNP, plus CASA-specific)
- **Negative-rate wilderness (2014–2021):** NIM compression; cheap, un-loved.
- **COVID dividend freeze (2020):** the ECB/ACPR ban; CASA reserved the 2019 result.
- **2022 re-rating:** rate lift-off → NII surge → the European-bank re-rating.
- **★ The CASA-vs-Group complexity** has been a persistent perception drag — the listed entity is more market-sensitive (insurance + Amundi + CIB) than the retail-heavy Group, so CASA's earnings can lag the Group's in market-stress quarters (e.g. Q1-26).

## 3. Perception evolution
1. **2015–2021 — "the cheap, complex mutual-controlled bank":** discount for the structure + negative rates.
2. **2022–2024 — "the high-yield rate beneficiary":** NII + Amundi + a fat, growing dividend.
3. **2025–2026 — "deep-value, high-yield, fee-tilted":** ~7× earnings, ~6.9% yield, RoTE 13.5% (above BNP), but **Hold consensus** — cheap, but the market wants a catalyst (see [narrative map](../05_narratives/CREDIT_AGRICOLE_narrative.md)).

## 4. Analyst sentiment (Jun 2026)
- **Consensus: Hold**, avg PT ~€20.6 (~26% above the ~€16.4 spot), ~6.9% dividend yield — see [factual file §0/§8](../03_companies/CREDIT_AGRICOLE.md). Cheap and high-yielding, but the Hold reflects the missing near-term catalyst + CASA's market-beta.
- **The debate:** does the deep value close (NII + Amundi + ACT-2028 delivery → re-rate toward €20+) or does CASA stay a value trap with fee/insurance earnings lagging in any market wobble?

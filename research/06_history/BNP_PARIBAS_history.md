# BNP Paribas — 10-Year History, Trajectory & Analyst Sentiment

> The decade arc that defines every European bank: **the negative-rates wilderness (2015–2021) → the 2022 rate lift-off re-rating.** Plus the 2020 COVID dividend ban — the event that still shapes how the market trusts bank capital return. ## 2. The two defining episodes
- **★ The negative-rate wilderness (2014–2021):** with the ECB deposit rate below zero, NIM was crushed and European banks were seen as structurally un-investable — BNP traded **well below tangible book** (often ~0.4–0.5×) for years. The *narrative* was "value traps."
- **★ COVID dividend ban (Mar 2020):** the ECB/ACPR **ordered banks to freeze dividends** ([Jeantet](https://www.jeantet.fr/en/2020/04/banks-facing-acpr-call-and-ecb-recommandations-not-to-pay-dividends/)). Q1-20 net income fell to €1.3bn (from €1.9bn) with €502m of provisions ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2020/05/05/bnp-paribas-earnings-q1-2020-amid-coronavirus.html)). This still colours how the market prices bank capital-return durability.
- **★ The 2022 re-rating:** ECB lift-off ended the negative-rate era; NII surged; BNP sold **Bank of the West (~$16bn)** and funded big buybacks. The stock re-rated from ~0.4× toward ~0.8× book — the "great European bank re-rating."

## 3. Perception evolution
1. **2015–2021 — "structurally challenged value trap":** negative rates, litigation overhang (the 2014 US sanctions settlement), sub-0.5× book.
2. **2022–2024 — "rate beneficiary + capital return":** NII tailwind, buybacks, re-rating.
3. **2025–2026 — "the diversified champion that bought the fee pivot":** AXA IM deal; the oil-shock hike gives NII a *second wind* (see [narrative map](../05_narratives/BNP_PARIBAS_narrative.md)); new bear flags = private-credit + "AI disrupts banks."

## 4. Analyst sentiment (Jun 2026)
- **Consensus: Buy** (14 buy / 4 hold / 1 sell), avg PT ~€107 (~5% above spot), trading ~0.8× tangible book and ~8× FY27 earnings — see the [factual file §0/§8](../03_companies/BNP_PARIBAS.md). The Street likes the value + capital return but the PT implies the easy re-rating is largely done.
- **The debate:** can BNP sustain a >12–13% ROTE through the cycle (→ P/B toward 1.0×), or does a recession lift cost of risk and a private-credit scare cap it?

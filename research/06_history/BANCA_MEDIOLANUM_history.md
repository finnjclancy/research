# Banca Mediolanum — 10-Year History, Trajectory & Analyst Sentiment

> Unlike the universal banks, BMED's decade is a **structural AUM-compounding** story, not a rate story — steady managed-asset growth via the Family-Banker network, with a rate-driven NII kicker layered on from 2022. Companions: [factual](../03_companies/BANCA_MEDIOLANUM.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/BANCA_MEDIOLANUM_narrative.md). Research date 2026-06-28.

---

## 1. The trajectory (AUM + profit)
- **The decade story = compounding AUM.** Total assets grew steadily to a **record €155.8bn (+12%) in 2025** ([Investing.com FY25](https://uk.investing.com/news/company-news/banca-mediolanum-fy-2025-slides-net-income-up-11-aum-grows-12-93CH-4486003)) — a multi-year structural climb as Italian household savings migrated from deposits/BTPs into managed products via the advisor network.
- **Net income** climbed to a **record €1.24bn (+11%) in 2025**; net commission income €1.31bn (+12%), NII ~€812m ([Il Sole 24 Ore](https://en.ilsole24ore.com/art/banca-mediolanum-2025-record-profits-and-dividend-set-125-euro-AIsC0mDB)).
- **Dividend:** a high-yield, growing payout — €1.25/share for 2025 (base €0.80 + extraordinary), with countercyclical, fee-heavy revenue ([Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4794091-banca-mediolanum-high-dividend-yield-and-countercyclical-revenues)).
- *(Pre-2019 year-by-year figures are thinner in public aggregators — verify against BMED IR archives before quoting specific early-decade numbers.)*

## 2. The defining events
- **★ Founder-led model:** built by **Ennio Doris** on the tied "Family Banker" advisory network. **Ennio Doris died Nov 2021**; son **Massimo Doris** continues as CEO — a smooth, family-controlled succession (Doris family ~40% + Fininvest ~30%).
- **2020 COVID:** a market-driven dip in AUM/fees, then a strong recovery as inflows proved sticky (the "countercyclical revenues" thesis — advisors keep gathering through volatility).
- **★ 2022+ rate lift-off:** added a **new NII engine** on top of the AUM-fee model — NII +31% in Q1-26, +~15% guided for 2026. The structural gatherer suddenly also had a rate tailwind.

## 3. Perception evolution
1. **2015–2021 — "the steady Italian savings compounder":** quietly growing AUM + a fat dividend; a quality, lower-beta financial.
2. **2022–2024 — "compounder + rate kicker":** the NII tailwind boosted profits to records.
3. **2025–2026 — "resilient gatherer with a rate double-edge":** higher-for-longer lifts NII but makes deposits/BTPs a competing product (the inflow-cannibalisation watch) — see [narrative map](../05_narratives/BANCA_MEDIOLANUM_narrative.md).

## 4. Analyst sentiment (Jun 2026)
- Buy-leaning; PT ~€21 vs ~high-teens spot; valued at a **premium to plain Italian banks** for the quality/growth of the asset-gathering model — see [factual file §0/§7](../03_companies/BANCA_MEDIOLANUM.md). Low free float (~70% Doris/Fininvest) → can gap on monthly flow data.
- **The debate:** do managed inflows stay resilient (~€9bn) *despite* attractive risk-free yields (proving the advisor moat), or do savers drift to deposits/BTPs and stall the structural story?

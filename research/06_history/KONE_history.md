# Kone — 10-Year History, Trajectory & Analyst Sentiment

> The single cleanest read on the **Chinese construction cycle**: Kone rode the China new-building *boom* of the 2010s to dividend-aristocrat status, then the China property *bust* of the 2020s reframed it as a services-resilience story. Companions: [factual](../03_companies/KONE.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/KONE_narrative.md). Research date 2026-06-28.

---

## 1. The China-cycle arc
**2010s — the China new-build boom (the growth era):** China's elevator/escalator installed base grew at a **~+16% CAGR from 2012–2016 (2.0m → 4.3m units)**, and Kone held **>4.7% of the China market** ([Kone CMD 2017](https://www.kone.com/en/Images/KONE_CMD_2017_China_tcm17-60580.pdf)). China new-equipment demand was *the* growth engine. Revenue rose from a 2011–2015 range of **€6.3–8.65bn**, net income €0.64–1.05bn ([Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/246289/revenue-of-kone/)), and the stock **+112% over 2011–2016** — a quality compounder + **dividend aristocrat** (raised the dividend every year).

**Even at the peak, the seeds of the bear were visible:** as early as 2016–2017 Kone flagged **intense China competition** and a new-equipment market expected to be flat-to-declining in units — the margin pressure that would later bite.

**2020–2021 — COVID + the China peak:** a brief disruption then a peak in Chinese new-build.

**2022–2026 — the China property bust:** new-equipment demand fell sharply with the property downturn; **Greater China's order-book share dropped from ~40% (Q1-24) to ~30% (Q1-25)**. The investment case pivoted to the **maintenance & modernisation annuity** (recurring, high-margin) — and, newly, **Chinese stimulus-funded modernisation** (the "two new" + treasury-bond programs) as an offset (see [narrative map](../05_narratives/KONE_narrative.md)). Revenue reached ~€11.2bn by 2025.

## 2. Perception evolution
1. **2010s — "the China-construction growth compounder + dividend aristocrat":** owned for Chinese urbanisation exposure.
2. **2017–2021 — "premium quality, but China competition creeping":** still loved, margins under watch.
3. **2022–2026 — "China-property casualty cushioned by services":** the new-build collapse vs the resilient maintenance/modernisation annuity — the whole current debate.

## 3. Leadership
- Henrik Ehrnrooth led Kone for ~a decade (to mid-2020s); a new CEO has since taken over — *verify the current name/date before quoting.* The **Herlin family controls via A-shares** — a stable, long-term anchor throughout.

## 4. Analyst sentiment (Jun 2026)
- Owned as a defensive-quality compounder + a **China-property recovery option**; consensus PT (~€62) sits *above* the ~€51 spot — analysts see upside if China stabilises (see [factual file §0/§8](../03_companies/KONE.md)).
- **The debate:** does the China new-build trend bottom (its *second derivative* turning) with stimulus-funded modernisation + the services annuity dominating → re-rate; or does property keep deteriorating and pricing pressure cap margins? **The China order trend + the margin line are the tells.**

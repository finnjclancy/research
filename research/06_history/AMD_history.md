# AMD — 10-Year History, Trajectory & Analyst Sentiment

> One of tech's greatest turnarounds: from the **brink of bankruptcy (2014–15)** to a **~$680bn AI challenger**. How the Lisa Su era rebuilt the company and how the market's perception swung from left-for-dead to AI darling. Companions: [factual](../03_companies/AMD.md), [narrative](../05_narratives/AMD_narrative.md). Research date 2026-06-28.

---

## 1. The turnaround arc (the path over time)

**The crisis (2014–15):** When Lisa Su became CEO (Oct 2014), AMD was near collapse — market value **under $2bn**, a scattered low-margin portfolio losing share to Intel and Nvidia, the stock around **$2** ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2020/03/27/tech/lisa-su-amd-risk-takers), [Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Lisa_Su)).

**The bet — Zen (2015–16):** Su dropped the low-power/low-margin sprawl and made a **high-conviction bet on a clean-sheet CPU architecture, "Zen"** (led by Jim Keller) — funded *while the company was financially struggling*. This is the decision the whole turnaround rests on.

**The revival (2017–2019):** **Ryzen launched Mar 2017** (Zen, up to +52% IPC) → took desktop share from Intel; **EPYC** server chips opened the data-centre/cloud door (Amazon, Microsoft, Google adopting). **First net profit in 5 years in 2017**; revenue +7% (2016), +25% (2017) with accelerating quarterly growth ([Market Realist](https://marketrealist.com/2019/11/amds-turnaround-story-from-2012-to-2017-and-beyond/)).

**Diversification & scale (2020–2022):** the COVID PC boom + EPYC server share gains; non-CPU revenue went from **~10% of sales (2015) to ~40% (2021)**. **Xilinx acquired (~$49bn, closed Feb 2022)** added FPGAs/embedded. AMD became a credible Intel/Nvidia peer.

**The AI pivot (2023–2026):** the **Instinct MI300** (2023) launched AMD into AI accelerators; **MI350** scaled it; the **OpenAI (6GW) + Meta ($60bn) deals (2025)** and **MI400/2nm (2026)** made it *the* credible #2 to Nvidia. Market value reached **~$270bn (Oct 2025)** and ~$680bn by mid-2026 (≈$420/share). See [narrative map](../05_narratives/AMD_narrative.md).

## 2. Perception evolution (how the market's view swung)
1. **2015–16 — "left for dead":** priced for possible bankruptcy (~$2). A deep-value/turnaround speculation.
2. **2017–19 — "the Intel share-taker":** Ryzen/EPYC credibility; re-rated as a *CPU comeback* story.
3. **2020–22 — "the quality compounder":** data-centre + Xilinx; a legitimate large-cap semi.
4. **2023–24 — "the AI hopeful":** MI300 gave it an AI call-option; the stock began trading on *AI accelerator* optionality.
5. **2025–26 — "the credible #2 to Nvidia":** mega-deals + MI400 + the inference-share thesis → a crowded AI momentum long at ~58× forward. **The perception is now maximally optimistic — the inverse of 2015.**

## 3. Key inflection points
- **Oct 2014:** Lisa Su becomes CEO.
- **Mar 2017:** Ryzen launch — the turnaround's proof point.
- **2017:** first profit in 5 years.
- **Feb 2022:** Xilinx deal closes (~$49bn) — scale + diversification.
- **2023:** MI300 — the AI-accelerator entry.
- **2025:** OpenAI 6GW + Meta $60bn deals — the validation vs Nvidia (and ~20% warrant dilution).
- **Q1-26 (May-26):** +38% revenue, Data Center +57% → **+16% in a day** on the print.

## 4. Current sell-side / buy-side sentiment (Jun 2026)
- **Consensus: Buy / Strong Buy** — **35–51 analysts**, average targets clustering **~$430–$500** with a wide range **$235–$665** ([MarketBeat](https://www.marketbeat.com/stocks/NASDAQ/AMD/forecast/), [Public](https://public.com/stocks/amd/forecast-price-target)).
- **Notable broker moves (all raising):** **BofA (Vivek Arya) → $560** (42× 2027 EPS), **Citi (Atif Malik) upgrade to Buy → $575** (SOTP values the DC-GPU business alone at $281/sh), **Bernstein (Stacy Rasgon) → $600** (Outperform; 2027 EPS ~$14.60, path to ~$20 by 2028), **UBS → $670**. The bull case = inference share + MI400 + the deal backlog.
- **The bear/skeptic camp** (the wide $235 low end): software (ROCm vs CUDA) still lags, custom-ASIC (Broadcom) threat, ~58× multiple + dilution leave no room for a miss, hyperscaler share could plateau at 20–25%.
- **Net read:** a crowded, consensus-bullish AI long after a ~267% year. Like ASML, the asymmetry is to an AI-capex disappointment or a product slip — not to more good news. **The turnaround is complete and *priced*; the next chapter is the harder one (closing the gap with Nvidia).**

**The headline:** A cheaper, higher-yielding, more **diversified/fee-tilted** French bank than BNP, with a fortress Group CET1 (~17%) and a 6.9% dividend yield — but the listed stock (CASA) is more exposed to **capital-markets and insurance/asset-management swings**, which is exactly why Q1-26 net income *fell* during the Iran-war market turbulence even as the Group rose on retail NII.

## 1. The thesis & the narrative

**One line:** France's largest retail-banking group and a fee-income powerhouse (Amundi asset management, Crédit Agricole Assurances, CACEIS) — a high-yield, low-multiple value bank with a uniquely strong capital position but a complex listed-vs-group structure.

- **Bull story:** ~7× earnings, ~6.9% yield, fortress capital; diversified fee engines (Amundi €88bn 2025 inflows) cushion the cycle; ECB hikes lift retail NII (regional-bank NII +34.3% in Q1-26); ACT 2028 plan targets €8.5bn CASA net income and C/I <55% with a 50% cash payout + interim dividends from 2026.
- **Bear story:** **CASA's earnings are market-sensitive** (insurance + asset management + CIB) — a market drawdown hits them (Q1-26 down 7.9%); French sovereign/political risk; the consensus is only **Hold** (the value is recognised, the catalyst less clear); recession would lift cost of risk.
- **What the market pays for:** the **dividend + capital strength + fee-income resilience** — and increasingly, delivery of the ACT 2028 plan.

---

## 2. The business — structure matters

- **★ Listed entity = Crédit Agricole S.A. (CASA)**, the central body of the broader **Crédit Agricole Group**. The **Regional Banks (Caisses Régionales)** — *not* directly listed — own a controlling stake in CASA (via SAS Rue La Boétie) and drive much of the Group's retail NII. **So "Group" results (e.g. €2,097m Q1-26 net income) include the regional banks; "CASA" results (€1.56bn) are what the *listed share* earns.** Always specify which.
- **Divisions (CASA):** French retail (LCL + the regional-bank franchise), International retail, **Asset gathering** (Crédit Agricole Assurances — record Q1-26 premiums; **Amundi**, Europe's largest asset manager, ~60%-owned), **Large Customers** (CACIB corporate & investment bank, CACEIS securities services), and Specialised Financial Services (consumer finance, leasing).
- **★ Amundi** is the crown-jewel fee engine: **€88bn net inflows in 2025 (1.6× 2024)** ([MarketScreener FY25](https://www.marketscreener.com/news/credit-agricole-s-a-results-for-the-4th-quarter-and-full-year-2025-ce7e5bd3de80f52c)).

## 3. Income statement — the lines that matter

> Same bank toolkit as BNP (NII, cost of risk, C/I, CET1, RoTE), plus a heavier **fee/insurance** weighting:

- **★ Net interest income** — the ECB-rate lever; **regional-bank NII +34.3% YoY in Q1-26** is the standout ([GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/30/3284424/0/en/credit-agricole-sa-first-quarter-2026-results-robust-results-despite-market-turbulence.html)).
- **★ Fee & insurance income** (Amundi, CA Assurances) — bigger swing factor than at BNP; **market-sensitive** (down in Q1-26 turbulence).
- **★ Cost of risk** — the credit-cycle gauge (same as BNP — watch in any recession).
- **C/I ratio:** Group Q1-26 60.3% (−1.3pt YoY); **ACT 2028 target <55%** ([MarketScreener FY25](https://www.marketscreener.com/news/credit-agricole-s-a-results-for-the-4th-quarter-and-full-year-2025-ce7e5bd3de80f52c)).
- **★ RoTE:** 13.5% Group FY2025 — *higher than BNP's 11.6%* and a key relative-value point.
- **★ CET1:** Group ~17.4% (fortress); CASA phased-in ~11.8% (ACT 2028 ~11% target) — strong capacity to pay the dividend.
- **Dividend:** €1.13 for 2025 (+3%); 50% cash payout policy + interim dividends from 2026 — the income core of the thesis.

## 4. Capital allocation & strategy (ACT 2028)

- **ACT 2028 medium-term plan:** CASA **net income €8.5bn**, revenue CAGR +3.5%, **C/I <55%**, ~11% CET1, **50% cash dividend payout** with **interim dividends from 2026** ([MarketScreener FY25](https://www.marketscreener.com/news/credit-agricole-s-a-results-for-the-4th-quarter-and-full-year-2025-ce7e5bd3de80f52c)).
- **AI/efficiency push:** from 2026, an employee AI assistant, a Group data marketplace, and a Finance-function simplification aiming to halve time-to-market.
- Capital return skews to **dividend** (high yield) more than buybacks vs BNP.

## 5. Management & governance

- Mutualist structure: the regional banks' controlling stake means strategy is stable and conservative; minority CASA holders sit beneath the mutual. CEO and management are long-tenured (verify current CEO title before quoting).
- Governance feature: the mutual ownership caps takeover/M&A optionality but underpins the fortress capital and steady dividend.

## 6. Legal / regulatory — same as BNP, plus structure

- ECB SSM supervision, Basel/SREP buffers gate capital return.
- **French sovereign/political risk** (OAT-Bund spread) — shared with BNP.
- Insurance regulation (Solvency II) matters more here given CA Assurances' weight.

## 7. ★ How the stock trades on news — the sensitivity map

**Ranked return drivers:**
1. **★ ECB rate path / NII** — positive into the 2026 hiking regime (huge regional-bank NII growth).
2. **★ Equity-market level / volatility** — *more* important for CASA than BNP because of Amundi + insurance + CIB; a market drawdown directly dents CASA earnings (the Q1-26 lesson).
3. **Cost of risk / credit cycle** — recession is the downside (same as BNP).
4. **Amundi net flows** — the fee-engine tell.
5. **Dividend / capital return** — the income thesis; payout surprises.
6. **French sovereign spread**, sector beta (SX7E).

**Reflex:** move ≈ (NII surprise) + (market-beta on fee/insurance income) + (cost-of-risk surprise) + (dividend). **Key difference vs BNP: weight the market-level/fee sensitivity higher.** Q1-26 proved it — Group up on NII, but the *listed stock's* earnings fell on market turbulence.

## 8. What's priced in & positioning

- **Valuation:** ~7× earnings, ~6.9% yield, PT ~€20.6 implies ~26% upside but consensus is only **Hold** — cheap and high-yielding, but the market wants a catalyst (ACT 2028 delivery / sustained NII) before re-rating.
- **Positioning:** a value/income holding; less of a "clean rate play" than BNP because of the fee/insurance beta. Pairs naturally with BNP for a relative-value bank trade.

## 9. Catalysts & narrative-shift radar

- **Quarterly results** (FY2025 4 Feb 2026; Q1-26 30 Apr 2026), **ECB meetings**, **Amundi flow data**, **AGM/dividend**, **French politics/budget**.
- **Bull confirmed if:** NII keeps compounding, markets recover (lifting fee/insurance income), cost of risk stays low, ACT 2028 on track → re-rate toward the €20+ PT.
- **Bear confirmed if:** market drawdown persists (hits fees/insurance), recession lifts cost of risk, ECB reverses to cuts, French sovereign stress.
- **The tell:** watch the **split between Group and CASA** — if the listed stock's fee/insurance lines keep lagging the Group's retail NII, the value gap won't close.

**The headline:** A premium, **networks-led global utility** — the regulated grid business is now the engine (EBITDA +21%, rate base €51bn) — pivoting hard toward regulated US/UK networks (Avangrid, ScottishPower) at the exact moment **electrification + AI data-centre power demand** is lifting grid investment. Same rate-sensitivity as Enel, but a stronger balance sheet, more visible regulated growth, and a bigger capex runway (a **~€55bn grid plan to 2031**).

## 1. The thesis & the narrative

**One line:** The world's leading private grid/renewables operator — converting a massive, multi-year regulated **networks capex** programme into steady double-digit earnings and dividend growth, underpinned by electrification.

- **Bull story:** networks (regulated, stable, ~9.5% target returns) are growing fastest (+21% EBITDA, rate base +12% to €51bn); guidance *raised* to >8% growth at Q1-26; **~€55bn UK+US grid plan 2026–31**; ~€20bn dividends 2025–28 at a 65–75% payout. Electrification/AI power demand is a structural multi-decade tailwind ([Investing.com Q1-26](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/iberdrola-q1-2026-slides-profit-jumps-11-guidance-raised-to-8-93CH-4644123), [reNews](https://renews.biz/110243/iberdrola-profits-increase-on-network-growth/)).
- **Bear story:** **rate sensitivity** (long-duration regulated cashflows + a heavy, debt-funded capex plan — higher ECB/UK/US rates pressure valuation and financing cost); regulatory risk on allowed returns (UK Ofgem, US state PUCs, Spain); execution risk on a huge capex programme; FX (USD/GBP).
- **What the market pays for:** **regulated-asset-base growth + the dividend, discounted at the bond yield** — quality and growth visibility justify a premium to Enel.

---

## 2. The business — networks-first

- **★ Networks (the core):** regulated electricity grids in **Spain, the UK (ScottishPower, since 2007), the US (Avangrid), and Brazil (Neoenergia).** Regulated, rate-base-driven, the highest-quality earnings — now the priority for capital ([Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/iberdrola-acquire-avangrid-transmission-grid-renewables-offshore-wind/716712/)).
- **Renewables:** large wind (onshore + offshore) and solar fleet — growth + some merchant power-price exposure.
- **Supply/retail** across its markets.
- **★ Avangrid (US):** Iberdrola **delisted Avangrid in 2024** and bought the remaining minority (18.4% at $35.75/share, ~$2.6bn) to take it to **100%** — deliberately concentrating on **regulated US networks** in a strong-credit market; Avangrid then raised **$4bn of new equity** to fund grid capex ([Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/iberdrola-acquire-avangrid-transmission-grid-renewables-offshore-wind/716712/), [Iberdrola](https://www.iberdrola.com/press-room/news/detail/avangrid-iberdrola-s-controlled-subsidiary-u-s--issued-capital-increase-4-000-million)).

## 3. Financials — the lines that matter

- **★ Adjusted net profit:** €6,285m FY2025 (+12%); 2026 **>€6.6bn**, 2028 **>€7.6bn** ([reNews](https://renews.biz/110243/iberdrola-profits-increase-on-network-growth/), [Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/iberdrola-q1-2026-slides-profit-jumps-11-guidance-raised-to-8-93CH-4644123)).
- **★ Adjusted EBITDA & the networks split:** €15,684m, with **networks +21%** — the market rewards the regulated mix-shift.
- **★ Regulated asset base (RAB):** €51bn (+12%) — *the* growth metric for a networks utility; RAB growth ≈ future earnings growth.
- **★ Capex:** €14.46bn FY2025 (60% US+UK, 62% networks); ~€55bn UK+US grid plan to 2031 at ~9.5% returns — funded by debt + retained earnings + some equity (Avangrid raise).
- **★ Dividend:** €0.68 FY2025, floor €0.64, 65–75% payout, ~€20bn 2025–28; **Flexible Dividend (scrip)** lets holders take shares or cash.

## 4. Capital allocation & strategy (Strategic Plan 2025–2028)

- Capital is being steered decisively toward **regulated networks in AAA/strong-credit jurisdictions (US, UK)** and away from merchant exposure — a deliberate de-risking and quality-up ([Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/iberdrola-acquire-avangrid-transmission-grid-renewables-offshore-wind/716712/)).
- Funding the capex without breaking the balance sheet is the central execution question (hence the Avangrid equity raise).
- Growing dividend with a scrip option.

## 6. Legal / regulatory — the value driver

- **Regulated allowed returns** set by **Ofgem (UK), US state PUCs/FERC, and Spain's CNMC** — these reviews are the biggest structural catalysts (the ~9.5% UK return target is a key assumption).
- Spanish political/regulatory risk; US state-level rate cases; Brazil (Neoenergia) regulatory/FX.
- Energy-transition and grid-connection policy (electrification tailwind is policy-supported).

---

## 7. ★ How the stock trades on news — the sensitivity map

**Ranked return drivers:**
1. **★ Bond yields / rate path (inverse):** long-duration regulated cashflows + debt-funded capex → higher rates de-rate it (same mechanism as Enel; the June-2026 ECB hike is a headwind, a dovish pivot the catalyst). **Dominant valuation driver.**
2. **★ Regulated-return decisions** (Ofgem/PUC/CNMC) — direct hit to the RAB earnings.
3. **Rate-base / capex delivery** — RAB growth = earnings growth; capex execution.
4. **Dividend** — income thesis (scrip dilution nuance).
5. **FX (USD, GBP)** — large US/UK earnings translated to EUR.
6. **Power prices / renewables output** (smaller now given the networks tilt).

**Reflex:** move ≈ (rate/yield shift × duration, *inverse*) + (regulatory outcome) + (RAB/guidance surprise) + (FX). **vs Enel:** Iberdrola is higher-quality (more regulated, stronger balance sheet, US/UK-tilted) so it carries a premium multiple and is somewhat *less* punished on idiosyncratic/LatAm risk — but *more* exposed to UK/US regulatory and rate moves.

## 8. What's priced in & positioning

- **Valuation:** ~16× forward earnings, ~10.8× EV/EBITDA — a premium to the sector and to Enel, reflecting the networks quality and growth visibility. Analyst target ~€19.3 (Hold) sits *below* the ~€21.75 price — i.e. the market has already paid up for the quality; consensus sees limited near-term upside ([eToro](https://www.etoro.com/markets/ibe.mc)).
- **Positioning:** a core quality-utility / "electrification + grids" holding. The risk is that it's priced for perfection on a rate-sensitive asset in a hiking regime.

## 9. Catalysts & narrative-shift radar

- **Quarterly results** (FY2025 25 Feb 2026; Q1-26 reported, guide raised to >8%), **Strategic-Plan / CMD updates**, **regulatory reviews (Ofgem RIIO, US rate cases, CNMC)**, **dividend/scrip dates**, **ECB & bond-yield moves.**
- **Bull confirmed if:** rates ease (dovish ECB on falling oil) → utilities re-rate; favourable regulatory rulings; RAB + AI/electrification demand drive the >8% growth → 2028 >€7.6bn delivered.
- **Bear confirmed if:** rates keep rising; adverse regulatory return cuts; capex/FX execution stumbles; premium multiple compresses.
- **The tell:** **bond yields + the next big regulatory return decision** — those set the multiple and the earnings base more than any quarter.

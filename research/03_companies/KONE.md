**The headline:** A high-quality, cash-generative elevator company whose stock is dominated by **one swing factor: China**. New-equipment demand in China (its biggest E&E market) has been falling with the property downturn — but the **maintenance & modernisation** annuity (a growing installed base needing service) is the resilient, high-margin offset. The bull/bear debate is entirely **"is the China new-build drag bottoming, and does the services annuity dominate?"**

---

## 1. The thesis & the narrative

**One line:** A defensive-growth industrial with a powerful recurring-service model — as new-equipment volumes (especially China) shrink, the ever-growing maintenance base provides resilient, high-margin, compounding cashflow.

- **Bull story:** **maintenance & modernisation** is recurring, high-margin, and grows with the global installed base; China new-equipment is *already* deeply depressed (so the drag is well-flagged and shrinking as a share of the book); margins guided to recover (12.3–13.0%); strong cash conversion + dividend; APAC/MEA/India growth offsets China.
- **Bear story:** **China property** — new-building demand "expected to decline clearly in 2026" ([KONE](https://www.kone.com/en/news-and-insights/releases/financial-statement-bulletin-of-kone-corporation-for-january-december-2025-2026-02-06.aspx)); pricing pressure in China new-equipment hits margins; overall a low-growth, mature end-market; FX.
- **What the market pays for:** **order trend (esp. China) + the margin recovery + the resilience/growth of the service annuity.**

---

## 2. The business — two engines

- **★ New Building Solutions (new equipment):** sell-and-install elevators/escalators — **cyclical, China-and-construction-exposed.** This is the volatile, headline-driving part.
- **★ Services (Maintenance + Modernisation):** servicing the installed base + upgrading old units — **recurring, high-margin, sticky, growing.** The quality core of the investment case; the larger the maintenance base, the more it dominates earnings over time.
- **★ China dependence:** Greater China's share of the **order book fell from ~40% (Q1 2024) to ~30% (Q1 2025)** as the property downturn hit new-build ([Inderes](https://www.inderes.fi/en/research/kone-q324-the-ball-and-chain-from-china-is-getting-lighter)) — note the framing: *the China weight is shrinking, which de-risks the mix over time.*
- **Ownership:** the **Herlin family** controls via A-shares — stable, long-term, family-controlled (limited takeover optionality, conservative strategy).

---

## 3. Income statement — the lines that matter

- **★ Orders received** (esp. the **China vs ex-China split**) — the leading indicator. FY2025 €9.09bn (+6.8% comparable FX) — *growing despite China*, thanks to ex-China + services.
- **★ Adjusted EBIT margin:** 2026 guide **12.3–13.0%** — margin recovery (from China price pressure normalising + services mix) is the key earnings driver.
- **Sales:** €11.25bn (+1.3%) — low growth, as expected for a mature E&E market.
- **★ Services growth & maintenance base** — the resilient annuity; the metric that defines the quality premium.
- **Cash conversion / dividend** — €1.80/share; a steady-compounder income component.

## 4. Capital allocation & strategy

- Asset-light, cash-generative; returns cash via a **steady dividend**.
- Strategy centres on **growing the maintenance base** (recurring revenue) and digital/connected services, plus geographic mix-shift toward India/APAC/MEA as China normalises.

## 7. ★ How the stock trades on news — the sensitivity map

> A **China-property-sensitive quality compounder.** The reflex:

**Ranked return drivers:**
1. **★ China construction / new-building demand** — the single biggest swing; China-property headlines and KONE's China order trend move the stock most. *This is the cleanest China-property proxy in the book — see the China macro brief.*
2. **★ Order intake (China vs ex-China split)** vs consensus.
3. **★ Adjusted EBIT margin** vs the 12.3–13.0% guide — the margin-recovery story.
4. **Services/maintenance growth** — the resilience tell.
5. **Macro / construction cycle** (Europe, US, India).
6. **FX** (EUR reporter, global sales).

**Reflex:** move ≈ (China order/demand surprise) + (margin vs guide) + (services resilience) ± (macro). Because the China weakness is well-known, **the stock often moves on the *second derivative* — signs China is bottoming/stabilising can re-rate it even with absolute weakness.**

---

## 8. What's priced in & positioning

- **Valuation:** a quality premium for the services annuity; consensus PT (~€62) sits *above* the ~€50.6 price → analysts see upside, implying the market is currently discounting the China drag heavily.
- **Positioning:** owned as a defensive-quality compounder and (inversely) as a **China-property recovery option** — improving Chinese stimulus/property data is a positive catalyst. Pairs with the China macro brief.

---

## 9. Catalysts & narrative-shift radar

- **Quarterly results** (orders by region, margin), **China property/stimulus data**, **dividend/AGM**, **construction-market indicators**.
- **Bull confirmed if:** China new-build *stabilises/bottoms*, margins recover into the guide, services keep growing → re-rate toward the PT.
- **Bear confirmed if:** China property deteriorates further, new-equipment pricing pressure persists, margins miss.
- **The tell:** the **China order trend's second derivative** and the **margin line** — services resilience is the floor; China is the swing.

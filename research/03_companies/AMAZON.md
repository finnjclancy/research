**The headline:** A beat on every line — **AWS reaccelerated to +28% (fastest in 15 quarters)** on AI demand, retail margins hit records, total operating income +30% — yet the stock is **~18% off its May high**. The tension is the defining one for mega-cap AI: **~$200bn of capex on AI data centres is compressing free cash flow now** (TTM FCF just ~$1.2bn), and the market is debating whether the AWS/AI payoff justifies the spend.

---

## 1. The thesis & the narrative

**One line:** Three powerful engines — **AWS** (cloud + AI, the profit/growth driver), **Advertising** (high-margin, fast-growing), and **Retail/commerce** (scaling margins) — with the bull/bear now centred on whether the enormous AI capex converts into durable AWS growth and returns.

- **Bull story:** **AWS reaccelerating to +28%** with a ~$150bn run-rate as enterprises spend on AI; record retail margins (logistics/efficiency + AI); advertising compounding at high margin; **custom silicon (Trainium/Inferentia)** lowers AI cost and differentiates AWS; operating income +30%. The capex is "investing into demand."
- **Bear story:** **$200bn capex** crushes near-term FCF (~$1.2bn TTM) and lifts depreciation — an **AI-capex digestion / ROI** risk; if AWS growth doesn't hold, the spend looks value-destructive (the ~18% drawdown reflects this fear); cloud competition (Azure, Google Cloud); consumer/retail cyclicality; regulatory/antitrust.
- **What the market pays for:** **the AWS growth rate and the capex→FCF/ROI bridge.** AWS growth is the single most important number; capex/FCF is the swing risk.

---

## 2. The business — three engines

1. **★ AWS (cloud + AI — the value engine):** $37.6bn rev (+28%), $14.2bn op income — the **majority of group operating profit** and the AI-leverage point. Reacceleration is the bull case; it's also where the $200bn capex goes (servers, data centres, custom chips). ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/aws-earnings-q1-2026.html)).
2. **★ Advertising (high-margin growth):** sponsored products/search + Prime Video ads — fast-growing, very high incremental margin; an increasingly important profit driver (a key "hidden" engine).
3. **★ Retail/commerce (Online stores + Third-party + Subscriptions/Prime):** the volume base; the story here is **margin expansion** (fulfilment regionalisation, automation/robotics, AI-driven efficiency) — record retail margins in Q1-26.
- **★ Custom silicon:** Trainium/Inferentia AI chips inside AWS — "the chip business inside AWS" — lowers reliance on Nvidia and improves AI economics ([TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/amazon-beat-q1-2026-earnings-on-every-line-the-bigger-story-is-the-chip-business-inside-aws)).

## 3. Income statement — the lines that matter

- **★ AWS revenue growth & operating margin** — *the* number; +28% reaccel is the headline. AWS margin drives group profit.
- **★ Total operating income & margin:** $23.9bn, 13.1% (+30%) — record group profitability.
- **★ Capex & free cash flow:** **$43.2bn Q1 / ~$200bn FY**; TTM FCF compressed to ~$1.2bn — **the controversial line.** The capex/FCF bridge is the bear's focus.
- **Advertising revenue growth** — high-margin contributor (watch the disclosed run-rate).
- **Retail/North America & International operating margin** — the efficiency story.
- **Depreciation trajectory** — the lagged P&L cost of the capex wave.

---

## 4. Capital allocation

- **Dominated by AI capex (~$200bn)** — Amazon is reinvesting almost all cash into AWS/AI infrastructure (data centres, chips). No dividend; buybacks minimal — this is a **reinvestment** story, not a capital-return one.
- The capital-allocation debate *is* the stock debate: is $200bn/yr into AI the best use of cash, and what's the ROI?

---

## 5. Management & governance

- **CEO Andy Jassy** (ex-AWS chief) — the architect of AWS; his credibility on the AI-capex bet and AWS reacceleration is central. *Verify current title before quoting.*
- Management frames the capex as demand-driven and disciplined; the market is testing that with the FCF compression.

## 6. Legal / regulatory

## 7. ★ How the stock trades on news — the sensitivity map

> A **mega-cap AI/cloud + consumer** stock: the AWS growth rate and capex/FCF dominate.

**Ranked return drivers:**
1. **★ AWS revenue growth vs consensus** — the single biggest mover; the +28% reaccel is the bull anchor; any deceleration is the top risk.
2. **★ Capex guidance & FCF** — higher capex with unclear ROI pressures the stock (the source of the ~18% drawdown despite a beat); the capex→FCF bridge.
3. **Operating income / retail margin** vs expectations.
4. **Advertising growth** — high-margin upside.
5. **AI-capex sentiment across mega-caps** (the whole "AI ROI/digestion" debate; read-through to/from Microsoft, Google, Nvidia, AMD).
6. **Consumer health** (retail volumes, Prime); rates/risk appetite (long-duration mega-cap).

**Reflex:** move ≈ (AWS growth surprise) + (capex/FCF reaction) + (margin surprise) ± (AI-sentiment). Note the key lesson from Q1-26: **a beat on every line still sold off** because the market focused on capex/FCF — *the reaction depends on which line the narrative is fixated on, not just the beat.*

---

## 8. What's priced in & positioning

- **Valuation:** ~28× TTM earnings for a re-accelerating AWS + advertising + margin story — not extreme for the growth, but the **FCF compression makes valuation optically harder** and sentiment fragile (hence the drawdown).
- **Positioning:** a core mega-cap AI/cloud long; crowded. The asymmetric risk is an **AWS deceleration or an AI-capex ROI scare** — the same digestion fear that would hit ASML/AMD.

---

## 9. Catalysts & narrative-shift radar

- **Quarterly earnings** (AWS growth, capex, margins), **capex-guidance updates**, **AI/Trainium announcements**, **mega-cap peer cloud results (Azure/Google)**, **Prime Day/consumer data**, **Nvidia/AMD read-through**.
- **Bull confirmed if:** AWS growth holds/accelerates, advertising compounds, retail margins keep expanding, and the capex starts visibly converting to AWS revenue/FCF → re-rate.
- **Bear confirmed if:** AWS decelerates, capex keeps rising without FCF payoff (AI-digestion narrative takes hold), or consumer weakens.
- **The tell:** **AWS growth + the capex/FCF trajectory together** — reacceleration justifies the spend; deceleration turns $200bn of capex into the bear case overnight. This is the bellwether for the whole AI-capex book (ASML, AMD, utilities).

## 1. The thesis & the narrative

**One line:** A premiumising global brewer pivoting toward a broader "beverages" model via Britvic, with disproportionate exposure to Asian beer growth (notably China) — a defensive staples compounder with a self-help (synergy) kicker.

- **Bull story:** Britvic synergies **ahead of plan** (driving the 2025 profit beat); premiumisation + non-alc diversification; **India & Central Asia as top beer-growth markets**; margin expansion (+50bps to 15.7%); resilient dividend; expectation of a **return to growth in China in 2026**.
- **Bear story:** **China consumer fragility** (sentiment weak; organic *volumes* fell ~3% in a recent quarter); mature/declining developed-market beer; **input costs** (barley, aluminium, energy — oil-linked); FX (EM currencies); **GLP-1 read-through risk** to alcohol consumption; integration/execution risk on Britvic.
- **What the market pays for:** **organic operating-profit growth (esp. the China trajectory) + Britvic synergy delivery + premium mix.**

---

## 2. The business — beer + (now) soft drinks

- **Beer regions:** **Asia** (the growth + China engine — Carlsberg has a major **Western China** beer business via Chongqing Brewery; also Laos, India, Vietnam), **Western Europe** (mature, premiumisation), **Central & Eastern Europe / India / rest** (post-Russia exit — Carlsberg sold its Russian business Baltika).
- **★ Britvic (the transformation):** acquired UK soft-drinks maker Britvic (Robinsons, J2O, Tango, PepsiCo UK bottling) — diversifies into **non-alcoholic** and lifts scale; **£110m cost synergies**, ~30% delivered in 2025 (ahead of plan), 30–40% more in 2026 ([Carlsberg FY2025](https://www.carlsberggroup.com/newsroom/fy-2025-financial-statement/), [Bitget](https://www.bitget.com/amp/news/detail/12560605270381)).
- **★ Ownership:** the **Carlsberg Foundation** controls via A-shares — a stable, mission-driven anchor (limits takeover optionality; CARLB = the liquid B-share you trade).

---

## 3. Income statement — the lines that matter

- **★ Organic operating-profit growth (MPM)** — the headline KPI; FY2025 +22.7% (Britvic + mix + cost); 2026 guided **+2–6%**.
- **★ Organic revenue per hectolitre (rev/hl)** — the **premiumisation/pricing** gauge; +1.4% FY2025.
- **★ Volumes (organic)** — the demand gauge; recent quarters showed organic volume *declines* (~−3%) even as profit grew — *the price/mix-vs-volume tension, same as Unilever.*
- **Operating margin (MPM):** 15.7% (+50bps).
- **Reported vs organic revenue:** +18.8% reported is Britvic-inflated; **organic** is the true engine — always separate them.
- **Britvic synergy delivery** — a self-help earnings driver independent of the macro.

## 7. ★ How the stock trades on news — the sensitivity map

> A **defensive brewer with an EM/China growth tilt + a synergy self-help story.**

**Ranked return drivers:**
1. **★ China beer demand / volumes** — the single biggest swing; Chinese consumer-sentiment and stimulus headlines move it (key China read-through name). Western-China weather/economy matters.
2. **★ Organic operating-profit growth vs guide** + **Britvic synergy delivery**.
3. **★ Volume vs price/mix (rev/hl)** — premiumisation holding up vs volume declines.
4. **Input costs** (barley, aluminium, energy — oil-linked; links to the Iran/oil macro note).
5. **EM FX** (Asian currencies, GBP post-Britvic).
6. **GLP-1 / health-trend** structural watch; **bond yields** (defensive bond-proxy multiple).

**Reflex:** move ≈ (China-demand/volume surprise) + (organic profit vs guide + synergy news) ± (input costs / FX). Like Unilever, **lower beta** — but the **China factor gives it more idiosyncratic swing** than a pure developed-market staple.

## 8. What's priced in & positioning

- **Valuation:** ~15× earnings — *cheaper* than Unilever's ~23×, reflecting the China overhang and beer's maturity; the Britvic/non-alc pivot is the re-rating lever if it delivers.
- **Positioning:** a defensive staple owned partly as a **China-consumer recovery option** — improving Chinese stimulus/sentiment is an upside catalyst (pairs with the China macro brief and Kone).

---

## 9. Catalysts & narrative-shift radar

- **Quarterly trading statements** (China volumes, rev/hl, synergy progress), **China stimulus/consumer data**, **dividend/AGM**, **input-cost moves**.
- **Bull confirmed if:** China returns to growth in 2026, Britvic synergies keep beating, premiumisation lifts rev/hl, margins expand → re-rate toward peers.
- **Bear confirmed if:** China consumer stays weak / volumes keep falling, input costs spike, GLP-1 pressures alcohol, Britvic integration disappoints.
- **The tell:** the **China beer-volume trajectory** plus **Britvic synergy delivery** — the macro swing and the self-help, respectively.

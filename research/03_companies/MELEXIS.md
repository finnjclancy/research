**The headline:** A high-quality, high-margin **automotive-sensor specialist** in a cyclical downturn — FY2025 sales fell ~10% on the auto-semiconductor inventory correction and weak global car production, but management guides to a **second-half-2026 recovery**. The long-term story is **rising semiconductor content per car** (EVs, ADAS, comfort/safety electronics); the short-term reality is the auto cycle + China competition. Pays out most earnings as a fat dividend.

## 1. The thesis & the narrative

**One line:** A focused, fabless leader in magnetic/Hall-effect position and current sensors for cars — structurally growing content per vehicle, but cyclically tied to global auto production and the chip inventory cycle.

- **Bull story:** **content-per-car growth** (more sensors in EVs, ADAS, electrification) outpaces unit car growth; high gross margins (~40%+) and operating margins (~17%); through-cycle leader with pricing/design-win stickiness; **H2-2026 recovery** guided; generous dividend.
- **Bear story:** **auto-cyclicality + inventory correction** (FY2025 −10%); heavy reliance on auto (88%) — a single end-market; **Chinese domestic sensor competition** and China-auto pricing pressure; FX (USD billing); a downturn in car production hits hard.
- **What the market pays for:** **the auto-cycle inflection (sales trough → recovery) + content-growth + margins** — it's an early-cyclical auto-semis call.

---

## 2. The business — what it actually does

- **Fabless designer of mixed-signal/analog ICs and sensors** for cars: magnetic position sensors (Hall-effect), current sensors, latch/switch ICs, temperature sensors, motor drivers, embedded lighting — the silicon behind power steering, braking, EV powertrain, comfort and safety systems.
- **★ 88% automotive** — exceptional end-market concentration: a focused leader, but undiversified.
- **Content-per-car thesis:** each new car generation (esp. EV/ADAS) carries *more* Melexis chips — structural unit-content growth independent of car volumes.
- **Ownership:** founder-family controlled (Roland Duchâtelet / Françoise Chombar legacy) → stable, long-term, conservative; outsources fabrication (fabless), so capex-light and high-FCF.

## 3. Income statement — the lines that matter

- **★ Sales trend & the cycle:** €839.6m FY2025 (−10%); **the half-on-half trajectory is the key tell** — guided flat H1, growth H2 (a cyclical bottoming signal).
- **★ Gross margin (~40%) & operating margin (~17%)** — the quality/pricing-power gauges; margin resilience through the downturn is the bull point.
- **Content-per-car / design wins** — the structural growth driver (qualitative but central).
- **Dividend (€3.70):** Melexis pays out most of its earnings — a high-yield, capital-light model; dividend sustainability tracks the cycle.
- **FX (USD):** billing/translation exposure.

## 7. ★ How the stock trades on news — the sensitivity map

> An **early-cyclical auto-semi**: trades on the auto cycle, the inventory correction, and content growth.

**Ranked return drivers:**
1. **★ Sales trajectory vs the cycle** (the H1→H2 inflection; bookings/lead-time commentary) — the biggest swing; a confirmed recovery re-rates it.
2. **★ Global auto production / SAAR** and **inventory-correction signals** (read-through from auto OEMs and peers — NXP, Infineon, STMicro).
3. **★ Margins** vs the ~40% GM / ~17% OM guide.
4. **China auto / EV demand & competition** — volume and pricing.
5. **Content-per-car / design-win news** — structural narrative.
6. **FX (USD)**; broad semis sentiment (but *not* AI-capus-driven like ASML/AMD).

**Reflex:** move ≈ (sales/cycle-inflection surprise) + (margin vs guide) ± (auto-production & China). Crucially, **Melexis is an auto-cycle stock, NOT an AI stock** — don't read AI-capex newsflow into it; read auto production, inventories and China EV.

## 9. Catalysts & narrative-shift radar

- **Quarterly results** (sales trajectory, margins, lead-times), **auto-OEM production data & peer prints (NXP/Infineon/STMicro)**, **China EV demand**, **dividend**.

**The headline:** A high-quality, late-cycle industrial firing on all cylinders — **record orders, 19% margins, 25% ROCE** — riding the **electrification + automation + data-centre power** super-theme. Management has lifted targets, launched a $2bn buyback, and is **selling Robotics to SoftBank for $5.375bn** to focus the portfolio. ## 1. The thesis & the narrative

**One line:** A focused electrification-and-automation champion whose products (switchgear, drives, motors, power equipment) are exactly what data centres, grids and re-industrialisation need — converting a structural capex wave into record orders and best-in-class margins.

- **Bull story:** secular demand from **data centres (power-hungry AI), grid build-out, electrification and automation**; record $36.8bn orders, book-to-bill >1; margins at 19% and rising targets; disciplined portfolio (Robotics sale); strong FCF + buyback + dividend.
- **Bear story:** **late-cycle industrial cyclicality** — orders can roll over fast if capex slows or a recession hits (eurozone already contracted in Q1-26); valuation full (above target price); FX (strong CHF reporting vs global sales); short-cycle exposure.
- **What the market pays for:** **order growth + margin trajectory** into the electrification theme — and increasingly, AI/data-centre power demand as a distinct driver.

---

## 2. The business — what it actually does

- **Electrification:** low/medium-voltage products, switchgear, EV charging, power distribution — **the data-centre & grid play** (biggest, fastest-growing).
- **Motion:** electric motors, drives — energy-efficiency & industrial automation.
- **Process Automation:** control systems for process industries (energy, marine, mining).
- **Robotics & Discrete Automation:** **being divested to SoftBank ($5.375bn EV)** — a strategic simplification toward the higher-return electrification/automation core ([TipRanks](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/abb-delivers-record-2025-results-lifts-targets-and-unveils-2-billion-buyback)).
- Global, but a **strong-CHF reporter** earning largely in USD/EUR — FX translation matters.

## 3. Income statement — the lines that matter

- **★ Orders & book-to-bill** — the leading indicator for an industrial. $36.8bn record orders, **book-to-bill 1.14** (orders > revenue = growth ahead). *Order growth/decline is the #1 swing factor.*
- **★ Operational EBITA margin:** 19.0% — best-in-class; the margin trajectory (and management's raised targets) is the quality/re-rating driver.
- **Revenue growth:** +9% FY2025.
- **ROCE 25.3%, FCF $4.6bn** — capital efficiency and cash generation underpin the buyback/dividend.
- **Segment mix:** Electrification's growth & margin is the part the market most rewards.

## 7. ★ How the stock trades on news — the sensitivity map

> A **late-cycle quality industrial**: trades on orders, margins and the capex cycle.

**Ranked return drivers:**
1. **★ Order intake & book-to-bill vs consensus** — the leading indicator; a beat/miss on orders moves it most.
2. **★ Operational EBITA margin** vs expectations + target changes.
3. **★ Data-centre / electrification demand signals** — the structural narrative (read-through from hyperscaler capex, grid spend, AI-power stories — links to AMD/utilities).
4. **Macro/PMI & recession risk** — short-cycle orders are economically sensitive; a downturn hits orders fast.
5. **FX (CHF strength)** — translation drag.
6. **Portfolio actions** (Robotics close, buyback execution, M&A).

**Reflex:** move ≈ (order/margin surprise vs consensus) + (electrification-demand narrative) ± (macro/cycle) + (FX). Because it's priced for quality (above target price), **in-line orders can disappoint; it needs continued order acceleration to hold the premium.**

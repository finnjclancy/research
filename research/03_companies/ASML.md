## 1. The thesis & the narrative

**One line:** ASML is the single most strategically important company in the global semiconductor supply chain — it holds a **100% monopoly on EUV (extreme ultraviolet) lithography**, the only technology that can pattern the most advanced logic and memory chips. No AI chip at the leading edge gets made without an ASML machine.

- **The bull story (current, dominant):** AI is a structural, multi-year capex supercycle. Leading-edge logic (TSMC, Intel, Samsung) and HBM/DRAM (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron) all need more EUV and now **High-NA EUV**. Chip demand is "outpacing supply" and customers are *accelerating* 2026+ capacity plans ([CEO Fouquet, Q1-26](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q1-2026-financial-results)). This is what drove the Q1-26 guidance *raise* to €36–40bn and the run to all-time highs.
- **The bear story:** (1) **Concentration & cyclicality** — a handful of customers, lumpy multi-hundred-million-euro orders, so bookings whip around violently. (2) **China cliff** — China was an abnormally large share of sales in 2024–25 (pull-forward ahead of export controls) and is guided to fall sharply in 2026 ([Trendforce](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/10/15/news-asml-sees-sharp-2026-china-sales-drop-but-says-total-revenue-wont-fall-below-2025/)). (3) **Geopolitics/tariffs** — US export controls cap the China TAM; Trump tariff threats on EU semicap directly threaten gross margin ([Bloomberg, Jul 2025](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-16/asml-orders-beat-expectations-after-ai-investment-fuels-demand)). (4) **Valuation** — ~61× trailing earnings prices in a lot of the AI capex continuing.

**What the market is paying for:** not this year's EPS — it's paying for the *durability and slope* of EUV/High-NA adoption into 2030. That's why **bookings and forward commentary move the stock far more than the reported quarter.**

## 2. The business — what it actually does

- **Lithography monopoly.** ASML is the **sole supplier of EUV** scanners and the dominant supplier of DUV (deep ultraviolet). Lithography is the step that defines the smallest features on a chip; EUV (13.5nm wavelength) is required at the leading edge. This is the deepest moat in semis.
- **Revenue mix (two engines):**
  1. **System sales** — selling the machines (EUV, High-NA EUV, DUV). Lumpy, order-driven. In Q1-26 ASML sold **67 new lithography systems** ([Q1-26 PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q1-2026-financial-results)).
  2. **Installed Base Management** — services, upgrades, spare parts on the ~6,000+ installed machines. Recurring, higher-quality revenue: **€2.49bn in Q1-26** ([Q1-26 PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q1-2026-financial-results)).
- **Product ladder (know this — it's the growth driver):**
  - **DUV** — mature, cash-generative, big in China.
  - **Low-NA EUV** (NXE series) — the current leading-edge workhorse.
  - **High-NA EUV** (EXE:5000 R&D, **EXE:5200** production) — the next leg. ~$400m per tool, 175 wafers/hour, ~60% productivity boost ([Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/semiconductors/intel-installs-industrys-first-commercial-high-na-euv-lithography-tool-asml-twinscan-exe-5200b-sets-the-stage-for-14a)).
- **Customer concentration:** essentially **TSMC, Intel, Samsung** (logic/foundry) and **SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron** (memory). A small number of customers = lumpy orders = volatile bookings. This is structural and the root of the stock's volatility.

## 3. Income statement — the lines that matter

> For ASML, the *order book* matters more than the P&L. But know these:

- **Top line:** FY2025 €32.7bn; guided **€36–40bn for 2026** (a growth year "largely driven by a significant increase in EUV sales") ([Q4-25 PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q4-2025-financial-results), raised at [Q1-26](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q1-2026-financial-results)).
- **Gross margin:** 52.8% FY2025; guided 51–53% for 2026; long-term target **56–60% by 2030** ([Investor Day 2024](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2024/asml-investor-day-2024)). GM mix improves as EUV/High-NA and Installed Base scale; tariffs are the key downside risk to it.
- **R&D:** ~€1.2bn/quarter — enormous, and the moat-maintenance cost ([Q1-26 PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q1-2026-financial-results)).
- **EPS:** Q1-26 basic EPS €7.15; FY2025 net income €9.6bn ([Q1-26 PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q1-2026-financial-results), [Q4-25 PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q4-2025-financial-results)).
- **★ THE KPI THE MARKET TRADES ON — NET BOOKINGS.** Bookings are the leading indicator of revenue 1–2 years out. They are **lumpy and the single biggest source of share-price volatility.**
  - FY2025 total net bookings **€28.0bn**; Q4-25 alone **€13.2bn (€7.4bn EUV)** — a huge number that fed the bull case ([Q4-25 PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q4-2025-financial-results)).
  - Contrast: the Oct-2024 quarter booked just **€2.6bn vs ~€5.4bn expected** → stock crashed 16% (see §7).

## 4. Balance sheet, cash flow & capital allocation

- **Capital return:** FY2025 dividend **€7.50/share (+17%)**; a **new €12bn buyback** running through Dec 2028 ([Q4-25 PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/q4-2025-financial-results)). Strong FCF, net cash positive — not a leverage story.
- **★ Unusual capital-allocation move — the Mistral AI stake:** On **9 Sep 2025** ASML invested **€1.3bn** into French AI champion **Mistral**, becoming its **largest shareholder (~11%)** as part of a €1.7bn round that valued Mistral around €11.7–13.7bn. **CFO Roger Dassen joined Mistral's board.** It is a *strategic* partnership — integrating Mistral's models into ASML's products, R&D and operations — and is unusual: ASML rarely invests in start-ups and had never backed an AI firm ([Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/business/2025/09/09/chip-giant-asml-invests-13bn-in-french-ai-champion-mistral), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-09/asml-pumps-1-3-billion-into-mistral-in-boost-for-european-ai), [ASML PR](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2025/asml-mistral-ai-enter-strategic-partnership)). *Watch this as both a sentiment/"European AI sovereignty" narrative hook and a governance question.*

## 6. Legal / regulatory — the geopolitics is the story

- **Export controls are a structural ceiling on the China TAM.** US-led (and Dutch-government) restrictions bar EUV and the most advanced DUV from China. China nonetheless became an outsized share of sales in 2024–25 as customers **pulled forward** mature-node DUV ahead of tightening rules.
- **2026 China decline is guided and expected:** ASML expects "China customer demand and China total net sales in 2026 to decline significantly" vs the very strong 2024–25, **but does not expect 2026 total sales below 2025** ([Trendforce](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/10/15/news-asml-sees-sharp-2026-china-sales-drop-but-says-total-revenue-wont-fall-below-2025/), [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/asml-reports-flat-sales-again-forecasts-growth-for-2026-despite-collapse-of-chinese-business/)). → *AI demand is offsetting the China cliff; the bull case rests on that offset holding.*
- **Tariffs:** Trump's threatened ~30% tariffs on EU imports incl. semicap could hit ASML's gross margin directly and delay customer capex — the explicit reason for the Jul-2025 caution ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-16/asml-orders-beat-expectations-after-ai-investment-fuels-demand)).
- **⚠ To verify:** exact China % of system sales for FY2025 (was very high, ~40%+ in some 2024 quarters; directionally falling). Confirm from the FY2025 annual report before quoting a precise figure.

## 7. ★ How the stock trades on news — the sensitivity map (the core of the mandate)

> ASML is a **high-beta, narrative-and-bookings stock.** The reported quarter rarely matters; **forward bookings and CEO 2026/2027 commentary matter enormously.** Two case studies define the elasticity:

**Case 1 — 15 Oct 2024: −16% in a day (biggest fall since 1998).** Net bookings came in at **€2.6bn vs ~€5.39bn expected**, and management cut 2025 revenue guidance to **€30–35bn** (below the ~€36bn consensus), citing a slower non-AI recovery and weak China. The *reported* quarter was fine — the **bookings miss + guide cut** did the damage ([TMTPost](https://en.tmtpost.com/post/7286625), [Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2024/11/04/why-asml-stock-lost-17-in-october/)).
**Lesson:** a bookings miss of ~50% vs expectations = double-digit one-day move. Bookings are the variable to have a prior on.

**Case 2 — 16 Jul 2025: −11%, ~$30bn wiped.** Q2 *beat* (sales €7.7bn, bookings €5.5bn/$6.4bn) **but** Fouquet said "we still prepare for growth in 2026 but **cannot confirm it** at this stage," citing tariffs/macro. A single sentence of forward caution overwhelmed a beat ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/07/16/asml-q2-2025-earnings-report.html), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/07/16/asml-cannot-confirm-growth-in-2026-wiping-out-30-billion/)).
**Lesson:** the stock trades on the **forward narrative, not the print.** A change in confidence on the *next year* is worth ~10%.

**Implication for the reflex:** before every ASML print, my prior must be (a) consensus net bookings, (b) what's priced for 2026/2027 EUV slope, (c) the China assumption, (d) tariff/GM risk. The move ≈ (bookings surprise vs consensus) + (any change in forward-year confidence) × high sensitivity.

**Ranked return drivers:**
1. **Net bookings vs consensus** (esp. EUV bookings) — highest impact, most volatile.
2. **Forward-year (n+1) growth confidence** from the CEO — narrative re-rate.
3. **AI capex newsflow** (TSMC/Intel/Samsung/hyperscaler capex; Nvidia demand) — read-through.
4. **China / export-control / tariff headlines** — TAM and margin.
5. **High-NA adoption milestones** (Intel 14A, Samsung 1.4nm, TSMC A14P) — long-term slope.
6. Memory/HBM capex cycle; broader semis sentiment (SOX index beta).

## 8. What's priced in & positioning

- **Valuation:** ~61× TTM earnings, near all-time highs — the market is pricing a *durable* AI-driven EUV upcycle and the path toward the €44–60bn 2030 ambition. Leaves little room for a bookings disappointment.
- **Sell-side:** Strong Buy, 38 buy / 2 sell, avg PT €1,584 (i.e. roughly in line with spot — the stock has run *to* targets) ([MarketBeat](https://www.marketbeat.com/stocks/NASDAQ/ASML/)).
- **Positioning read:** a crowded, consensus AI long and a core European mega-cap holding. That means **asymmetric downside to a bookings/China/tariff disappointment** (crowded long unwinds hard, as Oct-24 and Jul-25 showed), and upside requires bookings to keep beating an already-raised bar.

## 10. How to spot a narrative shift

- **Bull story dies if:** net bookings roll over for 2+ quarters (backlog coverage thins — Barclays flagged 2026 coverage at a 3-year low in Jul-25 ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2025/07/16/asml-shares-drop-11-cautious-2026-growth-outlook/))); the CEO again "cannot confirm" the forward year; AI capex digestion signs (hyperscaler capex cuts, TSMC capex guide-down); tariffs land and hit GM.
- **Bear story dies if:** EUV bookings keep printing double-digit €bn quarters; High-NA adoption broadens beyond Intel; AI capex re-accelerates; China stabilises rather than falling off a cliff. (Q4-25's €13.2bn bookings and the Q1-26 guidance raise were exactly this.)
- **The tell:** watch whether the stock *responds* to good AI news. If beats stop being rewarded, the AI-capex narrative is getting tired regardless of the numbers.

- **My prior & why:** I had it as a *rate-sensitive bond proxy* → with the ECB hawkish I expected it to be a laggard, maybe +0.5%. What I under-weighted: the market is increasingly pricing it as an **AI-power infrastructure** name, not a yield proxy (see its [narrative map](../05_narratives/IBERDROLA_narrative.md)).
- **What happened:** it outperformed the index ~150bp on a *no-financial-news* day, purely on the AI-power narrative.
- **★ The insight — where I diverged:** I anchored too hard on the rate sensitivity. The market is **decoupling Iberdrola's multiple from bond yields** and re-rating it on the data-centre power story — the same regime in which Enel (more of a pure rate/deleveraging play) did *not* move. That divergence *within* the utilities is the signal: the AI-power narrative is now strong enough to override the rate headwind for the names with explicit data-centre exposure.
- **Lesson / update:** stop treating "utilities" as one rate-beta bucket. Tag Iberdrola/ABB as **AI-power** (re-rate up on data-centre newsflow) vs Enel as **rate/deleveraging**. Adding a line to the Iberdrola §7 sensitivity map: *"on a no-news day, data-centre headlines now move it more than a 5bp yield move."*

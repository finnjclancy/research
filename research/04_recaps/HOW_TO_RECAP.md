# How to Write the Daily Recap

> The method for doing this **every day**, from "looking at the news" to a recap that actually adds value. Template: [`_TEMPLATE.md`](_TEMPLATE.md). Worked example: [`2026-06-27_example.md`](2026-06-27_example.md).

---

## Purpose (why it exists)
A daily log to **sharpen the news→range reflex** and make your thinking visible — and, when shared with peers, a knowledge-sharing note. Either way it must be **insightful, honest, and detailed**: the value is in the *thinking* (real insights, clear communication), not in logging activity or ticking a box.

## ★ The one principle that separates good from bad
**Lead with the insight, not the news.** The guide is explicit about the traps:
- ❌ "Don't just blog your day." (A list of what you did isn't insight.)
- ❌ "Don't just summarise the news story." → *"This news came out and the stock went up 2%"* has **no value**.
- ✅ Instead: **"I thought it would do X, but it surprised me by trading so high — I think the market focused on Y."**

That single move — **expectation → reality → why the gap** — is the whole game. It also happens to be *exactly* what this research system is built to produce (the [`NARRATIVE_ENGINE`](../00_system/NARRATIVE_ENGINE.md) reflex: `stock — range — reason`). **The recap is the calibration loop for that reflex.**

## What to include (the sections)
1. **Macro** — the pivotal story today + read-through to your names (with your view vs what happened).
2. **Single Stock(s)** — news in *your* names: your initial take, how it traded, **where your view diverges** (dividends, earnings, price). The core section.
3. **Classes / talks / chats** — what you attended/discussed, the takeaway, how you'll apply it; interactions with peers / other researchers.
4. **Future opportunities** — situations/stocks you're reading up on.
5. **Tools & tech** — useful tools/functions; struggles.
6. **Personal development / questions** — anything notable; questions you had.

---

## ★ The daily workflow (how to actually do it)

**1 · Morning scan (15–20 min).** Sweep the macro tape + your coverage. *What is the one pivotal story today* (rates, oil, AI capex, a print)? Which of your 16 names has news? Sources: the [dashboard](https://www.sruth.app), FT/Bloomberg/Reuters, company RNS/IR, sell-side notes, the [economic calendar].

**2 · ★ Log the prior BEFORE you see the reaction (the non-negotiable).** For each item that matters, jot — *as if you don't yet know how it closes* — **what you expect and why**: a rough range + the driver + **what's already priced in** (pull this straight from the name's [factual §7 sensitivity map](../03_companies/) and [narrative map](../05_narratives/) — that's your prior bank). One honest line beats a perfect hindsight paragraph. *If you had no prior, that's itself the finding — a gap to close.*

**3 · Watch how it trades.** Mark your prior to market: right/wrong on direction, in/out of your range, and — the gold — **what did the market actually focus on?** Did it shrug off good news (exhausted bulls)? Gap and fade? React to a second-order detail?

**4 · End of day, write it (20–30 min).** Don't dump everything — pick the **1–3 most insight-rich items** and go deep; touch the rest in a line. For each stock: *news → my prior & why → what happened & how it traded → the insight (where I diverged / what the market cared about) → the lesson.* Then add classes/chats, tools, key takeaways (honest, mistakes included), and focus for tomorrow.

**5 · Read your group's recaps.** Build on others; note where you can help or where they spotted something you missed.

**6 · Weekly.** Skim the week for recurring error patterns → update the relevant company one-pagers/sensitivity maps so next week's priors are sharper. End the week with a "key takeaways this week / focus for next week" block (the examples do this well).

## How it plugs into the rest of this workspace
- **Your prior bank** = the [company factual files](../03_companies/) (§7 sensitivity maps) + [narrative maps](../05_narratives/) + [histories](../06_history/) (how the name moved on *similar* news before — your comparables).
- **The macro frame** = the [scenario tree](../01_macro/MACRO_SCENARIO_TREE.md) + [regime note](../01_macro/ECB_RATE_REGIME.md): when news breaks, recognise *which branch* just got more probable.
- **The recap closes the loop** — every entry sharpens the reflex and feeds updates back into the files.

## What good looks like (the two reference recaps)
Two strong reference recaps — lengthy (not required daily), but each hits the bar. The patterns to copy:

**Example 1 — Fri Aug 11 (Macro / Single Stock / Classes / Desk).**
- *Macro (UK):* tied a BoE talk the day before to today's strong UK GDP print → **read-through to policy** ("likely keeps pressure on the BoE; screens moved the Sep hike probability 80%→88%"). Connected a class to live data — that's insight, not summary.
- *Single stock (Saipem +6.2% vs index):* not "it rose on contract news" — but **why the rally was that big**: "the reaction was driven by the *sizeable % of 2023 orders* this could represent — analysts est. ~10% of offshore E&C new orders." That's the *market-focus* insight.
- *Classes (rights issues):* takeaway framed as a transferable lesson ("communicating confidence levels; high conviction needed to preposition").
- *Desk rotation:* "started to think more in terms of vol"; noticed how often ER adjust event-vol and dividend estimates with traders. Specific, reflective.

**Example 2 — 14 July (Chats / Classes / Single Stock / Weekly takeaways).**
- *Chats:* a discussion on UK housing/PSN LN that reasoned through the **catalyst chain** — supply/demand imbalance → falling asking prices → balance-sheet/inventory write-down risk → **dividend at risk if cash is restrained** → *assigning a probability to that scenario.* That's the job in one bullet.
- *Tools:* concrete functions — BBG **SURP** (peer ripple from earnings surprises), the historical-data add-in to study moves around CPI/ECO events.
- *Weekly takeaways:* "getting a better feel for my basket"; learning to **estimate a story's impact via prior moves, revenue/bottom-line effect, and guidance/dividend implications**; named the inputs (a podcast, a video on key-driver differentiation) and the conclusion ("growth-impacting stories are the most impactful").
- *Focus for next week:* concrete — prep the presentation stock, **assign probabilities and price-range moves to selected catalysts.**

**The common thread:** every point is *expectation → reality → why*, or *a chain of reasoning toward a tradeable view (probability + price range)*. Copy that, not the length.

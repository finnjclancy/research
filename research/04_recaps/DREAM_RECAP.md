## 🗣️ 3 questions for 3 colleagues *(the daily habit — ask one each, every day)*
> The point isn't the answer alone — it's building relationships, surfacing how experienced people *think*, and pressure-testing my own views. Make them specific; note what I'd *do* with the answer.

1. **To a trader / market-maker —** *"Is the 'long banks / short utilities, higher-for-longer' trade actually crowded on the book, or is that just the loud consensus? I want to know if the positioning is as one-sided as it feels."* → if it's crowded, the asymmetry is to a dovish surprise; tells me how to size the bank/utility pair.
2. **To a senior researcher who covers semis/AI —** *"What's the single signpost you'd watch to know the AI-capex cycle is rolling over — before it shows in bookings?"* → I want their *leading* indicator, not the lagging one I already track (Amazon's capex guide).
3. **To a macro strategist / someone off-desk —** *"How do you read the gap between Warsh's hawkish dots and oil falling? Is the Fed's hawkishness real, or a credibility play that breaks dovish once inflation cools?"* → sharpens my view on the [rate regime](../01_macro/ECB_RATE_REGIME.md) and the bank/utility timing.

Not today.

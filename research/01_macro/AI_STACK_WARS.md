## 0. The one-paragraph state of play

In June 2026 the AI narrative fractured along four fault lines at once: (1) the **US government banned access to the two best Western models** (Anthropic's Fable 5 & Mythos 5) and is forcing **KYC/identity verification** on AI — handing the *usable* open-model crown to **China's GLM-5.2**, which was trained **without a single Nvidia chip**; (2) **hyperscaler AI capex (~$725bn for 2026) is about to exceed their cash flow** (aggregate FCF crosses zero ~Q3 2026), and the market has started **punishing capex** — turning the AI build-out into a *stock liability*; (3) **Nvidia has gone from chip vendor to the financier of the entire stack** (~$40bn of equity into its own customers + GPU-collateralised lending), inviting "circular financing / Enron" comparisons; and (4) the **Warsh Fed**, installed by Trump to cut rates, has instead turned **hawkish** into an oil-driven inflation spike, removing rate-cut guidance and flipping its dot-plot toward a *hike*. **Every one of these stories is bearish for the crowded AI-capex longs in the book if it deepens — and the supply chain (ASML, AMD) is most exposed.**

## 1. The Fable 5 / Mythos 5 export ban + the KYC regime change

**What happened:** Anthropic released **Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 on 9 June 2026**; on 12 June, Commerce Secretary **Howard Lutnick** placed both under **export controls barring access "by any foreign national, whether inside or outside the United States,"** citing a claimed jailbreak that could expose Mythos 5's cybersecurity reasoning ([Nextgov](https://www.nextgov.com/artificial-intelligence/2026/06/anthropic-suspends-top-ai-models-after-us-export-control-order/414173/), [Time](https://time.com/article/2026/06/13/anthropic-fable-mythos-ban-US-security/), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/06/13/anthropic-disables-fable-mythos-export-controls-national-security-threat/)).
- Because Anthropic **can't verify nationality in real time**, it had to **disable both models for everyone, worldwide** to comply ([IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/thought-for-the-week-us-government-order-forces-commercial-suspension-of-two-fronteir-ai-models)).
- Anthropic **disputed** the order — only "verbal evidence of a potential narrow, non-universal jailbreak," and the same technique applies to **OpenAI's GPT-5.5**, which was *not* restricted ([Cybersecurity Dive](https://www.cybersecuritydive.com/news/anthropic-us-government-export-ban-mythos-fable/822909/)).
- **The KYC endpoint:** to ever re-enable Fable 5, Commerce requires Anthropic to **verify identity + nationality**. From **8 July 2026**, Claude (Free/Pro/Max) requires **identity verification via Persona** — government ID + live selfie + liveness detection ([TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318778/20260621/claude-identity-verification-starts-july-8-what-facial-data-anthropic-collects.htm), [Crypto Briefing](https://cryptobriefing.com/anthropic-fable-5-identity-verification-backlash/)). GPT-5.6 "Sol" also went through a **government-approval process** ([Digital Applied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/us-ai-gatekeeping-china-open-source-advantage-2026)).

**The game theory / who's driving it:** the **US government** is treating frontier models like **munitions** (the ITAR/export-control playbook). Motive: national-security primacy + leverage. **Unintended consequence (the narrative that matters):** gatekeeping the best *closed* US models **cedes the open, self-hostable, no-permission-needed layer to China** — exactly where developers and sovereigns are migrating. NEAR's **Illia Polosukhin** said the ban "flipped the switch on decentralized AI" ([BigGo](https://finance.biggo.com/news/1797d056318b5d95)).

**→ Book:** indirect but real. (a) The **KYC/identity-verification + on-shore-compute** regime is a *demand pull for compliant US data-centre capacity* (mild positive for the hyperscaler/AI-power chain: Amazon/AWS, ABB, utilities). (b) It *accelerates the "sovereign AI" build-out* (every country wants its own stack) — positive for total compute demand but **negative for US closed-lab pricing power**. (c) It is a **template for tighter semis/model export policy** → tail risk for ASML/AMD's China TAM.

## 2. The China open-weight takeover & "token minimising"

**The crown moved East.** **GLM-5.2 (Zhipu/Z.ai, released 13 June 2026)** — a 744B-parameter MoE, 1M-token context, **MIT license** — scores **51 on the Intelligence Index v4.1**, ahead of DeepSeek V4 Pro (44), Kimi K2.6 (43), MiniMax-M3 (44); hits **62.1 on SWE-bench Pro**; and does it at **~1/6th the API cost of GPT-5.5** ([VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/z-ais-open-weights-glm-5-2-beats-gpt-5-5-on-multiple-long-horizon-coding-benchmarks-for-1-6th-the-cost), [MindStudio](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/what-is-glm-5-2-open-weight-model)). On Code Arena it is **the strongest model currently *usable at all*, "since Claude Fable 5 was removed following the US export ban"** ([Latent Space](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-glm-gpt-glm-52-passes-vibe)). Its **IndexShare** sparse-attention cuts per-token FLOPs ~2.9× at 1M context. **Z.ai is forecasting an "Open Fable" by end-2026** — i.e. an open model matching Anthropic's banned flagship.

**★ "Token minimising" — the cost story enterprises are acting on:**
- Chinese models are **5–30× cheaper**: DeepSeek V3.2 at **$0.28/M input** vs GPT-5.2 at ~$10/M (**35×**) ([AICost](https://aicost.org/blog/chinese-ai-models-cost-advantage-2026)).
- The mechanism is **Mixture-of-Experts**: DeepSeek activates **37B of 671B** params/query; Kimi K2.5 **32B of 1T** — **90–97% less inference compute** than equivalent dense models ([IntuitionLabs](https://intuitionlabs.ai/articles/deepseek-inference-cost-explained)).
- **Self-hosting:** quantised 4-bit Qwen/DeepSeek run on a single RTX 4090 at **<$0.01/M tokens** in electricity; enterprise self-managed inference stacks (vLLM/Triton/Ray) jumped from **11.3% → 17.9%** adoption in a quarter ([VentureBeat tracker](https://venturebeat.com/infrastructure/how-deepseeks-radical-architecture-is-shattering-silicon-valleys-token-moat)).
- **The result:** **Chinese models crossed ~60% of OpenRouter token consumption by mid-2026 (from <2% in early 2025)** ([ChatForest](https://chatforest.com/reviews/chinese-ai-models-openrouter-dominance-deepseek-kimi-minimax-glm-2026/)). China's domestic token economy is ~**140 trillion calls/day** ([AgentMarketCap](https://agentmarketcap.ai/blog/2026/04/14/china-ai-token-economy-140-trillion-daily-calls-global-dominance)).

**The game theory:** China is running an **open-source commoditisation strategy** — give away frontier-class weights to (a) win global mindshare/soft power, (b) undercut US labs' token pricing, and (c) build a **non-Nvidia, non-US-permission supply chain**. It directly attacks the **revenue model of the closed US labs** (OpenAI/Anthropic), whose moat was "best model + you must pay per token." The US response — **OpenAI's gpt-oss (20b/120b, Apache 2.0)** and **Reflection AI** (an American open-weight lab; its Shinsegae/Korea deal shows sovereign demand) — is real but **benchmark-behind the Chinese open models** ([wccftech](https://wccftech.com/the-us-makes-its-first-modern-foray-into-open-source-models-with-gpt-oss-but-how-does-it-stack-up-against-chinese-counterparts/), [The American Spectator](https://spectator.org/american-dominance-in-ai-starts-with-open-weight-models/)).

**→ Book:** **this is the deflationary core of the AI-Reckoning risk (S3).** If frontier intelligence is **cheap, open, and self-hostable**, then (a) the **closed labs' revenue** that justifies hyperscaler capex is in doubt → pressure to **cut capex** (S3 trigger) → **bearish ASML/AMD/Amazon-AWS demand**; and (b) "token minimising" means **less compute per unit of value over time** — the long-run bear case for the whole compute build-out. Counter: cheaper tokens → **Jevons paradox** (more total usage) → could *raise* aggregate compute (bullish). **The book's AI longs hinge on which of these wins.**

## 3. The chip game theory: Nvidia vs the labs vs custom silicon

**The labs and hyperscalers are building their own chips to escape Nvidia's margin** (Nvidia just printed **~$81.6bn quarterly revenue, +85% YoY, ~$75.2bn data-centre** — *that* margin is what everyone wants to claw back) ([Fortune/Burry](https://fortune.com/2025/11/24/big-short-investor-michael-burry-nvidia-cisco-ai-bubble/)):
- **Broadcom is the arms dealer of the revolt** — design partner behind **Google TPU, Meta MTIA, Microsoft Maia**, and (confirmed early 2026) **OpenAI + Anthropic's "Titan" accelerator program**. **OpenAI unveiled its first Broadcom-built chip on 24 June 2026** (slipped to Q3 2026); Broadcom shipped the first **2nm custom SoC with 3.5D packaging** in Feb 2026 ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/24/openai-unveils-its-first-custom-chip-built-by-broadcom/), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/semiconductors/custom-ai-asics-examined-from-broadcom-to-mtia)).
- **Anthropic trains on ~500,000 Trainium2 chips at AWS; AWS has deployed >1M Trainium** ([Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/semiconductors/open-ai-building-its-own-chip-still-dependent-on-nvidia)).
- **BUT** — every one of these **still runs major workloads on Nvidia**; custom silicon is *diversification, not replacement* (yet).

**★ Nvidia's counter-attack — commoditise the model layer (the user's thesis, confirmed):** Nvidia is **investing $26bn in open-weight models** and shipping the **Nemotron 3 family** (Nemotron 3 Super 128B; a 550B pretrained model; NVFP4 on Blackwell), plus **PersonaPlex** which "commoditised the voice-AI stack overnight" ([techbuzz](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/nvidia-bets-26b-on-open-weight-ai-models-to-challenge-openai), [the-decoder](https://the-decoder.com/nvidia-steps-into-the-open-source-ai-gap-that-openai-meta-and-anthropic-left-behind/)). **The logic:** if the *labs* threaten Nvidia by building chips, Nvidia threatens the *labs* by **giving away models** — if the model layer commoditises, the labs lose pricing power and **value pools back into hardware (Nvidia) and the application layer**, not the model. **Nvidia profits whether open or closed wins, because both run on its chips** ([MindStudio](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/nvidia-26b-open-source-ai-bet-explained)). This is a **classic platform-holder "commoditise your complement" play** (à la Google/Android, Microsoft/IE).

**→ Book — AMD is the most-exposed name:**
- **Bull for AMD:** the **diversification-away-from-Nvidia** wave is exactly AMD's opening (a credible #2 GPU + a more open software stack vs CUDA lock-in). Every hyperscaler/lab that wants a second source is an AMD opportunity (cf. AMD's OpenAI/Meta deals in [`../03_companies/AMD.md`](../03_companies/AMD.md)).
- **Bear for AMD:** the *custom-ASIC* wave (Broadcom/TPU/Trainium) competes with **both** Nvidia *and* AMD merchant GPUs — if hyperscalers shift training/inference to in-house ASICs, the merchant-GPU TAM (AMD's market) shrinks. **Broadcom is arguably the bigger structural winner than AMD here.**
- **For ASML:** more chip *designers* (labs, hyperscalers, Nvidia, Broadcom, Huawei) all racing to leading-edge = **more demand for EUV/High-NA** — *as long as the capex holds.* ASML is agnostic to *who* wins the chip war; it loses only if total leading-edge capex falls (S3).

## 4. The neo-lab / circular-financing / allocation-lock-in game

**Nvidia has become the financier of its own demand.** It deployed **~$40bn+ of AI equity in 2026**: **$30bn into OpenAI**, **Anthropic's Series G** (a $30bn round → **$380bn valuation**), **xAI's $20bn Series E** (xAI merged with SpaceX in Feb 2026), **CoreWeave ($2bn), Nebius ($2bn), IREN, Corning, Mistral** ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/09/nvidia-embraces-ai-investor-topping-40-billion-in-equity-bets-2026.html), [Spaziocrypto](https://en.spaziocrypto.com/ai/nvidia-40-billion-ai-equity-investments-2026-openai/)). Plus **GPU-collateralised lending**: **>$11bn** lent to neoclouds to buy Nvidia chips; **CoreWeave alone has $14.2bn of GPU-backed debt** ([Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4915653-nvidia-coreweave-and-nebius-inside-the-circular-financing-of-the-gpu-boom)).

**★ The allocation-lock-in leverage (the user's thesis, confirmed):** Nvidia treats **neoclouds as its "primary distribution channel" for Blackwell/Rubin through 2030** and backs them with equity + long-term allocation commitments + AI-factory design help ([SHARON AI](https://sharonai.com/blog/nvidias-positioning-shift-and-the-rise-of-neoclouds/)). The **leverage is implicit**: a neocloud or neo-lab that defects to TPUs/ASICs risks losing **GPU allocation priority, equity backing, and Nvidia's halo in its IPO/raise.** Critics call it **"pre-funding the purchase of your own GPUs"** ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/03/23/neocloud-expansion-nvidia-ai-strategy-thecubepod/)). **Michael Burry** likens it to **Enron-style vendor financing** and "the Cisco of this bubble," noting **87% of VC now flows to AI, 38% of high-yield and 49% of investment-grade debt issuance is AI-linked** ([Yahoo/Burry](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/burry-calls-fraud-hyperscalers-4-131841279.html), [Cryptonomist](https://en.cryptonomist.ch/2026/05/20/michael-burry-ai-bubble-warning/)).

**The game theory:** Nvidia is using **capital as a moat** — when your customers can build substitutes (chips) and your suppliers can squeeze you, you **lock in distribution and demand with equity + financing**, raising defection costs. It works while the music plays; it is **reflexive and fragile** if AI revenue disappoints (the collateral — GPUs — depreciates faster than the debt; a demand wobble cascades through the leverage).

**→ Book:** this is the **financial-plumbing transmission of S3.** A crack in AI demand doesn't just cut orders — it **detonates the GPU-collateralised debt + circular equity**, which would hit sentiment across the *entire* complex faster and harder than fundamentals alone (ASML, AMD, Amazon, ABB, utilities all re-rate on the *systemic* fear, not just their own numbers). **This is why the AI-Reckoning move would overshoot.**

## 5. The hyperscaler capex/FCF crisis — "cut capex to save the stock"

**The numbers:** 2026 hyperscaler capex ≈ **$725bn** — **Microsoft $190bn, Amazon $200bn, Alphabet $185bn, Meta $125–145bn** ([StrongMocha](https://strongmocha.com/ai-infrastructure-data-centers/the-725-billion-question-hyperscaler-capex-q1-2026-and-what-the-earnings-don-t-a/)). Capex is growing ~70%/yr while operating cash flow grows ~23%/yr — **aggregate free cash flow crosses zero around Q3 2026** (Oracle already negative; **Amazon crossing ~now**; Alphabet ~Q1 2027; Meta ~Q3 2027; Microsoft ~Q3 2028) ([Epoch AI](https://epoch.ai/data-insights/hyperscaler-capex-vs-cash-flow)). Capex/revenue has gone from 10–15% pre-AI to **25–30%**; Morgan Stanley sees hyperscaler **borrowing topping $400bn in 2026 (2× 2025)** ([Breckinridge](https://www.breckinridge.com/insights/the-price-of-ai-how-capex-is-rewriting-tech-balance-sheets)).

**★ The market has flipped capex from virtue to vice:** **Meta's market cap fell ~$175bn after a quarter it *beat*; Alphabet fell ~6% on an $84.75bn equity raise** to fund hardware ([Let's Data Science](https://letsdatascience.com/blog/hyperscaler-ai-capex-outruns-cash-flow-q3-2026-oracle)). **The game theory (the user's thesis):** once the market punishes capex, each hyperscaler has an incentive to **signal capex discipline to defend its stock** — but it's a **prisoner's dilemma**: the first to *credibly* cut capex protects its FCF/stock **but signals AI demand is softening**, which would **crater the AI supply chain (Nvidia, ASML, AMD)** and invite "they're blinking" narratives. So they keep spending in lockstep — until one breaks. **The first credible hyperscaler capex guide-down is the single most likely trigger of the AI Reckoning (S3).**

**→ Book — this is the most direct, datable risk to the book's AI longs:**
- **Amazon ([`../03_companies/AMAZON.md`](../03_companies/AMAZON.md)):** *epicentre.* Its **$200bn capex with FCF crossing zero now** is precisely why the stock fell ~18% from its May high despite a beat. **Amazon's own capex guide is the trigger to watch first.**
- **ASML / AMD:** their order books *are* hyperscaler capex. A coordinated capex slowdown is the bookings-miss / demand-air-pocket that re-rates them (cf. ASML's documented −16%/−11% reactions).
- **ABB / Enel / Iberdrola:** lose the **data-centre power-demand growth leg** if capex slows.

## 6. US–China chip game theory — the export controls are backfiring

**The core fact that changes everything: China trained a frontier model without Nvidia.** GLM-5 (Feb 2026) and now **GLM-5.2** were trained **entirely on Huawei Ascend (910B/910C)** — Zhipu coordinated ~**100,000 Ascend processors**; training took ~**15% more compute time** but was **cheaper** (low chip price + **government subsidies**) ([Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/huawei-led-team-claims-it-post-trained-deepseeks-1-6-trillion-parameter-models-on-ascend-910c-chips), [Let's Data Science](https://letsdatascience.com/blog/china-trained-frontier-ai-model-glm-5-without-nvidia)). **DeepSeek V4** is explicitly tuned for Ascend ([resultsense](https://www.resultsense.com/news/2026-04-24-deepseek-v4-huawei-ascend-chips)).

**The game theory of the chip war (both sides):**
- **US bet:** deny China leading-edge chips (Nvidia H20 ban, ASML EUV ban) → kneecap Chinese AI.
- **China's counter (working):** **forced import-substitution** — Huawei Ascend + SMIC + MindSpore + government subsidies built a **parallel, self-sufficient stack**. Export controls **accelerated rather than prevented** Chinese hardware independence ([Let's Data Science](https://letsdatascience.com/blog/china-trained-frontier-ai-model-glm-5-without-nvidia)).
- **The bind for the US:** every tightening (a) **destroys US vendors' China revenue** (Nvidia, ASML, AMD lose the largest market) while (b) **strengthening Huawei/SMIC's domestic scale** — the opposite of the intent. And **China's open-weight strategy exports its models globally** regardless of chip controls.

**→ Book:**
- **ASML:** the swing variable. SMIC still needs ASML **DUV** to make Ascend chips at scale — so DUV-to-China is leverage the US/Netherlands hold. But further EUV/DUV tightening **caps ASML's China TAM** (already guided down in [`../03_companies/ASML.md`](../03_companies/ASML.md)). A *negotiated* easing (trade-deal optimism) is upside; escalation is downside.
- **AMD:** China is a capped TAM (MI308 licenses); a Huawei-dominated China is a **lost market** but also **removes a competitor's distraction**. Net: China is a shrinking part of AMD's story; the AI-capex cycle in the West dominates.
- **Melexis:** Chinese auto-chip localisation is a slow competitive threat (see [`../03_companies/MELEXIS.md`](../03_companies/MELEXIS.md)).

## 7. The government-as-investor turn — and how it links to the model ban

**Washington is becoming an equity investor in AI/chips.** Precedent: **Aug 2025, the US converted $11.1bn of CHIPS Act grants into 433.3m Intel shares at $20.47 — a ~10% (9.9%) non-voting stake** ([Intel](https://www.intc.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1748/intel-and-trump-administration-reach-historic-agreement-to)). Now: **Sen. Sanders' "American AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act" (1 June 2026)**; **Trump in talks with OpenAI/Altman about a government stake (5 June)**; Lutnick floating a **"national and economic security fund"** (housing Japan/Korea trade-deal investments); Hassett calling Intel "a down payment on a sovereign wealth fund" ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/05/trump-open-ai-altman-stake.html), [Semafor](https://www.semafor.com/article/06/17/2026/trump-advisers-weigh-structure-of-potential-ai-stakes), [Modern Diplomacy](https://moderndiplomacy.eu/2026/06/25/americas-most-surprising-new-tech-investor-in-2026-is-the-federal-government/)).

**★ The connective tissue (the user's insight):** the **model ban + KYC + government equity stakes** are one coherent move — **the AI complex is being nationalised/securitised.** The state is simultaneously (a) **gatekeeping who can use the models** (Fable ban, KYC, GPT-5.6 approval), (b) **taking equity in the winners** (Intel template → OpenAI talks), and (c) **directing the export regime**. The labs **withdrawing models from customers** is the private sector complying with (a). **Game-theory motive:** the US is treating AI as **strategic national infrastructure** (like defence/nuclear) — primacy over profit, with the government as both regulator *and* shareholder.

**→ Book:** mostly indirect, but: (a) **state backing de-risks the US AI build-out** (a floor under the capex story — bullish supply chain at the margin); (b) it **raises the geopolitical/regulatory beta of the whole complex** (ASML/AMD now trade on policy headlines as much as fundamentals); (c) a US **sovereign-AI-fund** + **"sovereign AI" demand globally** is a **structural tailwind to total compute** (Amazon/AWS, ABB, utilities) even as it fragments the market.

## 8. The Fed: the Warsh game theory ("hawkish to let air out, not to hike")

**The setup:** Trump installed **Kevin Warsh as Fed Chair (sworn in 22 May 2026)** explicitly **seeking rate cuts** — Trump said Warsh "would not have gotten the job" if he didn't want to cut ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/trump-kevin-warsh-fed-chair-interest-rates.html), [NBC](https://www.nbcnews.com/business/economy/kevin-warsh-sworn-in-fed-chair-rcna346433)). **Then the oil shock hit** (gasoline up, inflation at a 3-year high). At his **first meeting (17 June 2026)** Warsh **held at 3.50–3.75% (12-0), removed the rate-cut outlook, and the dot-plot flipped — 9 of 18 now see a *hike* in 2026**; inflation forecasts were raised to **3.6% headline / 3.3% core** (from 2.7%), with **17 of 18 seeing upside inflation risk** ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html), [Fed statement](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm), [Fox](https://www.foxbusiness.com/economy/federal-reserve-interest-rate-decision-june-17-2026)).

**★ The user's hypothesis — "hawkish to let air out, but no real intention to hike" — is a sharp read, and there are two competing interpretations:**
1. **Genuine hawk (face value):** Warsh, a long-time hard-money hawk, is reacting to a real inflation spike and means it — higher-for-longer, possible hike.
2. **★ Credibility theatre (the contrarian read):** Warsh is a **Trump appointee the market suspects is political**, so he *must over-signal independence and inflation-vigilance* to earn credibility — and **jawboning hawkish (a dot-plot hike, removing cut guidance) is a cheap way to cool inflation expectations and let froth out of markets *without actually hiking*.** Talk does the work; the oil shock provides cover. Once oil rolls over (the de-escalation in [`ECB_RATE_REGIME.md`](ECB_RATE_REGIME.md)), the inflation impulse fades and Warsh can pivot to the cuts Trump wants — having banked credibility. The **"task forces to overhaul Fed operations"** and balance-sheet focus fit a Chair managing optics ([CNBC recap](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-meeting-today-live-updates.html)).

**Why it matters which one it is:** under (1), higher US rates persist → pressure on long-duration AI longs + the dollar; under (2), the hawkishness is a **fade** — the moment oil eases, the dovish pivot (S1) arrives and **rate-sensitive longs (utilities) and growth multiples re-rate up.** **Watch the gap between Warsh's *words* (hawkish) and the *data* (oil rolling over) — if oil falls and he stays hawkish, it's theatre about to break dovish.**

**→ Book:** this *is* the US-rate axis of the book. Mirrors the ECB note: hawkish-for-real helps banks / hurts utilities + long-duration growth (ASML/AMD/Amazon multiples); a "theatre → dovish pivot" helps utilities + growth and caps the bank trade. **The Fed-independence question also feeds the "Sell America" scenario (S7)** — a Fed seen as politically captured is a key ingredient of a dollar/Treasury-confidence crisis.

## 9. ★ Synthesis — what it means for the book & the scenario tree

**The four fault lines all point the same way for the crowded AI longs — *down if they deepen, with the supply chain most exposed*:**

| Narrative | Direction if it deepens | Most-exposed book names |
|---|---|---|
| China open-weight + token-minimising deflates lab revenue | bearish AI capex | ASML, AMD, Amazon |
| Hyperscaler capex > FCF → "cut to save the stock" | bearish (the **trigger**) | Amazon (epicentre), ASML, AMD, ABB, utilities |
| Circular financing / GPU-debt cracks | bearish (systemic, overshoots) | whole complex |
| China trains on Huawei (export controls backfire) | caps China TAM / policy beta | ASML, AMD, Melexis |
| Government nationalises the stack (ban/KYC/stakes) | mixed: de-risks US build-out, raises policy beta | ASML, AMD, Amazon |
| Warsh hawkish-for-real | bearish long-duration / bullish banks | ASML/AMD/Amazon multiples ↓; BNP/ACA ↑ |
| Warsh theatre → dovish pivot | bullish long-duration + utilities | Enel/Iberdrola ↑; growth multiples ↑ |

**The single most important watch-item:** **the next hyperscaler capex guide (Amazon first).** It is the cleanest, most datable trigger for **S3 (AI Reckoning)** — and because of the circular financing + GPU-debt leverage (§4), the move would **overshoot** the fundamentals. Conversely, a capex *raise* + AWS reacceleration is **S4 (AI Vertical)** and extends the crowded longs.

**New game-theoretic nuance to carry on the intercom:** the AI complex is now a **reflexive, levered, politically-entangled system** — so AI-stock moves increasingly trade on **second-order signals** (a capex hint, a Burry tweet, a policy headline, a Chinese model release) *before* the fundamentals confirm. The narrative leads the numbers by more than usual. **Pre-position the ranges for these triggers; don't wait for the print.**

## 10. Unique perspectives & contrarian takes circulating (the "what people are talking about")
- **"Margin migration, not bubble"** — the debate isn't *if* AI pays off but *where the margin lands*: chips (Nvidia) → models (labs) → apps. Token-minimising + open weights argue the **model layer is being commoditised**, so margin migrates to **hardware + applications**, hollowing out the labs ([Business Model Analyst](https://businessmodelanalyst.com/ai-bubble-margin-migration/)).
- **"Nvidia is the Cisco of this cycle"** (Burry) — peak-multiple hardware vendor financing its own demand; the GPU-collateralised-debt spiral is the fragility ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/11/24/big-short-investor-michael-burry-nvidia-cisco-ai-bubble/)).
- **"Export controls handed China the open-source crown"** — gatekeeping closed US models pushed the world onto Chinese open weights; US AI *soft power* is eroding even as US *capability* leads ([RAND](https://www.rand.org/pubs/perspectives/PEA4686-1.html)).
- **"Jevons vs deflation"** — does cheaper inference *raise* total compute (bullish) or *shrink* the revenue pool (bearish)? The whole AI-capex thesis rides on this.
- **"Sovereign AI is the next demand leg"** — every nation wants its own stack (Reflection/Shinsegae, EU sovereignty, ASML-Mistral) — a structural tailwind to total compute that partly offsets the capex-digestion fear.
- **"The first hyperscaler to cut capex wins the stock but loses the narrative"** — the prisoner's-dilemma framing of the capex crisis.

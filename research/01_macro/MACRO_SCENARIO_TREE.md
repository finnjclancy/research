# The Macro Scenario Tree — narrative-shift decision paths

> The long decision tree: **how the macro story may change → how each sector's perception/place changes → how each company's story flips.** Narrative-first. Built on [`../00_system/NARRATIVE_ENGINE.md`](../00_system/NARRATIVE_ENGINE.md) and [`../02_sectors/SECTOR_NARRATIVES.md`](../02_sectors/SECTOR_NARRATIVES.md); current regime in [`ECB_RATE_REGIME.md`](ECB_RATE_REGIME.md).
>
> **How to read:** each **Sx** is a *new story the world could start telling itself.* Probabilities are rough feel (June 2026), not precision — they exist so you can weight branches. Each scenario branches **MACRO → SECTOR → the COMPANY that becomes *most relevant* (the name you call first on the intercom)**, with the **direction & character** of the perception shift. Magnitudes are *relative* (the company files' §7 hold the elasticities); the tree tells you *which way and how hard*.
>
> **Starting point (the story today):** an easing oil shock (Brent off its >$120 peak), the **ECB's first hike since 2023**, a **crowded AI-capex super-cycle** (ASML/AMD/Amazon/ABB stage-3), a **broken-but-stabilising Novo**, and a **weak China**. Everything below is a deviation from *that*.

## S1 — "The Relief": oil collapses, Iran fully de-escalates → disinflation → dovish ECB/Fed pivot
**Probability:** Medium-High (the base case if Hormuz reopens). **Driven by:** de-escalation (politicians/diplomacy) → oil down → central banks (credibility now lets them ease) → fast money rotates. **Trigger to watch:** Brent breaking back below pre-war levels; a soft eurozone CPI print; ECB/Fed tone-softening.

**Mechanism:** the inflation impulse that forced the June hike fades → rate-cut expectations rebuild → the discount rate on long-duration assets falls → the bank-vs-utility see-saw reverses.

**SECTOR paths:**
- **Utilities → re-rate UP** (the bond-proxy + electrification story gets its dovish tailwind; *place in world improves: "defensive growth at a lower discount rate"*).
- **Banks → de-rate from the peak** (the NII tailwind is now "as good as it gets"; the story shifts from "rates rising" to "rates have peaked").
- **Long-duration growth (semis, mega-cap tech) → multiple expansion** (lower discount rate helps, *independent* of the AI debate).
- **Staples → modest input-cost relief**; **consumer/discretionary → bid** (real incomes improve).

**COMPANY paths (most relevant first):**
- **Iberdrola / Enel ↑↑** — *the cleanest expression.* Perception flips from "rate-pressured bond proxy" to "electrification growth you can finally pay up for." Iberdrola (higher quality) leads; Enel adds yield + deleveraging. **Most relevant call.**
- **BNP / Crédit Agricole / Banca Mediolanum ↓** — perception shifts from "rate winner" to "peak earnings, what's the next leg?" The *quality* of the de-rate depends on whether cuts come *with* a soft landing (mild) or a recession (severe — see S2). BMED also helped by markets-up though.
- **ASML / AMD / Amazon ↑** — multiple expansion on lower rates *even if* AI fundamentals are unchanged — but this is the *weaker* driver vs the AI debate (S3/S4 dominate them).

## S2 — "Stagflation entrenches": inflation sticky AND growth weak (oil re-spikes or wages/services stick)
**Probability:** Medium. **Driven by:** Hormuz re-closing / OPEC discipline / sticky services inflation, *with* eurozone already-contracting growth. Central banks trapped (can't cut without re-igniting inflation; can't hike without deepening recession). **Trigger:** rising CPI + falling PMIs together; oil back toward $100+.

**Mechanism:** the worst regime — rising costs + weak demand + no central-bank rescue. Multiples compress broadly; defensives and pricing-power win; cyclicals and credit-sensitive lose.

**SECTOR paths:**
- **Staples → relative winners on defensiveness BUT input-cost squeeze** (net: the ones with *pricing power* win, the ones with *volume* exposure lose).
- **Industrials → de-rate** (cyclical orders roll over).
- **Banks → trapped** (NII up on higher rates, but **cost of risk rises** as the economy weakens — the leverage turns against them).
- **Utilities → mixed** (higher rates bad, but defensiveness + inflation-linked regulated returns cushion).
- **Discretionary → de-rate** (squeezed consumer).

**COMPANY paths (most relevant first):**
- **Carlsberg / Unilever** — split screen: **input costs (barley, aluminium, oil-linked, ag) squeeze gross margin** (↓), but **defensive flows + pricing power** (↑). Net: Unilever (more pricing power, premium) holds better; Carlsberg (China + volume) more exposed. Perception: "defensive haven" vs "margin victim" — *the volume/price split decides which*.
- **Kone / ABB ↓** — cyclical orders de-rate; "late-cycle quality" becomes "late-cycle, full stop."
- **BNP / ACA ↓** — the bear case for banks: **cost of risk** becomes the headline, overwhelming the NII benefit. Perception flips from "rate winner" to "recession-credit risk."
- **Novo ↑ (relative)** — non-cyclical pharma demand is a defensive port; the GLP-1 story is macro-independent.

## S3 — ★ "The AI Reckoning": AI-capex digestion / ROI doubt → hyperscalers slow the spend
> **Deep dive + full sourcing in [`AI_STACK_WARS.md`](AI_STACK_WARS.md).** As of June 2026 this is no longer hypothetical — the *mechanism is loaded*: hyperscaler 2026 capex ≈ **$725bn** with aggregate **FCF crossing zero ~Q3 2026** (Amazon crossing now); the market has **flipped capex from virtue to vice** (Meta −$175bn mcap after a *beat*; Alphabet −6% on an equity raise); Nvidia's **~$40bn of circular AI equity + GPU-collateralised debt** is a leverage amplifier (Burry's "Cisco/Enron" warning); and **China's open-weight models (GLM-5.2) + token-minimising** are deflating the lab revenue that justifies the spend.

**Probability:** Medium-High and rising (the single highest-impact risk to the book, because so much is stage-3 crowded). **Driven by:** a hyperscaler (Amazon/Microsoft/Google/Meta) guiding capex *down* or flagging weak AI ROI; **media flips to "AI bubble"**; fast money unwinds the crowded long; CEOs stop being rewarded for "AI" framing. **★ The cleanest, most datable trigger: the next hyperscaler capex guide — Amazon first** (its $200bn capex + FCF crossing zero is the epicentre). Because of the circular-financing/GPU-debt leverage, **the move overshoots the fundamentals.**

**Mechanism:** the most crowded narrative in markets breaks. Because ASML/AMD/Amazon/ABB are all stage-3, **good news stops working and small misses overshoot.** This is one macro story that re-prices *five* sectors at once (the AI-capex wire).

**SECTOR paths:**
- **Semis → de-rate hard** (the toll-road and the engines both sold; ASML bookings narrative cracks, AMD's deal-visibility doubted).
- **Mega-cap cloud → de-rate** (the capex that was "investing into demand" becomes "value destruction").
- **Industrials → lose the data-centre order leg** (ABB's electrification premium compresses).
- **Utilities → lose the AI-power-demand growth story** (back to being "just" rate-sensitive bond proxies — a *double* hit if rates aren't also falling).

**COMPANY paths (most relevant first):**
- **ASML ↓↓** — *the highest-conviction call.* Perception flips from "indispensable AI monopoly" to "peak-cycle, China-exposed, priced for perfection." Bookings become the prosecutor's evidence. **Largest, fastest move** given stage-3 crowding (cf. its documented −16%/−11% reactions).
- **AMD ↓↓** — even more violent (higher beta, ~58× multiple, OpenAI/Meta deals re-read as concentration risk + dilution). Perception: "Nvidia challenger" → "speculative AI beta."
- **Amazon ↓** — the **$200bn capex** flips from bull to bear overnight; "AWS reaccel" must hold or it's the epicentre. *Amazon's own capex guide is the most likely trigger of this whole scenario — watch it first.*
- **ABB ↓** — electrification premium compresses as data-centre orders soften; "arms dealer of the capex boom" → "late-cycle industrial above target price."
- **Enel / Iberdrola ↓ (story), but cushioned** — lose the AI-power narrative, yet if the Reckoning *also* drags rates down (risk-off), the bond-proxy bid partly offsets. Net mild-negative on story, ambiguous on multiple.

## S4 — "AI Goes Vertical": productivity/AGI proof → capex accelerates, TAM re-rates up
**Probability:** Medium. **Driven by:** a step-change AI capability/monetisation proof; hyperscalers *raising* capex; CEOs and media reinforcing; momentum + passive flows pile in. **Trigger:** AWS/Azure re-acceleration + raised capex + a killer enterprise-AI ROI datapoint.

**Mechanism:** the crowded narrative gets *more* crowded and *justified* — stage-3 extends rather than breaks. The AI-capex wire fires positive across five sectors.

**COMPANY paths:** **ASML ↑↑** (bookings re-accelerate, High-NA adoption broadens), **AMD ↑↑** (share-gain + new hyperscaler deals), **Amazon ↑** (capex vindicated by AWS growth → FCF fear fades), **ABB ↑** (data-centre orders surge), **Enel/Iberdrola ↑** (the power-demand story gains a hard tailwind — *the under-appreciated way to play AI without paying semis multiples*). Perception across all: "the super-cycle is real and longer than feared."

## S5 — "China Bazooka": Beijing goes all-in on reflationary stimulus
**Probability:** Low-Medium. **Driven by:** CCP politics (growth/employment/social-stability imperative) → large fiscal + property + consumption stimulus. **Trigger:** a credible multi-trillion-yuan package; property stabilisation measures; a CNY/credit-impulse turn.

**Mechanism:** China-demand re-rates; commodities/oil firm (a mild global-inflation read-through that complicates the rate story); China-beta names lead.

**SECTOR paths:** **Industrials (China construction) ↑**, **Staples (China consumer) ↑**, **Semis (China chip demand) ↑**, **Materials/commodities ↑** (not in book directly).

**COMPANY paths (most relevant first):**
- **Kone ↑↑** — *the cleanest China-property proxy.* Perception flips from "China new-build drag" to "China bottoming → the services+new-build combo re-rates." The second-derivative trade (it can rip on *less-bad* China). **Most relevant call.**
- **Carlsberg ↑** — Western-China beer volumes inflect; "China consumer recovery option" pays off.
- **ASML / Melexis ↑** — China chip-demand and auto demand improve (ASML: more DUV/China systems; Melexis: China EV/auto).
- **ABB ↑** — China industrial/automation orders.
- *Caveat:* if the stimulus reflates oil/commodities, it **complicates S1's dovish pivot** — watch the cross-current.

## S6 — "China Japanification": property collapse + deflation deepen
**Probability:** Medium. **Driven by:** failed stimulus, debt-deflation, consumer retrenchment. **Trigger:** worsening property data, falling China CPI/PPI, weak consumption.

**Mechanism:** China-demand names de-rate; **but China deflation exports disinflation globally** → helps the dovish-rate (utility) trade.

**COMPANY paths:** **Kone ↓↓** ("China bottom" keeps not arriving → de-rate; the services annuity becomes the only support), **Carlsberg ↓** (China consumer drag persists), **Melexis ↓** (China EV/auto + price competition), **ASML ↓ (mild)** (China demand soft, offset by AI ex-China). **Utilities ↑ (relative)** — global disinflation pulls rates down (the S1 tailwind via a different door).

## S7 — "Sell America": US fiscal/dollar/Treasury confidence crisis (+ Fed-independence threat)
**Probability:** Low-Medium (tail, but rising as a *narrative*). **Driven by:** US deficits/debt, Trump pressure on Fed independence, **bond vigilantes**, reserve-diversification; gold up, USD down, term premium up. **Trigger:** a failed Treasury auction / spiking long yields / a credibility-shaking political move on the Fed; gold breaking out.

**Mechanism:** capital rotates *out of US assets* → US mega-cap de-rate + USD weakness; **European/non-US assets relatively bid**; higher US term premium pressures long-duration globally. A reflexive confidence story (media-amplified).

**SECTOR paths:** **US mega-cap tech → de-rate** (valuation + USD); **European equities → relative safe-haven bid**; **gold/non-US → up**; rates up at the long end (bad for global duration).

**COMPANY paths (most relevant first):**
- **Amazon ↓ / AMD ↓** — US mega-caps bear the brunt: de-rating + (for EUR-based holders) USD translation loss. Perception: "American exceptionalism premium" deflates.
- **European banks ↑ (relative)** — BNP/ACA/BMED benefit from EU capital repatriation + still-decent rates; "own Europe not America."
- **ASML — mixed** — European-listed (a relative positive) but USD-exposed earnings and a global-semis beta (a negative); the China/US-policy cross-currents dominate.
- **Utilities — pressured** if global long yields rise (term premium), helped if it's purely a US-equity-risk event.

- **Iberdrola / Enel ↑** — grid investment is central to EU energy security/electrification; regulated-growth story gets a policy tailwind.

## S10 — "Tariff Wall": Trump global tariff / trade-war escalation
**Probability:** Medium (a recurring live risk). **Driven by:** US politics (leverage, "wins"), retaliation cycles. **Trigger:** new tariff announcements on semis/autos/imports; retaliation.

**Mechanism:** supply-chain cost + demand-uncertainty shock; export-heavy and import-heavy names hit; a stagflationary tilt (cost-push inflation + growth drag → complicates rates).

**COMPANY paths (most relevant first):**
- **ASML ↓** — semicap tariffs hit gross margin + delay customer capex (the *explicit* reason for its Jul-2025 caution). Perception: "indispensable monopoly" → "geopolitical hostage."
- **Melexis ↓** — auto tariffs hit car production + auto-chip demand.
- **Amazon ↓** — import COGS (China sourcing) squeeze retail margin.
- **Broad ↓ on uncertainty**; banks relatively insulated (domestic), staples mixed (cost vs defensiveness).

## AI-complex sub-scenarios (S11–S14) — added June 2026
> The AI fault lines have become distinct, tradeable narratives in their own right. Full game theory + sources in [`AI_STACK_WARS.md`](AI_STACK_WARS.md). These often fire *together* with S3/S4.

### S11 — "Token deflation / margin migration": open weights + cheap inference commoditise the model layer
**Probability:** Medium-High (structurally underway). **Story:** Chinese open models (GLM-5.2 at ~1/6th GPT-5.5's cost) + MoE/self-hosting (Chinese models ~60% of OpenRouter tokens) mean **intelligence gets cheap and open** → the *closed labs'* per-token revenue erodes → the capex that funds it looks unjustified. **Margin migrates** from models → hardware + applications.
- **Sector path:** bearish the *revenue* underpinning AI capex → feeds S3. Ambiguous for compute *volume* (Jevons paradox could offset).
- **Company paths:** **ASML/AMD/Amazon ↓** if it triggers capex cuts; **but Amazon (AWS) ↑ relative** as a *neutral arms-dealer of inference* hosting whatever model wins (incl. open/Chinese) — AWS monetises tokens regardless of which model. *The nuance: token deflation hurts the model-makers more than the infra-renters.*

### S12 — "The AI complex gets nationalised": model bans + KYC + government equity stakes
**Probability:** Medium (already happening — Fable 5/Mythos 5 ban, July-8 KYC, Intel-stake precedent, OpenAI-stake talks). **Story:** the US treats AI as strategic infrastructure — gatekeeps model access, takes equity in winners, tightens exports.
- **Sector path:** **raises policy-beta of the whole complex** (ASML/AMD/Amazon trade on policy headlines); de-risks the US build-out at the margin (state floor); fragments the global market (sovereign-AI demand).
- **Company paths:** **ASML/AMD ↓ on tighter-export headlines, ↑ on easing/trade-deal headlines** (now a policy-driven stock); **Amazon/AWS ↑** on sovereign + KYC-compliant on-shore-compute demand. *Trade the policy calendar, not just earnings.*

### S13 — "Chip Cold War splits": Huawei/SMIC build a parallel non-Nvidia stack
**Probability:** Medium (GLM-5/5.2 already trained on Huawei Ascend). **Story:** export controls **backfire** — China achieves near-parity domestic AI hardware + subsidies → a bifurcated world (US stack vs China stack).
- **Company paths:** **ASML ↓ China-TAM** (but retains DUV leverage over SMIC), **AMD/Melexis ↓ China share**; a *negotiated easing* is the upside surprise. Escalation = lost China revenue for the US semis chain.

### S14 — "Nvidia commoditises the labs": the platform holder attacks its complement
**Probability:** Medium (Nvidia's $26bn open-model/Nemotron bet is live). **Story:** to counter labs building their own chips (Broadcom ASICs/TPU/Trainium), Nvidia gives away models → labs lose pricing power → value pools to hardware + apps.
- **Company paths:** mildly **bearish the closed-lab economics** (not directly in book); **AMD mixed** — benefits from "diversify off Nvidia" demand but threatened by the custom-ASIC (Broadcom) wave that bypasses *all* merchant GPUs; **ASML neutral-to-positive** (more chip designers racing to leading edge = more EUV, as long as total capex holds).

## How to use this tree live (the workflow)

1. **Hold the current regime in your head** (today: easing-oil / first-ECB-hike / crowded-AI / weak-China). Know which scenarios are "one datapoint away."
2. **When news breaks, don't compute — recognise:** *which branch did this just make more probable?* Then read off the pre-worked **most-relevant name + direction**.
3. **Layer the company file's §7 elasticity + the life-cycle stage (§3 of the Engine)** to turn "direction" into a **range**, and deliver: *"stock — range — reason — follow-up."*
4. **The biggest, fastest money is in the crowded-narrative breaks (S3) and the washed-out reliefs (S9a)** — where positioning + stage amplify the move most. Watch those branches hardest.
5. **Cross-currents are real:** S5 (China stimulus) can reflate oil and undercut S1 (dovish pivot); S7 (sell-America) and S8 (Europe wakes up) reinforce each other. Note when two scenarios point the same way on a name — that's your highest-conviction call.

> **Update cadence:** re-score scenario probabilities weekly as the macro evolves; re-tag each name's life-cycle stage after every print. The tree is a living map, not a one-off.

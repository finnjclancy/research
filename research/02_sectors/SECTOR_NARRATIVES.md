# Sector Narratives — each industry's place in the world

> Layer-2 of the narrative map. For each sector in the book: **its place in the world, why it's necessary, why anyone owns it, the growth story, the bull/bear narrative, and the single macro shift it's most levered to.** Narrative-first, not numbers. Built on [`../00_system/NARRATIVE_ENGINE.md`](../00_system/NARRATIVE_ENGINE.md); branches in [`../01_macro/MACRO_SCENARIO_TREE.md`](../01_macro/MACRO_SCENARIO_TREE.md).
>
> The book spans 8 narrative blocks. Each entry ends with **"levered to"** = the macro scenario whose change re-rates the sector most.

## A. Semiconductors & AI compute — *the picks, shovels & engines of the AI build-out*
**Names:** ASML (litho monopoly), AMD (AI accelerators), Melexis (auto sensors — *different sub-narrative*).

- **Place in the world:** the physical substrate of the digital economy. Compute is the new oil; these companies sit at the chokepoints — ASML makes the only machines that print leading-edge chips; AMD designs the engines; Melexis the nervous system of cars.
- **Why necessary:** there is no AI, no cloud, no electrified car without semiconductors. The deeper the digitisation of the world, the more non-negotiable the demand. ASML's necessity is near-absolute (monopoly).
- **Why own it:** exposure to the **defining structural growth theme of the decade (AI)**, with ASML offering the lowest-risk "toll-road" version and AMD the highest-torque "challenger" version.
- **Growth story:** AI training+inference demand → hyperscaler capex → more leading-edge wafers → more EUV/High-NA + more GPUs; "$1trn semis by 2030"; content-per-device rising everywhere.
- **Bull narrative:** AI is a multi-year, under-supplied super-cycle; demand outpaces supply; the chokepoint owners capture the rents.
- **Bear narrative:** **the most crowded narrative in markets** → vulnerable to an *AI-capex digestion / ROI reckoning*; brutal cyclicality and customer concentration; China/export-control ceiling; valuations price perfection.
- **★ Most levered to:** the **AI-capex narrative** (acceleration vs digestion) and **US–China export policy**. *Melexis is the exception — levered to the global auto cycle, NOT AI; don't conflate.*

## B. Banks — *the leveraged transmission of the rate cycle & the economy's credit plumbing*
**Names:** BNP Paribas, Crédit Agricole, Banca Mediolanum (*asset-gatherer sub-narrative*).

- **Place in the world:** the circulatory system — they intermediate savings into credit, and their profitability is a geared bet on **the price of money (rates) and the health of borrowers (credit).**
- **Why necessary:** an economy cannot function without credit creation and payment rails; banks are systemically protected (and regulated) for exactly that reason.
- **Why own it:** **cheap, high-capital-return, leveraged exposure to rising rates** and a re-rating from below book toward book as ROTE proves durable; fat dividends + buybacks.
- **Growth story:** higher-for-longer rates lift net interest margin; capital build → ever-larger buybacks; fee/insurance/AM diversification (BNP's AXA IM, ACA's Amundi, BMED's flows).
- **Bull narrative:** "rates up, credit holds" → record earnings + capital return → P/B re-rates toward 1.0×.
- **Bear narrative:** banks are a **bet against a recession** — if rates rise *into* a downturn, cost of risk explodes and the leverage works in reverse; rate *cuts* erode NII. French sovereign/political risk overhangs BNP/ACA.
- **★ Most levered to:** the **ECB rate path** *and* the **growth/credit cycle** — the ideal regime is "rates high, economy fine"; the nightmare is "stagflation → recession."

## C. Utilities — *the bond-proxy backbone & the unsung winner of electrification*
**Names:** Enel, Iberdrola.

- **Place in the world:** the regulated spine of the energy system — they own the grids and generation that everything else plugs into. Increasingly, **the bottleneck for AI** (data centres need power and grid connection).
- **Why necessary:** electricity is the most essential infrastructure; networks are natural monopolies with regulated, inflation-linked returns.
- **Why own it:** **defensive yield + a regulated-growth kicker** — a bond-like income stream with a capex-driven earnings growth engine (grids, renewables).
- **Growth story:** electrification + EVs + AI data-centre power demand → a multi-decade grid/renewables capex super-cycle at regulated returns (Iberdrola's ~€55bn grid plan; Enel's €53bn).
- **Bull narrative:** "the electrification + AI-power decade" + a **dovish rate pivot** that re-rates the bond-proxy; defensive in a slowdown.
- **Bear narrative:** **rate-sensitive bond proxies** — higher discount rates + higher debt cost de-rate them; regulatory return cuts; heavy debt-funded capex; LatAm/political risk (Enel).
- **★ Most levered to:** **bond yields / the rate path (inverse)** — the mirror image of banks. Secondary: the durability of the **AI-power-demand** story and **regulatory decisions**.

## D. Industrials (electrification & automation) — *the arms dealers of the physical capex boom*
**Names:** ABB (electrification/automation), Kone (elevators — *China-property sub-narrative*).

- **Place in the world:** the companies that physically build and electrify the world — data centres, factories, grids, buildings. The "real economy" expression of the AI/electrification themes.
- **Why necessary:** electrification, automation and re-industrialisation all require this equipment; data-centre power kit is a chokepoint (ABB).
- **Why own it:** **quality cyclical** exposure to electrification/data-centre/re-shoring capex, with strong margins, cash and order visibility (book-to-bill).
- **Growth story:** data-centre power, grid build-out, automation/re-shoring, energy efficiency → record orders (ABB); for Kone, the resilient services annuity offsetting cyclical new-build.
- **Bull narrative:** "electrify everything + AI needs power infrastructure" → multi-year order super-cycle.
- **Bear narrative:** **late-cycle cyclicals** — orders roll over fast in a downturn; ABB is priced for the theme to continue; Kone is hostage to **China property**.
- **★ Most levered to:** **the global capex/PMI cycle + the AI-power-demand story** (ABB); **China property** (Kone).

## E. Consumer Staples — *the defensive ballast & pricing-power compounders*
**Names:** Unilever, Carlsberg (*China/EM sub-narrative*).

- **Place in the world:** the everyday essentials — the low-volatility ballast of a portfolio; brands as moats against private label.
- **Why necessary:** people consume them through every cycle; demand is inelastic — the definition of defensive.
- **Why own it:** **defensive, dividend-paying compounding** with pricing power; a place to hide in risk-off / late-cycle; EM demographic growth.
- **Growth story:** premiumisation + EM volume growth + portfolio focus (Unilever shedding Ice Cream; Carlsberg adding Britvic/non-alc); margin expansion via productivity.
- **Bull narrative:** "volume growth returns at stable pricing" + a flight to defensives if growth scares hit.
- **Bear narrative:** **bond-proxy multiple compression** when rates rise; input-cost (oil/ag) squeeze; weak EM consumer (China for Carlsberg); private label; **GLP-1 demand risk** to food/alcohol volumes; structurally low growth.
- **★ Most levered to:** **the volume-vs-price quality of growth**, **input costs (oil)**, **EM/China consumer**, and the slow-burn **GLP-1** structural threat.

## F. Health Care / Pharma (GLP-1) — *the obesity supercycle & the drug-pricing battleground*
**Names:** Novo Nordisk.

- **Place in the world:** the science of human health — and, via GLP-1, the single biggest new pharma category in a generation (obesity as a treatable mass-market condition).
- **Why necessary:** obesity/diabetes is a vast, under-treated global epidemic; the TAM is enormous and durable.
- **Why own it:** exposure to the **obesity-drug supercycle** — but now at a *deep discount* after Novo lost the lead to Eli Lilly (a fallen-angel / turnaround narrative).
- **Growth story:** obesity penetration is still tiny vs the eligible population; oral formulations + new indications (cardio, sleep apnoea, MASH) + supply expansion broaden the TAM.
- **Bull narrative:** "the TAM is so big #2 still wins"; Novo's oral franchise + pipeline (amycretin) stabilises share; washed-out valuation.
- **Bear narrative:** Lilly is **more efficacious and out-innovating**; **US drug-pricing politics** (Trump MFN/TrumpRx) cap the most profitable market; pipeline execution scars; patent cliffs.
- **★ Most levered to:** **competitive dynamics vs Eli Lilly** (efficacy/share), **pipeline readouts**, and **US drug-pricing policy**.

## G. Consumer Discretionary / E-commerce — *the platform economy & the consumer's pulse*
**Names:** Zalando (EU fashion), Amazon (everything-store + AWS cloud).

- **Place in the world:** the digitisation of consumption — platforms intermediating how people buy. Amazon is also a **hyperscaler** (AWS = AI/cloud) and an **advertising** giant — a three-headed mega-cap.
- **Why necessary:** e-commerce penetration keeps rising; Amazon's AWS is critical AI/cloud infrastructure (overlaps sector A).
- **Why own it:** **scale-driven platform economics + margin expansion**; Amazon = a way to own the AI-capex theme *and* a recovering consumer + high-margin ads.
- **Growth story:** Amazon — AWS AI reacceleration + advertising + retail-margin expansion; Zalando — GMV growth + ecosystem (B2C + B2B logistics) + AI efficiency + ABOUT YOU synergies.
- **Bull narrative:** "AI monetises through the hyperscalers" (Amazon) / "European e-commerce reaccelerates profitably" (Zalando).
- **Bear narrative:** **AI-capex digestion / FCF compression** (Amazon's ~$200bn capex); discretionary/consumer cyclicality; thin margins + low-cost competition (Shein/Temu vs Zalando); cloud competition (Azure/Google).
- **★ Most levered to:** **AI-capex ROI (AWS growth vs capex)** for Amazon; **European consumer health + low-cost-import competition/regulation** for Zalando.

## H. Communication Services / Gaming — *content, IP and the attention economy (here: a breakup)*
**Names:** Embracer (→ Fellowship Entertainment).

- **Place in the world:** interactive entertainment — owning and monetising IP (games, franchises). Embracer specifically is a **corporate special situation**, not a clean sector play.
- **Why necessary:** entertainment/attention is a structurally growing share of consumer spend; durable IP (Lord of the Rings, Tomb Raider) is scarce and licensable.
- **Why own it:** **sum-of-the-parts value-unlock + deleveraging** as the conglomerate breaks into focused listed pieces; IP optionality (film/TV/licensing).
- **Growth story:** the breakup crystallises value; IP-led model (licensing + AAA development); the spun parts (Asmodee, Coffee Stain) re-rate independently.
- **Bull narrative:** "the parts are worth more than the whole, and debt is coming down."
- **Bear narrative:** hit-driven, lumpy game economics; serial-restructuring execution risk; spin-offs can de-rate (Coffee Stain did); hard to model.
- **★ Most levered to:** **corporate-action execution + IP monetisation** — idiosyncratic, largely macro-insensitive (a useful diversifier in the book).

## Cross-sector synthesis — the four meta-narratives that wire the book together
1. **The AI-capex super-cycle** — wires A (ASML, AMD) → G (Amazon/AWS) → D (ABB) → C (utilities, via power demand). *One narrative, five sectors. The single biggest shared risk/reward.*
2. **The rate cycle** — banks (B) ↑ vs utilities (C) & long-duration growth (A, G) ↓ as yields rise; the inverse on a dovish pivot. The cleanest **pairs axis** in the book.
3. **China** — Kone (D, property), Carlsberg (E, consumer), ASML/Melexis (A, chips). A China reflation or collapse re-prices all of them together.
4. **GLP-1 / health** — Novo (F) directly; a structural demand threat to Carlsberg/Unilever (E).

> When picking what you like/dislike, locate the name on these four wires first — most of the book's risk is shared, not idiosyncratic.

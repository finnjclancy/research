## 1. Where Carlsberg sits — *premiumisation + diversification* against structural alcohol decline

The beer sector's dominant narrative is **structural volume decline** — people (especially the young) **drink less**, favouring health, moderation, and no/low-alcohol; **GLP-1** is the looming accelerant ([Food Ingredients First](https://www.foodingredientsfirst.com/news/alcohol-decline-wine-spirits-beer-trends.html)). Carlsberg's counter-narrative is **"value over volume":**
- **★ Premiumisation is working:** premium beer grew **+4% organically** in 2025 vs a **−1.7% decline in overall beer volumes**; **core beer fell below half of revenue for the first time** in 2025 — the mix-shift is real.
- **★ Non-alcoholic diversification:** the **£3.3bn Britvic** acquisition (UK soft drinks; £100m synergies by 2029) + the **Carlsberg 0.0 / NOLO** push (NOLO +30% in key markets) explicitly diversify *away* from declining beer.
- **★ China (its largest market):** a "mature stage" of **stable volume + strong value growth via premiumisation**; Carlsberg **grew share despite a −1% volume decline** ([Daxue](https://daxueconsulting.com/beer-market-in-china-2/), [ChinaDaily](https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202502/11/WS67aab450a310a2ab06eab751.html)).
- **★ GLP-1 — so far muted:** management says "**initial data indicates we are not seeing any significant impact on alcohol purchase**" — a key, reassuring data point vs the sector fear.

## 3. ★ Bull narratives that re-rate it UP
1. **★ "Value beats volume"** — premiumisation + NOLO + Britvic keep organic operating-profit growing (guide +2–6%) *despite* falling beer volumes → the market rewards the mix-shift over the volume decline. *The core bull.*
2. **"China value-growth + share gains"** — Carlsberg keeps taking share in a premiumising China; consumer sentiment stabilises (stimulus) → the biggest single upside.
3. **"GLP-1 fear overdone"** — continued "no significant impact" data de-risks the structural bear → multiple re-rates from the depressed ~15× (cheaper than Unilever).
4. **"Britvic synergies beat"** — non-alc diversification delivers ahead of plan (already running ahead).

## 4. ★ Bear narratives that re-rate it DOWN
1. **★ "China consumer stays weak"** — Carlsberg's largest market falters; volumes keep falling faster than premium mix can offset. *The dominant downside.*
2. **"Structural decline + GLP-1 bites"** — alcohol moderation accelerates and GLP-1 data turns negative → the secular bear becomes a present-tense earnings problem.
3. **"Input-cost squeeze"** — barley/aluminium/energy spike compresses margins.
4. **"Premiumisation plateaus"** — if trade-up stalls in a weak consumer, the value-over-volume engine sputters.

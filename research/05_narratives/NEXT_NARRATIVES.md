## PART 1 — The emerging cross-cutting drivers (deeper than the [Market Drivers masterclass](../07_big_moves/MARKET_DRIVERS.md))

### D1 — ★ The "Inference Flip": training → inference (the bull offset to capex digestion)
**The story:** the AI compute mix has flipped from training to **inference**. Inference now = **~85% of enterprise AI budget and ~2/3 of all global AI compute**, with the "Inference Flip" dated to **early 2026**. Token consumption is forecast to **multiply ~24× to ~120 quadrillion/month by 2030** (agents use 5–30× more tokens per task), while per-token cost falls **60–70%/yr** → a potential **"margin inflection"** that funds the capex ([Goldman Sachs](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/ai-agents-forecast-to-boost-tech-cash-flow-as-usage-soars), [Zylos](https://zylos.ai/research/2026-04-13-inference-economics-ai-agent-compute-markets)).
- **Why it matters:** this is the **single most important offset to the AI-capex-digestion bear (S3)**. If inference demand explodes (Jevons), the capex is justified and ASML/AMD/Amazon keep compounding.
- **The catch (Gartner):** **40% of AI-agent projects will be cancelled by 2027** on cost overruns — so the bull rests on agents actually delivering ROI.
- **→ Book:** bullish ASML (more chips), **especially AMD** (inference is its tokens-per-dollar strength), Amazon/AWS (inference monetisation). The training-vs-inference framing is the new lens on the whole AI complex.

### D2 — ★ AI depreciation & earnings quality (Burry's accounting attack)
**The story:** Michael Burry alleges hyperscalers are **stretching GPU "useful life" to 5–6 years** when the real economic life is **2–3** (Nvidia ships a 2–3× more efficient generation every ~3 years). His estimate: depreciation **understated by ~$176bn 2026–28, overstating profits 20%+** (Oracle ~27%, Meta ~21%). **Meta extended useful lives 3× in 3 years; Amazon *shortened* its schedule (took a $700m hit).** ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/11/big-short-investor-michael-burry-accuses-ai-hyperscalers-of-artificially-boosting-earnings.html), [Level-Headed](https://www.levelheadedinvesting.com/p/are-ai-chips-useful-lives-creating-useless-earnings))
- **Why it matters:** if "AI earnings are fake" becomes the consensus narrative, it's a credibility hit to the whole complex — and it *compounds* the FCF-zero-by-summer-2026 capex story.
- **→ Book:** a *relative* positive for **Amazon** (it shortened useful lives = conservative, harder to accuse) vs Meta/Oracle; a sentiment risk for the AI supply chain. Watch for SEC/analyst scrutiny of depreciation disclosures as a catalyst.

### D3 — ★ Behind-the-meter power (the threat to the "utilities-as-AI-winners" thesis)
**The story:** with grid interconnection queues stretching to **2035** while compute doubles every 18 months, hyperscalers are going **behind-the-meter** — on-site nuclear/gas/SMRs that bypass the grid (Microsoft–Constellation **Three Mile Island restart**, AWS–Talen **1.92GW Pennsylvania nuclear PPA**, SMRs with ~99.9% uptime) ([PowerMag](https://www.powermag.com/the-smr-gamble-betting-on-nuclear-to-fuel-the-data-center-boom/), [QuantStrategy](https://quantstrategy.io/blog/the-role-of-nuclear-energy-in-meeting-ai-data-center-power/)).
- **Why it matters:** the bull case for **Iberdrola/Enel** is "AI needs our power." But if hyperscalers **self-generate behind-the-meter, they bypass the regulated utility** — load growth that doesn't accrue to the grid owner. A nuance the market under-appreciates.
- **→ Book:** **two-sided for utilities** (PPA upside vs bypass risk — watch the *mix*); **unambiguously bullish ABB** (on-site power still needs switchgear/transformers/electrical kit regardless of grid-vs-behind-the-meter); supports the **nuclear/SMR renaissance** (D-cluster of FUTURE_NARRATIVES).

### D4 — ★ High-NA EUV adoption divergence (ASML's growth-leg timing)
**The story:** High-NA EUV (EXE:5200, ~$400m/tool) adoption is **splitting** — **Intel (14A, 2027), Samsung & SK hynix** are adopting; **TSMC has *deferred* High-NA for its 1.4nm node on cost**, sticking with low-NA. ASML expects the first High-NA logic/memory products "within months." ([Trendforce](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/05/01/news-behind-tsmcs-high-na-euv-deferral-low-na-stays-strong-customer-landscape-shifts-and-asml-quietly-pivots/), [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/greatspeculations/2026/01/09/what-2026-has-in-store-for-asml-high-na-euv-dram/))
- **Why it matters:** High-NA is ASML's next decade-long growth leg. **TSMC's deferral is a yellow flag** (the biggest customer waiting on cost); but Intel/Samsung/SK hynix + **DRAM/HBM** adoption (2026 = "the year of DRAM" for EUV) keep the leg alive. The *pace* of High-NA is a slow-burn ASML narrative.
- **→ Book:** ASML-specific — TSMC re-committing (or not) to High-NA at A14P is a key signpost; DRAM-maker EUV orders are the offset.

### D5 — The rate-pivot timing (when the oil-driven hawkishness breaks)
**The story:** the 2026 oil shock drove the ECB hike + Warsh-Fed hawkishness. With oil now easing, **when does the inflation impulse fade and the hawkishness break dovish?** The consensus is still "higher-for-longer." → the bank/utility rotation hinges on this.
- **→ Book:** the cleanest pairs tell — **banks (BNP/ACA/BMED) ↑ while hawkish, utilities (Enel/Iberdrola) ↑ on the pivot.** Watch the gap between Warsh's words (hawkish) and the data (oil/core inflation). Detailed in [`../01_macro/ECB_RATE_REGIME.md`](../01_macro/ECB_RATE_REGIME.md).

### D6 — ★ European bank consolidation (the M&A wave, politically blocked)
**The story:** European bank M&A surged **$43bn (2024) → $73bn (2025)** — the strongest in over a decade. But the marquee cross-border deals are **blocked by politics**: **BBVA–Sabadell failed** (shareholders rejected, political opposition), **UniCredit–Commerzbank stalled** (German govt opposition; Orcel may wait to 2027), **UniCredit–Banco BPM pulled** (Italian golden-power conditions). New EU merger rules (Jan 2026) aim to lower barriers, but the banking union is incomplete (no EDIS) ([GlobalCapital](https://www.globalcapital.com/article/2fqcduueoptnxxhfmwnb5/rejuvenated-european-banking-sector-driven-to-still-more-m-a-in-2026), [ION](https://ionanalytics.com/insights/mergermarket/european-bank-ma-pipeline-stacked-as-rates-scale-tech-drive-deal-flow/)).
- **→ Book:** **BNP/ACA as potential *consolidators*** (scale + capital) and the sector's scarcity re-rating; **BMED as a potential target / scarcity asset** in the Italian wealth-M&A wave (Mediobanca/Banca Generali). Domestic-vs-cross-border is the open question.

### D7 — GLP-1 category expansion (from obesity to a platform)
**The story:** GLP-1s are moving beyond obesity/diabetes into **MASH, Alzheimer's, cardiovascular, addiction, prediabetes, osteoarthritis, renal** — a "platform" TAM. In Alzheimer's, GLP-1s show amyloid/tau-clearance + neuroinflammation benefits; **Novo's semaglutide Alzheimer's (evoke/evoke+) readout is a major binary** ([Clarivate](https://clarivate.com/life-sciences-healthcare/blog/drugs-to-watch-2026-qa-lillys-next-generation-weight-loss-drugs/), [Alz.org](https://www.alz.org/blog/2025/glp-1s-and-alzheimer-s-what-you-need-to-know)).
- **→ Book:** the *upside* re-rate path for **Novo** (a positive Alzheimer's/MASH readout reframes it from "losing obesity to Lilly" to "GLP-1 platform"); the *demand-destruction* read-through deepens for **Unilever/Carlsberg** (more indications = more users = more consumption impact).

### D8 — Agentic commerce (AI agents transacting)
**The story:** AI agents that **shop, book, and transact autonomously** reorder the front-end of commerce (who is the "customer"), advertising (agents don't watch ads), and search.
- **→ Book:** **Amazon** (could own the agent layer *or* be disintermediated); **Zalando** (curation/marketplace amplified or bypassed). Early, but a genuine "next narrative" for the discretionary names.

### D9 — China stimulus *transmission* (does it reach the consumer/property?)
**The story:** China's "two new" + ultra-long treasury bonds are funding equipment renewal/modernisation; the question is whether stimulus *transmits* to the property and consumer.
- **→ Book:** **Kone** (stimulus → elevator *modernisation* — a tailwind, not just a property risk), **Carlsberg/Unilever** (consumer), **ASML/Melexis** (chip demand). The shared upside catalyst for the China cluster.

### D10 — The "AI complex" splits into haves/have-nots within the supply chain
**The story:** as the cycle matures, the market increasingly differentiates *within* the AI supply chain — chokepoints with pricing power (ASML litho, HBM) vs commoditising layers (merchant compute under ASIC threat).
- **→ Book:** ASML (chokepoint, agnostic to who wins) is structurally better-positioned than AMD (merchant GPU, ASIC-threatened) in a maturing cycle — even though both are "AI plays."

## PART 3 — Synthesis: the three "next narratives" most likely to move the book

1. **★ "The Inference Flip saves (or doesn't save) the AI capex."** This is the single highest-stakes next narrative — does inference/agentic demand (tokens 24×, margin inflection) justify the $200bn+ capex, or do agent-project cancellations (Gartner 40%) + the depreciation attack (Burry's $176bn) win? **It decides ASML, AMD, Amazon, ABB, and the utilities together.** Watch: AWS growth + the capex guide + agent-ROI evidence.
2. **★ "The rate-pivot rotates the book."** When the oil-driven hawkishness breaks, **utilities (Enel/Iberdrola) and growth multiples re-rate up while the bank-NII trade caps.** The cleanest, most-datable rotation. Watch: oil → core inflation → ECB/Fed language.
3. **★ "GLP-1 becomes a platform, not just obesity."** A positive **Novo Alzheimer's/MASH** readout reframes Novo from "losing to Lilly" to "GLP-1 platform" (the washed-out re-rate) — *and* deepens the demand-destruction overhang on **Unilever/Carlsberg**. Watch: the evoke/Alzheimer's readout + category-expansion data.

> **The meta-point:** every "next narrative" above is a bet on *what the market starts caring about next.* Hold the now-priced narrative and the next one side by side for each name — when a signpost fires, you already know the direction and roughly the magnitude. That's the whole job.

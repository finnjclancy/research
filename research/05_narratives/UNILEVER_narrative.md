## 1. The sector's narrative weather (consumer staples)

**The dominant story:** *"is volume-led growth back, and can it offset the structural threats?"* Post-inflation, staples are judged on **the quality of growth — volume vs price** (the market rewards *volume*-led USG, punishes price-only). Three structural narratives hang over the sector:
1. **★ GLP-1 demand destruction** — weight-loss drugs are cutting consumption of indulgent food/snacks (a **2–4% secular volume decline in indulgent snacks in Western markets**), forcing reformulation ([Unilever PR](https://www.unilever.com/news/press-and-media/press-releases/2026/strong-volume-growth-full-year-outlook-reconfirmed/)). *The big structural staples bear.*
2. **EM growth vs saturated developed markets** — the growth is in EM; DM is mature.
3. **Premiumisation vs value-seeking/private-label.**

---

## 2. Where Unilever sits — the *reshaping compounder* shedding its weak spots

Unilever's narrative is **active portfolio surgery to escape the sector's structural drags:**
- **★ Ice Cream demerged (Dec 2025)** → **Foods being combined with McCormick (agreement 31 Mar 2026)** to create a "global flavour powerhouse" ([Unilever Q1-26](https://www.unilever.com/files/unilever-q1-2026-full-announcement.pdf)). **Effect: Unilever is *shedding the GLP-1-exposed, lower-growth food businesses* to focus on Beauty & Wellbeing, Personal Care, and Home Care** — i.e. it is *de-risking the very narrative (GLP-1) that threatens the sector.* This is the defining Unilever story.
- **Volume growth is back and accelerating:** Q1-26 USG **3.8%, volume +2.9%**; **Power Brands +5%** (4% volume) ([Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/unilever-q1-2026-slides-volume-growth-accelerates-to-29-93CH-4648036)).
- **EM is the engine:** EM +5.7% (volume +4.2%, India-led); **Asia-Pacific-Africa = 45% of turnover**, +5.9% USG.

## 4. ★ Bull narratives that re-rate it UP
1. **★ "Reshaped RemainCo = higher-growth, higher-margin, GLP-1-light"** — once Ice Cream + Foods are out, Unilever is a focused Beauty/Personal/Home Care compounder with *less* GLP-1 exposure and *more* premiumisation. *Perception shift:* from "sprawling food-and-soap conglomerate" to "focused personal-care growth compounder" → re-rate. *The core bull.*
2. **"Volume-led growth sustains"** — USG volume keeps accelerating (2.9%+) at stable pricing, Power Brands +5% → quality-of-growth premium justified.
3. **"EM/India compounding"** — the 45%-of-turnover APAC engine keeps delivering 5%+ volume growth.
4. **"Capital return"** — 60% payout + €6bn buyback + Foods/Ice Cream proceeds.

## 5. ★ Bear narratives that re-rate it DOWN
1. **★ "EM can't offset saturated DM"** — Barclays' Ackerman: EM (55% of food) growth "still not enough to make up for Europe and the U.S. where the market is saturated." If DM stays flat and EM wobbles, the growth story stalls at a premium **~23× multiple**.
2. **"GLP-1 spreads beyond food"** — if weight-loss drugs start denting personal-care/wellness consumption too, even RemainCo isn't safe.
3. **"Input-cost squeeze"** — an oil/commodity spike compresses gross margin; pricing power tested.
4. **"Reshaping disappoints"** — McCormick/Foods deal value or execution underwhelms; dis-synergies.

## 6. The narrative-shift tells
- **★ USG volume/price split** — sustained, accelerating *volume* is the whole quality case.
- **The Foods→McCormick deal completion + RemainCo growth/margin profile** (the re-rating catalyst).
- **Power Brand growth + EM/India volume.**
- **Input costs (oil/commodities)** + **bond yields** (multiple).

## 7. Scenario read-through
- **Reshaping delivers + volume-led growth:** a quality-compounder re-rate. ↑
- **Higher-for-longer (oil sticky):** input-cost squeeze + mild multiple headwind. ↓ (modest — low beta)
- **Risk-off / late-cycle:** defensive bid — relative outperformer. ↑ (relative)
- **GLP-1 acceleration:** structural overhang, but Unilever *less* exposed post-Foods than peers.

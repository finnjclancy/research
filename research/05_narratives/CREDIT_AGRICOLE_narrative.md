## 1. Where CASA sits — the *cheap, high-yield, fee-tilted* French bank

Same sector weather as BNP (re-rating + M&A wave + the oil-shock NII whipsaw + the new private-credit/AI-disruption bear flags), but Crédit Agricole's narrative has a distinct flavour:
- **Cheapest + highest-yielding** of the book's banks: **~7× earnings, ~6.9% dividend yield**, **RoTE 13.5%** (higher than BNP's 11.6%) — a classic **value + income** story.
- **Amundi** (Europe's largest asset manager, ~60%-owned) is its **fee/private-assets engine** — €88bn 2025 inflows — the same "diversify into fees" theme BNP plays via AXA IM.
- **★ The CASA-vs-Group nuance is itself a narrative:** the *listed stock* (CASA) is **more market-sensitive** than BNP because of its heavy insurance + Amundi + CIB weighting — Q1-26 CASA earnings *fell* on the Iran-war market turbulence even as the *Group* rose on regional-bank NII. So CASA carries **more equity-market beta** and **less pure rate-leverage** than BNP.

## 3. ★ Bull narratives that re-rate it UP
1. **★ "Deep value closes the gap"** — at ~7× and 6.9% yield with a 13.5% RoTE, a sustained NII tailwind + Amundi flows + ACT-2028 delivery forces the market to re-rate the discount toward the €20+ PT. *The core upside.*
2. **"Markets recover → fees fire"** — a risk-on rebound lifts Amundi/insurance income (the lever that *fell* in Q1-26) → CASA's market-beta works *for* it.
3. **"Income compounder"** — the 50% cash payout + interim dividends + fortress Group CET1 (~17%) underpin a high, safe, growing yield.

## 4. ★ Bear narratives that re-rate it DOWN
1. **★ "Market drawdown hits CASA harder"** — a risk-off equity move dents fees + insurance + CIB *simultaneously* (the listed-stock's amplified downside vs the Group). *The distinctive CASA risk.*
2. **"Recession lifts cost of risk"** — the universal bank downside.
3. **"It stays a value trap"** — cheap + high-yield but **Hold consensus**; without a catalyst the discount persists.
4. **"Rate cuts return / French sovereign stress."**

---

## 5. The narrative-shift tells
- **★ The Group-vs-CASA earnings split** — if the listed stock's fee/insurance lines keep lagging the Group's retail NII, the value gap won't close.
- **Amundi net flows** (the fee-engine tell) + **equity-market level**.
- **Cost of risk + ECB path + French politics/OAT.**

## 6. Scenario read-through
- **Higher-for-longer + markets firm:** ideal — NII *and* fees both work; deep-value re-rate. ↑
- **Risk-off / market drawdown:** CASA underperforms BNP (more fee/market beta). ↓
- **S1 (dovish pivot):** supports markets/Amundi (helps CASA's fee tilt) even as NII fades — net neutral-to-positive, arguably *better* for CASA than BNP.
- **Recession:** cost of risk + market hit together. ↓↓

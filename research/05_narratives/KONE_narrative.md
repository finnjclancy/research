# Kone — Narrative Map

> Narrative-first companion to [`../03_companies/KONE.md`](../03_companies/KONE.md). Research date 2026-06-28. **The cleanest China-property proxy in the book — its order book is a window onto Chinese construction.**

---

## 1. The sector's narrative weather (elevators & escalators)

**The dominant story:** *"the China new-build collapse vs the services annuity."* E&E is a two-narrative stock: a **cyclical, China-property-exposed new-equipment** business in decline, offset by a **recurring, high-margin maintenance & modernisation** annuity that grows with the installed base. The whole debate is **"is the China new-build drag bottoming, and does the resilient services/modernisation story dominate?"**

---

## 2. Where Kone sits — China-property proxy + a *new* modernisation/stimulus angle

- **China is ~20% of Kone's revenue (its largest single market)** and the swing factor; new-building demand is "expected to decline clearly in 2026."
- **★ The fresh, under-appreciated bull angle — modernisation + Chinese stimulus:** China's aging elevator base needs replacing, and **Chinese policy is *funding* it** — the "**two new**" initiatives (equipment renewal) + **ultra-long special treasury bonds** are channelling money into modernisation, "unlocking new growth drivers" for Kone ([Elevator World](https://elevatorworld.com/news/daily-news/kone-strengthens-presence-in-china-with-modernizations/)). Kone won the **Kunshan project (2,106 elevators, 96% of the tender)** — evidence the modernisation pivot is real. **So Chinese stimulus, usually framed as a Kone *risk* (property), is becoming a Kone *tailwind* (modernisation).**
- The maintenance base (>99% uptime service) is the steady, sticky annuity underneath.

---

## 3. Cross-trend web

| Trend | Effect on Kone narrative |
|---|---|
| **China property downturn** | The dominant bear — new-equipment demand falling. |
| **★ China stimulus ("two new" + treasury bonds)** | A *new* bull — funds equipment modernisation/renewal (offsets the new-build drag). |
| **Services/maintenance annuity** | The resilient floor — grows with the installed base regardless of new-build. |
| **Global construction cycle** (ex-China: India, APAC, ME) | The growth offset to China. |
| **Rates** | Mild (construction financing) — far less than for utilities/banks. |

---

## 4. ★ Bull narratives that re-rate it UP
1. **★ "China bottoming + modernisation offsets new-build"** — the *second derivative* of China orders turns less-negative, and **stimulus-funded modernisation** fills the gap. *Perception shift:* from "China-property casualty" to "China modernisation + services compounder" → re-rate toward the ~€62 PT (vs ~€51 price). *The key upside — and it can happen on improving 2nd-derivative even with absolute weakness.*
2. **"Services dominate"** — the maintenance annuity + margin recovery (12.3–13.0% guide) proves the quality/defensiveness → premium holds.
3. **"Ex-China growth accelerates"** — India/APAC/ME clearly grow, offsetting China.

## 5. ★ Bear narratives that re-rate it DOWN
1. **★ "China property keeps deteriorating"** — new-equipment demand and pricing fall further; stimulus underwhelms → the modernisation offset isn't enough. *The dominant downside.*
2. **"Margin miss"** — China new-equipment price competition prevents the guided margin recovery.
3. **"Mature, low-growth"** — even with stabilisation, E&E is a low-growth market that struggles to justify a premium.

---

## 6. The narrative-shift tells
- **★ The China order trend's *second derivative*** + the **new-equipment vs modernisation split** — the single most important read.
- **China property & stimulus data** ("two new"/treasury-bond flows into modernisation).
- **Adjusted EBIT margin vs the 12.3–13.0% guide.**
- **Services/maintenance base growth** (the resilience floor).

## 7. Scenario read-through
- **China stimulus / property stabilisation:** the upside catalyst — Kone is a leveraged China-recovery option (pairs with the China macro brief). ↑↑
- **China property deepens:** the core downside. ↓
- **Global recession:** construction/new-build hit, but services annuity cushions (more defensive than ABB). ↓ (less than cyclicals)

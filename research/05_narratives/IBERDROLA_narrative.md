# Iberdrola — Narrative Map

> Narrative-first companion to [`../03_companies/IBERDROLA.md`](../03_companies/IBERDROLA.md). Research date 2026-06-28. **Holds the utilities "sector weather" used by [`ENEL_narrative.md`](ENEL_narrative.md) too.**

---

## 1. The sector's narrative weather (regulated utilities) — a story in transition

**The old story:** *"bond proxy"* — utilities = stable dividends discounted at the bond yield; you bought them for yield and bought them *more* when rate cuts were coming. Under that lens the 2026 oil-shock rate *hikes* are a headwind (higher discount rate + debt cost).

**★ The NEW, more powerful story (the re-rating narrative):** *"utilities are the AI-electricity bottleneck."* AI data-centre power demand is exploding — global data-centre electricity could **roughly double (485 TWh 2025 → ~950 TWh 2030)**, with AI-centre use **tripling to ~465 TWh** ([IEA via Enlit](https://www.enlit.world/library/ai-and-data-centre-electricity-use-continue-to-surge-iea-finds)). **"Speed to power" is now the binding constraint on AI build-out** — which makes **grid owners and clean-power generators strategic.** This converts utilities from sleepy bond proxies into **AI-infrastructure plays.** The tension between these two narratives (rate-sensitive bond proxy vs AI-power growth) *is* the utilities trade in 2026.

---

## 2. Where Iberdrola sits — the *premium AI-power + grids* utility

Iberdrola has positioned itself as **the** name for the AI-power narrative:
- **Microsoft PPA** for 150 MW dedicated wind in Spain; **taking *equity* in AI data centres** via a JV with **Echelon Data Centres (20% stake)** — i.e. moving *up* the value chain from power supplier to data-centre *owner* ([Net Zero Compare](https://netzerocompare.com/articles/iberdrola-moves-deepder-into-ai-data-centres-as-clean-power-demand-accelerates), [EnkiAI](https://enkiai.com/iberdrola-ai-initiatives-for-2025-key-projects-strategies-and-partnerships/)).
- **Norges Bank JV: 1,500 MW Iberian renewables, >$2bn.**
- **>€100bn investment to 2031 (€58bn to 2028), ~two-thirds to networks** — the regulated rate-base growth engine.

So Iberdrola straddles **both** narratives: a **rate-sensitive regulated-networks** story *and* an **AI-power-demand growth** story. The bull case is that the AI-power leg lets it out-grow the rate headwind.

## 4. ★ Bull narratives that re-rate it UP
1. **★ "The AI-power utility"** — data-centre PPAs + equity stakes + grid-connection scarcity reframe Iberdrola as an **AI-infrastructure growth** name, not a bond proxy. *Perception shift:* the multiple decouples *upward* from bond yields → re-rates on a growth (not yield) basis.
2. **"Rate-base compounding"** — networks RAB (€51bn, +12%) keeps growing at ~9.5% allowed returns; favourable Ofgem/PUC rulings.
3. **"Rates roll over"** — if oil eases and yields fall (S1), the bond-proxy tailwind returns *on top* of the AI-power story → double catalyst.
4. **"US/UK regulated tilt de-risks"** — the shift to AAA-jurisdiction networks (Avangrid 100%) earns a quality premium.

## 5. ★ Bear narratives that re-rate it DOWN
1. **★ "Higher-for-longer crushes the multiple"** — sticky oil/inflation keeps yields up; a heavily-levered, long-duration capex plan de-rates regardless of the AI story. *The dominant downside.*
2. **"Regulatory cut"** — an adverse Ofgem/PUC/CNMC allowed-return decision hits the rate-base earnings directly.
3. **"AI-power demand is over-hyped / data-centre digestion"** — if the AI-capex cycle cracks (S3), the data-centre power-demand growth leg deflates too.
4. **"Capex/balance-sheet strain"** — funding €100bn+ stresses the balance sheet in a high-rate world (equity raises like Avangrid's $4bn dilute).

## 6. The narrative-shift tells
- **★ Bond yields / the rate path** — still the single biggest multiple driver (watch oil → inflation → ECB/Fed).
- **Data-centre PPA / equity-stake announcements** — each one builds the AI-power re-rating.
- **Regulated-return decisions** (Ofgem RIIO, US rate cases, CNMC).
- **RAB growth + capex-funding mix** (debt vs equity vs scrip).
- **Hyperscaler capex signals** — if data-centre build-out slows (S3), the power-demand story softens.

## 7. Scenario read-through
- **S4 (AI Vertical) + S1 (dovish):** the dream combo — AI-power growth *and* a falling discount rate → strong re-rate. ↑↑
- **Higher-for-longer (oil sticky):** the multiple stays capped despite the AI story. ↓
- **S3 (AI Reckoning):** the data-centre power-demand leg deflates *and* it's still rate-sensitive — a double hit. ↓
- **Regulatory shock:** idiosyncratic downside independent of macro.
